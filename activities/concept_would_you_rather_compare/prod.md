### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child chooses between two playful options and explains the preference or tradeoff.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know that a preference names one choice, a reason explains it, and a tradeoff notices value in the other choice.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that different perspectives can be fair even when one option is their favorite.
- **D (Do):** Children compare two playful options, choose one, explain why, and name one good thing about the other option.

**3. Design Highlight**

Choices stay safe, silly, and non-shaming. The repeated action is compare, choose, and explain.

**4. Typical Scenario**

The child chooses whether they would rather hop like a frog or glide like a kite, then gives one reason.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm invitation] "I found a perfect moment for Two-Choice Compare Chat. You can be the Choice Comparator today. Ready for a tiny mission?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes" or nods.
2. (Unexpected) "What mission?" or gives an unrelated idea.
3. (No response) Child watches or stays quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. I will keep it short and playful."
2. [friendly] "Good question. I will show one tiny choice at a time."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "You can answer with one word, a point, or a small idea."

**Screen:** The activity badge "Choice Comparator" appears with three empty progress tokens.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction

**AI says:** [clear guide tone] "Here is the rule: I give you one small challenge, you answer, and we save one progress token. Three tokens make the final reveal."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay."
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do it my way?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Round one is ready."
2. [validating] "Yes, your idea can help. I will still keep each round easy to answer."
3. [wait 2s] [modeling] "I will start, and you can copy my first answer if you want."

**Screen:** A simple rule strip appears: Listen or look, answer, save a token. Token 1 pulses.

#### Step 3: Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Pick One:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "Would you rather have rainbow shoes or moon boots?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "moon boots."
2. (Unexpected) "both."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [choice marker] "Moon boots win your first vote. I am pinning them on the chosen side."
2. [validating] "Both is a fun wish. This round needs one winner, then we can be fair to the other side."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Point to rainbow shoes or moon boots."

**Screen:** Two choice bubbles appear. Token 1 fills with a short label from the child's response.

**Round 2 -- Say Why:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "What makes that choice better for you?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I can jump high."
2. (Unexpected) "because yes."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [reasoning tone] "Jumping high is your reason. The moon-boot side gets a reason ribbon."
2. [soft scaffold] "Because yes can start the answer. Add one tiny reason, like jump high or feel bouncy."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: I pick them because I can jump."

**Screen:** A reason ribbon attaches to the chosen bubble. Token 2 fills with a short label from the child's response.

**Round 3 -- Compare the Tradeoff:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "What is one good thing about the option you did not choose?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "rainbow shoes are pretty."
2. (Unexpected) "nothing."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [fair-minded] "Pretty is a fair point for rainbow shoes. Now both sides have evidence."
2. [gentle challenge] "Nothing is a strong vote. Look again and give the other side one small good point."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: rainbow shoes have bright colors."

**Screen:** Both bubbles stay visible with one reason each. Token 3 fills with a short label from the child's response.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [delighted reveal] "Look at that. Your three tokens made something new: The final screen shows the chosen side and the fair reason for the other side, making preference feel thoughtful."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I made it" or explains the result.
2. (Unexpected) "Can we change one?"
3. (No response) Child watches the reveal.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [celebrating] "Yes. Your answers caused this reveal."
2. [replay invitation] "That is a great next-round idea. This version shows the path you made first."
3. [wait 2s] [soft recap] "I will read the three tokens back so you can see your path."

**Screen:** The three tokens expand into a final board labeled "Choice Comparator". Each token stays visible so the payoff is earned by the child's earlier actions.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm celebration] "You earned the Choice Comparator badge. You used Perspective by noticing the important part, and Connection by connecting your answers to what happened next."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again" or names a favorite round.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for a different topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud guide] "Next time we can make a new path with a new answer."
2. [flexible] "That can be the next mission. This badge saves what you did today."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle goodbye] "Your Choice Comparator badge is saved."

**Screen:** The badge, the three round tokens, and a next-step card remain visible. Next-step card: "Try one new answer next time."
