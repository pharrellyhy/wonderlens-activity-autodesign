## Tiny Curator Gallery

### A. Basic Info

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Activity Name | Tiny Curator Gallery |
| Activity Category | 5 -- Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo) |
| Recommended Tier | T2 (ages 6-8) |
| Core IB Key Concepts | Connection and Form |
| Related Concepts | Classification, Evidence, Rule, Relationship |
| ATL Skills Focus | Thinking Skills (classification and logic), Communication Skills (explaining evidence), Self-Management Skills (organizing materials) |
| Experience Pillar | Adventure |
| Game Style | quest_collector |

### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child chooses three or four nearby objects, arranges them as a tiny exhibit, and explains the child-made grouping rule.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Objects can be grouped by a rule; one object can belong for more than one reason; a curator label explains a grouping.
- **U (Understand):** Connection helps us explain why separate objects belong in one set. Form helps us notice features that support the rule.
- **D (Do):** Choose objects, arrange them, name a rule, and explain evidence for the grouping.

**3. Design Highlight**

The AI never gives the category first. The child acts as curator, builds the exhibit, and earns the final label by defending their own rule.

**4. Typical Scenario**

Child gathers a spoon, cup, plate, and napkin, arranges them together, then explains, "They are all for snack time."

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T2 (ages 6-8)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [curious gallery whisper] "This place has tiny museum pieces everywhere. You can be the curator. What objects might belong together?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "These three toys." / "I can use leaves."
2. (Unexpected) "I only like this one." / "I do not know."
3. (No response) Child looks around or holds one object.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [interested curator tone] "Good start. Do not tell me the rule yet. Choose your exhibit pieces first."
2. [warmly narrowing] "Start with one favorite. Then ask, what could sit beside it for a reason?"
3. [wait 2s] [gentle prompt] "Look for three things that share color, job, material, place, or story."

**Screen:** A blank gallery shelf appears with four empty object spots. A ribbon reads "Curator's rule: hidden for now." A small camera button says "Add exhibit piece."

#### Step 2: Gallery Rules -- Curator Choice

**AI says:** [clear mission tone] "Your job is to make one tiny exhibit. Pick three or four objects. Arrange them so a visitor can guess your rule."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I will make a snack exhibit." / "I picked round things."
2. (Unexpected) "You choose the rule." / "Can I change my mind?"
3. (No response) Child studies the empty shelf.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Great. Keep the rule in your head while you place the pieces."
2. [respectful handoff] "You choose. Curators can change a rule when the exhibit teaches them something."
3. [wait 2s] [soft scaffold] "Try one easy rule: same use, same place, same material, or same shape."

**Screen:** The shelf gets two zones: "Main exhibit" and "Maybe shelf." A rule ribbon stays blank. Three small badges wait: "Choose," "Arrange," "Explain."

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Curation

**Round 1 -- First Exhibit Piece:**

**AI says:** [attentive guide tone] "Place your first object in the exhibit. What made you choose this one?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "It is a spoon. It is for eating." / "This leaf is red."
2. (Unexpected) "Because I like it." / "It is mine."
3. (No response) Child places or photographs one object.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [thoughtful] "That gives us a clue. The rule might be about use, color, shape, material, or place."
2. [validating then extending] "Favorites can start exhibits. What is one thing a visitor can notice about it?"
3. [wait 2s] [gentle observation cue] "I will mark it as Piece 1. Look at its color, shape, material, or job."

**Screen:** The first photo or object card fills slot 1. A floating note says "Possible clue." The rule ribbon remains hidden.

**Round 2 -- Add and Compare:**

**AI says:** [curious visitor tone] "Add a second object beside it. Does this one follow the same idea, or should it go on the maybe shelf?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Same idea." / "It also helps with snack."
2. (Unexpected) "It is different but I want it." / "It should go far away."
3. (No response) Child moves the second object around.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [curator applause] "You are sorting now. Tell me the link between Piece 1 and Piece 2."
2. [respectful sorting coach] "Different can still belong. Name the reason. If the reason is not ready, use the maybe shelf."
3. [wait 2s] [choice prompt] "Put it close if it belongs. Put it on maybe if you are still deciding."

**Screen:** Slot 2 appears next to slot 1. A thin line asks "same rule?" The maybe shelf glows yellow but does not reject the object.

**Round 3 -- Complete the Exhibit Rule:**

**AI says:** [building suspense] "Now add your final piece or pieces. When the exhibit feels right, give the whole group a curator label."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "All snack things." / "Red nature things." / "Things that roll."
2. (Unexpected) "This one breaks the rule." / "I found a better rule."
3. (No response) Child arranges pieces silently.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [delighted] "That is a curator label. Your rule is: `{child_made_grouping_rule}`. What evidence proves it?"
2. [flexible and precise] "Good catch. Curators revise. Change the rule, move that piece to maybe, or explain the exception."
3. [wait 2s] [soft sentence starter] "Try this: 'These belong together because...'"

**Screen:** Slots 3 and 4 open. The child can leave slot 4 empty. When the child names the rule, the ribbon fills with `{child_made_grouping_rule}` and the shelf lights trace around the grouped objects.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment -- Visitor Test

**AI says:** [dramatic visitor voice] "A tiny visitor arrives. They ask, 'Why do these belong together?' Curator, give your one-sentence tour."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "They belong together because they are all for snack." / "They are all red leaves."
2. (Unexpected) "Because I said so." / "I changed it again."
3. (No response) Child looks at the finished shelf.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [impressed visitor tone] "Tour accepted. I can hear the rule and the evidence."
2. [warm challenge] "Curators do get the final say. Help the visitor see it with one clue they can notice."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle model] "I see a possible tour: 'These belong together because they all ____.'"

**Screen:** A tiny visitor ticket stamps "Rule explained." The exhibit title appears as "{child_made_grouping_rule} Gallery." The maybe shelf folds away or becomes a small "future exhibit" card.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [proud gallery host] "Tiny Curator, you used Connection. You showed why separate objects belong together. You used Form too. You noticed what each piece was like."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Can I make another exhibit?" / "I know a new rule."
2. (Unexpected) "Can these be sorted another way?" / "I want to add more."
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraged] "Yes. A new exhibit can use a new rule."
2. [excited extension] "That is advanced curator thinking. Same objects, second rule, new gallery."
3. [wait 2s] [soft celebration] "Your Tiny Curator badge is saved."

**Screen:** A "Tiny Curator" badge appears over the finished shelf. The words "Connection" and "Form" glow beside the exhibit label. A next-step card says, "Try a second rule with the same objects."
