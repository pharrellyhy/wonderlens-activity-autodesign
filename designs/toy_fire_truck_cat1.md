## Activity: Rescue Radio (救援呼叫站)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: Rescue Radio (救援呼叫站)
- **Activity Category**: 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device, Indoor, Quiet)
- **Recommended Tier**: T0 (ages 2–4)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Function** (How does it work?) & **Connection** (How is it connected to other things?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Safety, Role, Community, Helping
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Communication Skills (listening, expressing), Thinking Skills (creative thinking), Social Skills (empathy)
- **Trigger Entity**: Toy fire truck
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a red toy fire truck on the living room floor.

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After photographing a toy fire truck, the AI reacts with excitement and introduces a "Rescue Radio" — the child becomes the fire truck's dispatcher, deciding where the truck goes and what sound it makes for each emergency. Each round presents a tiny rescue scenario (a cat stuck up high, a puddle blocking the road, a teddy bear who is cold), and the child tells the fire truck what to do, complete with siren sounds and rescue calls.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the words "siren," "rescue," "emergency," "hose," "ladder." Learn that fire trucks help people and animals in different ways.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that a fire truck has a specific **Function** — it rescues and helps — and that helpers are **Connected** to the people and animals they serve.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice expressive vocalization and sound imitation (Communication Skills — expressing), imagine solutions to simple scenarios (Thinking Skills — creative thinking), and consider how helpers make others feel (Social Skills — empathy).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Rescue Radio" metaphor turns the child into the dispatcher — the one who tells the fire truck where to go. Each round is a different tiny emergency with a different rescue sound (WEE-OO! WHOOOOSH! SPLASH!). The child gets to make the sounds and decide what happens. The role of "Rescue Captain" celebrates the child as the one in charge of helping.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a toy fire truck on the living room floor → AI hears the "rescue radio" crackling → child dispatches the truck to 3 different mini-rescues.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T0 (ages 2–4)]

**Step 1: Transition Bridge**

> **AI says**: "*(gasping with excitement)* WOW! A fire truck! It's SO red and shiny! WEE-OO WEE-OO! Does your fire truck go fast?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah!" / "So fast!" / "VROOM!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's red!" / "It's mine!" / "I like trucks!"
> 3. (No response) Looks at the fire truck or screen silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* SO fast! VROOOOM! I bet it zooms to help everyone! Your fire truck is ready!"
> 2. "*(warm and agreeing)* It IS yours! And it's a super cool red truck! I love it! I bet it can go WEE-OO really loud!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(playful whisper)* I think I hear something... WEE-OO! Your fire truck wants to go! Should we help it?"
>
> **Screen**: The child's fire truck photo fills the center of the screen. Small red and orange sparkle animations pulse around the truck. A tiny animated siren light blinks on top.

**Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, like sharing a secret)* Guess what! Your truck has a rescue radio! CRACKLE CRACKLE! I hear someone who needs help! I'll tell you who — and YOU tell the truck where to go! Like this — FIRE TRUCK, GO HELP! Ready?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yeah!" / "Go!"
> 2. (Uncertain) "Help who?" / "What?"
> 3. (No response) Watches the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* HERE WE GO! WEE-OO WEE-OO! First rescue coming!"
> 2. "*(reassuring)* Someone fun! A little kitty! Or a teddy! I'll tell you — you just say GO! Let's try!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gently)* The radio says... BEEP BEEP! A kitty needs help! Can you say GO, FIRE TRUCK?"
>
> **Screen**: A small cartoon radio icon appears next to the fire truck photo, with animated crackle lines. Simple text-free icons show the pattern: radio icon → arrow → fire truck icon. The screen glows warmly.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction (3 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — Kitty Rescue (simple, high-success):**
>
> **AI says**: "*(urgent but playful)* BEEP BEEP! The radio says — a little kitty is stuck up high! Oh no! Can you tell the truck — GO HELP THE KITTY! Say it loud!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Go help the kitty!" / "GO!" / "Help kitty!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Meow!" / "I have a kitty!" / "Kitty is scared!"
> 3. (No response) Silent or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* The truck is ZOOMING! WEE-OO WEE-OO! It uses the big ladder — UP UP UP! The kitty is safe! HOORAY! You saved the kitty!"
> 2. "*(delighted)* MEOW! Yes! The kitty says HELP ME! Your fire truck hears it — ZOOM! Up goes the ladder! SAFE! You helped the kitty!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* The kitty is waiting! Can you say GO? ... *(whispers)* Just say GO and the truck will zoom!"
>
> **Screen**: A simple cartoon scene appears: a cute kitty icon sitting on top of a tall tree. When the child responds, the fire truck animates zooming across the screen with a ladder extending upward. The kitty hops onto the ladder. Hearts float up. The scene is bright and cheerful.

> **Round 2 — Puddle Rescue (introduce a different sound):**
>
> **AI says**: "*(radio crackle sound)* CRACKLE! New rescue! A big puddle is blocking the road! People can't walk! What sound does the hose make? WHOOOOSH! Can you say WHOOOOSH?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "WHOOOOSH!" / "Whoosh!" / attempts the sound
> 2. (Unexpected) "Splash!" / "Water!" / "I like puddles!"
> 3. (No response) Watching or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* WHOOOOSH! The water goes EVERYWHERE! The puddle is gone! The road is clear! You did it, Captain!"
> 2. "*(laughing)* SPLASH! Yes! Water goes SPLASH and WHOOOOSH! Your truck sprays and the puddle is GONE! People are so happy!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* Try it with me! WHOOOO... OOSH! Your turn! Even a little whoosh works!"
>
> **Screen**: A cartoon road with a big blue puddle appears. The fire truck rolls in from the left. When the child responds, a hose animation sprays water with a WHOOOOSH motion graphic. The puddle shrinks and disappears. Little cartoon people wave and cheer.

> **Round 3 — Teddy Bear Rescue (funny/surprising, peak engagement):**
>
> **AI says**: "*(giggling)* BEEP BEEP! Oh this is a funny one! A teddy bear is cold! It's shivering — BRRR BRRR! What should the fire truck bring? A warm blanket! Can you say BRRR for the teddy?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "BRRR!" / "Blanket!" / "Teddy is cold!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I have a teddy!" / "Fire!" / "Hug the teddy!"
> 3. (No response) Looks at screen or own toys.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm and cozy)* BRRR BRRR — here comes the blanket! WRAP WRAP! The teddy is warm now! It says THANK YOU! You're the best rescuer!"
> 2. "*(loving it)* YES! A hug! That's even BETTER than a blanket! The fire truck brings a hug AND a blanket! The teddy is SO happy! What a great idea!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(softly)* The teddy is going BRRR... can you go BRRR too? ... *(makes sound)* BRRR! Now the truck brings a cozy blanket! All warm!"
>
> **Screen**: A shivering cartoon teddy bear appears with animated shake lines. The fire truck arrives with a big cozy blanket on top. When the child responds, the blanket wraps around the teddy. The teddy smiles big. Warm golden glow fills the screen. Little star sparkles appear.

**Step 4: Celebration + Role Title**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* WOW! Three rescues! You saved a kitty, cleared a puddle, and warmed a teddy! You are a RESCUE CAPTAIN! The best one ever!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Cheers, laughs, repeats "Rescue Captain!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "More!" / "Again!" / "Another one!"
> 3. (No response) Smiles or looks at screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming)* Captain [child's pronoun]! Everyone is safe because of YOU! WEE-OO WEE-OO — victory siren!"
> 2. "*(excited)* You want MORE? I love it! You can play rescue anytime! But first — your special badge! You earned it!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(softly proud)* That's your badge, Captain. You helped everyone today. So brave!"
>
> **Screen**: A shining golden badge appears center screen with the text "RESCUE CAPTAIN" and a small fire truck icon in the middle. Confetti rains down in red and gold. The three rescue scenes (kitty, puddle, teddy) appear as tiny thumbnail icons around the badge. A celebration chime plays.

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective tone)* You know what you discovered? Your fire truck has a special job — it helps and rescues! That's called **Function** — what things DO! And every rescue connected you to a kitty, to people, to a teddy. That's called **Connection** — helpers are connected to everyone they help! You're an amazing Rescue Captain!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) "WEE-OO!" / "I'm a captain!" / waves at screen.
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or says nothing.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(joyful)* WEE-OO WEE-OO! Captain signing off! See you next rescue!"
> 2. "*(gentle)* Bye bye, Captain! Your badge is saved! See you next time!"
>
> **Screen**: The words **"Function"** and **"Connection"** appear artistically — "Function" is styled with tiny gear and wheel icons inside the letters, colored fire-engine red; "Connection" is styled with little dotted lines linking the letters together, with small heart icons at each link point. The fire truck photo sits centered below with the three rescue scene thumbnails fanning out from it, connected by gentle animated lines. Soft warm glow background. The Rescue Captain badge sits in the top corner.
