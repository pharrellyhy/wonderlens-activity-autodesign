## Activity: Green Treasure Hunt (绿色宝藏猎人)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: Green Treasure Hunt (绿色宝藏猎人)
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: Connection, Perspective
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Nutrition (mapping), Relationships (mapping), Wellbeing (mapping), Discovery (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking, transfer), Communication Skills (expressing)
- **Trigger Entity**: Green apple
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a green apple found under a tree in the garden
- **Mapping Source**: food_green_apple
- **IB Theme**: Who We Are (mapping: primary, weight=0.39)
- **Dimension Anchors**: appearance (physical), senses (physical), relationship (engagement)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: appearance — skin_speckles, shine_level; senses — texture

### B. Activity Overview

- **1. Brief Description**: The child discovers a green apple in the garden and becomes a "Green Treasure Hunter" on a mission to find other green things nearby. They photograph 3 green treasures, compare each find to the apple, and decide which treasure is most like the apple and which is most different — building a personal green collection along the way.

- **2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Green apples have glossy skin with pale speckles; green things in nature come in many shades; things can look similar but feel very different; apples grow on trees and hang from branches
  - **U (Understand)**: Many different things share the color green, but each one feels and looks unique in its own way (Connection); people can notice different things about the same object depending on what they pay attention to (Perspective)
  - **D (Do)**: Observe and describe visual details of natural objects (Research — observation); compare and sort items by shared and different features (Thinking — critical thinking); express personal preferences and reasoning (Communication — expressing)

- **3. Design Highlight**: The collection criterion is not just "green things" but specifically "green treasures that remind you of the apple." This forces the child to constantly compare each find back to the apple, deepening observation. The synthesis step asks which treasure is MOST like the apple and which is MOST different — a concrete perspective-taking exercise grounded in physical objects.

- **4. Typical Scenario**: A child in the garden photographs a green apple under a tree, then hunts for 3 more green treasures nearby, comparing each to the apple's shiny, smooth, speckled look.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the green apple.
> **Conversation anchor**: appearance — skin_speckles ("tiny pale dots sprinkled like apple freckles")
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* You found those tiny freckles on the apple! Little pale dots like sprinkles. I bet other green things hide freckles too. Want to go look?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah! Let's find more freckles!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to eat the apple."
> 3. (No response) Child looks around quietly or fidgets.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Great! Your eyes are sharp. Let's see what green treasures are hiding nearby!"
> 2. "*(warm, playful)* Apples are yummy! But first, let's go on a quick treasure hunt. We can find green things that look like your apple!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, encouraging)* Look at your apple's shiny green skin. What if we found more green things outside? Want to try?"
>
> **Screen**: Green apple photo centered with a soft golden glow around the speckled skin area. A small "conversation recap" badge shows a magnifying glass icon over the freckle detail.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start** (standalone)

> **Context**: Child photographs a green apple with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted surprise)* Wow, a green apple! Look how shiny and smooth it is. It is like a little green treasure ball. What do you think it feels like?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It feels smooth!" / "It's hard!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I found it on the ground!" / "It's yucky."
> 3. (No response) Child stares at the screen or touches the apple.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Smooth like a shiny marble! This apple is a real treasure. I wonder if there are more green treasures hiding in the garden."
> 2. "*(warm, validating)* You found it on the ground — like a hidden treasure! This shiny green apple is so cool. I bet more green treasures are hiding nearby."
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(playful, coaxing)* Feel it — is it smooth or bumpy? This green apple is so shiny! I think the garden has more green treasures for us."
>
> **Screen**: Green apple photo centered with a soft green glow. Tiny sparkle animations float around the apple to emphasize its glossy surface.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing — Green Treasure Hunter**

> **AI says**: "*(adventurous, rallying)* You are now a Green Treasure Hunter! Your mission has three parts. First, find three green things nearby. Second, take a photo of each one. Third, pick which treasure is most like the apple. Ready, hunter?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yes!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What if I can't find green things?"
> 3. (No response) Child looks uncertain or hesitates.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(pumped up)* Let's go! Look at leaves, grass, or anything green. Take a photo when you find one!"
> 2. "*(reassuring, conspiratorial)* Green things love to hide! Try looking at leaves on bushes, grass on the ground, or even green bugs. They are everywhere!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging, pointing)* Try looking down at the grass first. See anything green? Take a photo of it!"
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears with a "Green Treasure Hunter" badge at the top. Four collection slots are shown in a row — the first slot is filled with the green apple photo. Three empty slots glow softly, each marked with a question mark. A numbered checklist reads: 1) Find 3 green things. 2) Photograph each one. 3) Pick your favorite match.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

> **Round 1** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a green leaf)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited discovery)* Oh, a green leaf! Look at that! Is this leaf smooth and shiny like your apple, or does it feel different?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's bumpy!" / "It has lines on it."
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's just a leaf." / "I don't know."
> 3. (No response) Child waits silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* Bumpy with little lines — that is so different from the smooth apple! Great find, hunter. Two more treasures to go!"
> 2. "*(warm, extending)* A leaf is a great treasure! Feel it — is it smooth like the apple or a bit rough? Either way, your first green treasure is in the bag! Two more to go!"
> 3. "*(playful)* I see something green with tiny lines on it! Your leaf looks lighter green than the apple. Nice treasure! Two more to find!"
>
> **Screen**: Leaf photo slides into the second collection slot with a brief sparkle animation. A small counter shows "1 of 3 found." The apple and leaf photos sit side by side in their slots.

> **Round 2**:
>
> *(Child takes a photo of green grass or a green stem)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(impressed)* Look at that green! Does this one feel smooth and shiny like the apple, or is it thin and bendy?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's thin!" / "It bends!"
> 2. (Unexpected) Child describes color instead: "It's dark green."
> 3. (No response) Child waits.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(connecting)* Thin and bendy — the apple is round and firm, but this is long and soft. Same color, different shape! One more treasure, hunter!"
> 2. "*(validating)* Dark green — good eye! The apple is lighter green. You are great at noticing shades! One more treasure to go!"
> 3. "*(encouraging)* I see lots of green here! This one is way thinner than the round apple. One more treasure to find!"
>
> **Screen**: New photo slides into the third slot. Counter shows "2 of 3 found." Brief celebration animation — small green stars pop around the slot.

> **Round 3**:
>
> *(Child takes a photo of something else green — a green bug, moss, a green toy, a weed, etc.)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(thrilled)* One more green treasure! Does this feel the same as the apple, or totally different?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Totally different!" / Child describes a feature.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can I find more?" / "I want to keep going!"
> 3. (No response) Child seems tired or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebrating)* Totally different — but still green like the apple! Your collection is complete, Green Treasure Hunter!"
> 2. "*(warm, validating energy)* You love hunting! That is awesome. Let's look at all your treasures first, then you can keep exploring after!"
> 3. "*(gentle cheer)* You did it! Three green treasures plus your apple. That is four green things! Let's look at them all together."
>
> **Screen**: Final photo slides into the last slot with a burst of green confetti. All four slots now filled. A "Collection Complete!" banner appears briefly.

> **STUCK BRANCH** (if child cannot find a green item):
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful, conspiratorial)* Green things like to hide! Try looking at leaves on low bushes. Or check the grass near your feet. Even a tiny green stem counts as a treasure!"
>
> **If still stuck after second attempt**:
> **AI says**: "*(reassuring)* Look really close at the ground. See any green blades of grass? Or a small weed poking up? Take a photo of whatever you see!"
>
> **Screen**: A gentle pulsing arrow animation points downward, suggesting the child look at the ground. Small illustrated icons of leaves, grass, and stems appear around the border as visual hints.

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, curious)* Look at all your green treasures together! The apple, and three new friends. Now here is the big question — which treasure looks MOST like the apple? And which one looks the MOST different?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child picks one as most similar and one as most different, with a reason: "The leaf is most like it because it's shiny."
> 2. (Unexpected) Child picks one but cannot explain why: "That one." / Child picks the apple itself.
> 3. (No response) Child stares at the collection.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed, reflective)* The leaf is shiny like the apple — great thinking! And what about the most different one? You are really good at comparing things!"
> 2. "*(scaffolding warmly)* Good pick! Is it because they feel the same? Or look the same shape? You choose what matters! Every hunter sees things their own way."
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gently guiding)* Look at the pictures. Which green treasure is shiny and smooth like the apple? Point to it! And which one looks really different?"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos displayed in a grid layout. The apple photo has a small golden crown icon. Tap-to-select circles appear around each photo. When the child names a match, a dotted line draws between the apple and the chosen treasure. When they name the different one, a zigzag "different" line connects them.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* Amazing, Green Treasure Hunter! You found four green things in the garden. They are ALL green, but every single one feels different. The apple is smooth and round. The leaf has tiny lines. Why do you think so many things in the garden are green?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Because they grow!" / "The sun makes them green." / Creative answer.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know." / "Because green is pretty."
> 3. (No response) Child shrugs or looks around.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(awed)* Things that grow use green to soak up sunshine! You figured that out from your treasure hunt. That is so smart!"
> 2. "*(warm, encouraging)* Green IS pretty! And a lot of growing things pick green. Your eyes found so many different kinds. That is what makes you a great hunter!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(soft wonder)* It is a cool mystery! Green is nature's favorite color for growing things. You noticed how each green treasure is special and different."
>
> **Screen**: All four collection photos arranged in a circle with animated connection lines between them. Soft green sparkle particles float around the display. The center shows a small text: "All green, all different!"

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* You are an amazing Green Treasure Hunter! You found green treasures and compared every single one to your apple. You discovered that green things are connected — they share a color but each one is special. And you used your own perspective to choose which one matched the apple best. Nobody else would pick exactly like you! You earned your Green Treasure Hunter badge!"
>
> **Screen**: A "Green Treasure Hunter" badge animates onto the screen — a golden magnifying glass with a green apple inside and three small green treasure icons orbiting it. Below the badge, the words "Connection" and "Perspective" appear in artistic green-and-gold lettering with small icons: a chain link for Connection, an eye for Perspective. The four collection photos appear as small insets around the badge border. Gentle celebration music plays.
