## Activity: Helper Hotline (帮手热线)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: Helper Hotline (帮手热线)
- **Activity Category**: 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: Responsibility, Function
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Roles (mapping), Community (mapping), Safety (designed), Bravery (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Thinking Skills (creative thinking, transfer), Communication Skills (expressing ideas, listening), Self-Management Skills (emotional regulation)
- **Trigger Entity**: Firefighter (figure or picture)
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a firefighter figure or picture on the fridge
- **Mapping Source**: people_roles_firefighter
- **IB Theme**: How We Organize Ourselves (mapping: primary, weight=0.41)
- **Dimension Anchors**: emotions (engagement), relationship (engagement), function (physical)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: emotions — "What is the bravest thing you think firefighters do?"; function — "finding people and helping them get out safely"

### B. Activity Overview

- **Brief Description**: The child becomes a "Helper Hotline Operator" who receives pretend calls from people and animals who need help. Each round, the AI describes a new caller with a problem, and the child decides what the firefighter should do and what gear to bring. The child practices thinking about responsibility, tools, and helping others through imaginative role-play.
- **Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Firefighters help people get out safely (rescue_helping); they wear breathing gear to avoid smoke (smoke_protection); they check buildings and teach safety (fire_prevention); they use a radio with crackly beeps and voices (radio_sound); they wear thick gloves and a heavy coat with layers (jacket_layers, glove_color)
  - **U (Understand)**: Every tool a firefighter uses has a specific job — that is Function; firefighters choose to help others even when it is hard — that is Responsibility
  - **D (Do)**: Creative thinking — imagining what gear and actions fit each scenario; expressing ideas — explaining choices in words; transfer — applying firefighter knowledge to new situations
- **Design Highlight**: The "Helper Hotline" metaphor transforms the activity into a dispatch game. The child is not just answering questions — they are making decisions about who needs help and how. Each caller brings a different problem, so the child must think about which firefighter tools and actions match each situation.
- **Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a firefighter figure on the fridge, then plays a pretend dispatch game where they decide how to help different callers.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the firefighter.
> **Conversation anchor**: emotions — "What is the bravest thing you think firefighters do?"; function — "finding people and helping them get out safely"
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* You told me firefighters help people get out safely. That is so brave! What if we got to be the ones who send them on missions?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah! I want to send them!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to be the firefighter!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Then let's set up our Helper Hotline! You are the operator now."
> 2. "*(validating)* You would be an amazing firefighter! How about this — you tell the firefighters what to do. Like their boss!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Hey, what if we could call firefighters and give them a mission? Want to try?"
>
> **Screen**: Firefighter photo centered with a soft warm glow around it. A small "conversation recap" speech bubble shows a tiny firefighter icon helping someone, referencing the earlier conversation topic.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start** (standalone)

> **Context**: Child photographs a firefighter figure or picture with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted surprise)* Wow, a firefighter! Look at that brave helper. I bet this firefighter has saved so many people. If you could call this firefighter right now, what would you ask them to help with?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Help my cat!" / "Put out a fire!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know." / "Nothing."
> 3. (No response) Child is silent or looking around.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* That is a great idea! Firefighters help with all kinds of things. What if WE got to send them on missions?"
> 2. "*(encouraging)* That is okay! Firefighters get calls all day long. What if we pretended to answer those calls and decide what to do?"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(playful whisper)* I think this firefighter is waiting for a mission. Should we give them one?"
>
> **Screen**: Firefighter photo centered with a gentle pulsing glow. Small animated icons float around — a tiny helmet, a hose, and a fire truck — creating a playful atmosphere.

**Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo — Setting Up the Hotline**

> **AI says**: "*(playful, setting up the game)* Here is how our Helper Hotline works. Someone calls us and says they need help. You decide what the firefighter should do! Watch — I will show you."
>
> **AI says**: "*(mimicking a phone ring)* Ring ring! 'Hello, my kitten is stuck in a tree!' Hmm, what should we send? I think... a ladder! The firefighter can climb up and bring the kitten down. See? Your turn next!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Okay, I want to try!" / "My turn!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want the kitten!" / "Can I call?"
> 3. (No response) Child is watching quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(enthusiastic)* Great! Get ready — here comes the first call!"
> 2. "*(warm, redirecting)* The kitten is safe now! And here comes a new call on our hotline. Ready to answer it?"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* The phone is ringing! Should we pick it up together?"
>
> **Screen**: The firefighter photo moves to the top left corner. A large, colorful phone icon appears in the center with a "ring ring" animation (phone wiggling). After the demo, a small ladder icon slides in next to a happy kitten icon, showing the solved problem.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction (3–5 rounds)**

> **Round 1: The Smoky Kitchen** (simple, confidence-building)
>
> **AI says**: "*(phone ring sound effect)* Ring ring! 'Help! My kitchen is full of smoke and I cannot see!' Oh no! What should the firefighter bring to help?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A mask!" / "The breathing thing!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Water!" / "A fire truck!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Yes! The firefighter puts on breathing gear. It makes a soft whoosh-whoosh sound when they breathe. Now they can see through the smoke and help! Great thinking."
> 2. "*(validating)* Water is smart — firefighters do use water! And guess what — they also put on a special mask so they can breathe clean air in all that smoke. The mask makes a soft whoosh-whoosh sound. Cool, right?"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(helpful hint)* The kitchen is really smoky. The firefighter needs something to breathe clean air. What could help them breathe?"
>
> **Screen**: A cartoon kitchen scene with wispy gray smoke. When the child answers, a firefighter icon enters wearing a mask. Small "whoosh whoosh" text bubbles appear near the mask. The smoke clears gradually. A green checkmark appears when the problem is solved.

> **Round 2: The Scared Puppy** (introduces caring/responsibility angle)
>
> **AI says**: "*(phone ring)* Ring ring! 'There was a loud alarm and now my puppy is hiding under the bed. He is so scared!' Hmm, this one is tricky. What should the firefighter do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Talk to the puppy nicely!" / "Be gentle!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Pull it out!" / "Use a hose!"
> 3. (No response) Child is thinking.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(touched)* That is so kind! The firefighter kneels down and talks in a soft, calm voice. The puppy peeks out. You know what? Helping someone who is scared is a big responsibility."
> 2. "*(gently guiding)* Pulling might scare the puppy more! What if the firefighter got down low and talked softly? Sometimes helpers use a calm voice — that is one of their best tools."
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(warm prompt)* The puppy is really scared. What voice should the firefighter use — a loud voice or a soft voice?"
>
> **Screen**: A cozy room with a bed. A pair of puppy eyes peek out from underneath. When the child responds, the firefighter icon kneels down gently. The puppy slowly comes out. Small heart icons float up.

> **Round 3: The Neighbors Need to Know** (surprising/funny — peak engagement, introduces community)
>
> **AI says**: "*(phone ring)* Ring ring! 'Hello! I am cooking and the smoke alarm went off. The food just burned a little. But my neighbors are worried!' This time the firefighter needs to help the whole street feel safe. What should they do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Tell everyone it is okay!" / "Say it was just burned food!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Turn off the alarm!" / "Open a window!"
> 3. (No response) Child is unsure.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebratory)* Yes! The firefighter checks the kitchen, then goes outside and tells the neighbors, 'Everything is safe! Just some burned toast.' The whole street feels better. Firefighters help the whole community feel safe!"
> 2. "*(validating)* Opening a window is a great idea for the smoke! And then the firefighter can tell the neighbors everything is okay. Firefighters take care of the whole neighborhood — not just one house!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(scaffolding)* The neighbors are worried. Should the firefighter keep it a secret, or tell everyone it is safe?"
>
> **Screen**: A street view with several houses. Worried face icons appear in windows. When the child responds, the firefighter walks along the street and each worried face turns into a relieved smile. A "community safe" banner unfurls across the top.

> **Rounds 4–5 (if child is engaged): Open-Ended Calls**
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited)* You are SO good at this! Okay, now YOU make up the call. What problem does someone have? You tell me, and then you decide what the firefighter should do!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child invents a scenario: "A bird is on the roof!" / "There is water everywhere!"
> 2. (Unexpected) Child repeats an earlier scenario or says something silly: "A dinosaur is on fire!"
> 3. (No response) Child seems unsure what to invent.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* Oh wow, [repeats child's scenario]! What a call! Okay, what should the firefighter do about that?"
> 2. "*(laughing along)* A dinosaur! That IS an emergency! I think we need the biggest ladder AND the biggest hose. What do you think the firefighter says to the dinosaur?"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(offering a spark)* How about this — what if someone's ball got stuck way up high? Or their garden was flooded? You pick!"
>
> **Screen**: A blank "call card" appears in the center with a blinking cursor icon, inviting the child to fill in the scenario. When the child speaks, a cartoon version of their scenario appears. Firefighter icon waits for orders.

**Step 4: Celebration + Role Title**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* You just answered ALL those calls! You helped with smoke, a scared puppy, and the whole neighborhood. You are now an official Helper Hotline Hero! You knew exactly what each person needed."
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child cheers, laughs, or says "I did it!"
> 2. (Unexpected) Child wants to keep playing: "More calls!"
> 3. (No response) Child is smiling quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming)* You sure did! Every caller got the right help because of you."
> 2. "*(warm)* You are such a great operator! We can play again another day. But first, let me tell you something amazing about what you just did."
> 3. "*(gentle celebration)* I am giving you a big gold star. You helped so many people today!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden badge animation fills the center of the screen reading "Helper Hotline Hero" with a small firefighter helmet icon at the top. Confetti rains down. The firefighter photo reappears surrounded by the small icons of each caller (kitten, puppy, neighbors) all smiling.

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective)* You know what you showed me today? You figured out that every tool a firefighter uses has a special job — the mask for breathing, the ladder for climbing, the calm voice for scared friends. That is called Function — everything has a purpose! And you chose to help every single caller, even when it was tricky. That is called Responsibility — caring about others and taking action. You are a true helper!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child listens and smiles, or says "I like helping!"
> 2. (Unexpected) Child asks "What is function?" or repeats the word.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(proud)* And helpers like you make the whole community better. Great job today!"
> 2. "*(encouraging)* Function means everything has a job to do! The helmet protects the head. The hose sprays water. You figured that out all by yourself!"
> 3. "*(gentle closing)* You did an amazing job today. See you next time, Helper Hotline Hero!"
>
> **Screen**: The words **"Function"** and **"Responsibility"** appear in large, friendly lettering. "Function" is surrounded by small gear icons (helmet, mask, ladder, hose). "Responsibility" is surrounded by heart and handshake icons. The firefighter photo sits between them with a warm glow. The "Helper Hotline Hero" badge is displayed below.
