## Activity: The Rainbow Color Scout

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Rainbow Color Scout
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4-6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Causation** (Why is it like this? — sun and rain together cause colors to appear) & **Change** (How is it changing? — the rainbow fades as weather shifts, colors appear and disappear)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Energy (mapping — light energy from the sun spreads into colors), Cycles (mapping — rain-then-sun pattern repeats), Properties (mapping — bright vs faint, color order), Discovery (designed — finding hidden colors in the world)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking — comparing color sources), Self-Management Skills (organization — completing a multi-step outdoor mission)
- **Trigger Entity**: Double rainbow
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a rainbow in the sky after a rain shower
- **Mapping Source**: natural_phenomena_double_rainbow
- **IB Theme**: How the World Works (mapping: primary, weight=0.57)
- **Dimension Anchors**: appearance (physical — color bands, brightness, second arc faintness), senses (physical — cool air, no sound, cannot touch), relationship (engagement — comparing rainbow to other colorful things)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: appearance — second_arc_faintness ("a lighter second bow, like a whisper of colors"), senses — temperature ("air can feel cool")

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After the child photographs a double rainbow, the AI marvels at the two colorful arcs in the sky and wonders where else those bright colors might be hiding down on the ground. The child becomes a "Rainbow Color Scout" — searching the nearby area for things that show rainbow colors (red flowers, yellow leaves, orange berries, blue puddle reflections). After photographing 3 colorful finds, the child lines them up to build a ground-level rainbow and reflects on why the sky and the ground can share the same colors.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn that sun and rain together make a rainbow. Learn that the outer rainbow is fainter than the inner one. Learn that rainbow colors appear in order (red, orange, yellow, green, blue). Learn that light from the sun holds many colors inside it. Learn the word "faint" (lighter, harder to see).
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that rainbows are caused by sunlight passing through raindrops — sun and rain working together is the **Causation** (**why** rainbows appear). Understand that rainbows **Change** quickly — they fade as clouds move or rain stops, showing how weather scenes transform in minutes.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice careful outdoor observation by spotting colors that match the rainbow (Research Skills — observation). Practice photographing and collecting color evidence systematically (Research Skills — data collection). Practice comparing color qualities across different objects (Thinking Skills — critical thinking).

- **③ Design Highlight**: Since a child cannot collect more rainbows, the mission cleverly flips the task: bring the rainbow DOWN to earth by finding objects on the ground that match each rainbow color. The "Rainbow Color Scout" metaphor transforms the ephemeral sky event into a hands-on treasure hunt. The synthesis moment — lining up ground finds to mirror the sky arc — creates a satisfying visual parallel that reinforces causation thinking (same light energy, different sources of color).

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a double rainbow after a rain shower → AI reacts with wonder at the two arcs → child becomes a "Rainbow Color Scout" searching for rainbow colors nearby → photographs 3 colorful finds → arranges them into a ground-level rainbow → celebrates and reflects on why sun and rain make colors appear.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1 (ages 4-6)]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the double rainbow.
> **Conversation anchor**: appearance — second_arc_faintness ("a lighter second bow, like a whisper of colors")
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* You noticed the second rainbow is fainter — like a whisper of colors up there! All those bright colors in the sky... I wonder if we can find those same colors hiding down here on the ground. Want to go look?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes!" / "Let's find them!" / "I see some colors already!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "But the rainbow is up there." / "Colors on the ground?" / "I want to look at the rainbow more."
> 3. (No response) Child gazes at the sky or looks around quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Let's go! The rainbow put its colors in the sky. But I bet the same colors are hiding all around us too!"
> 2. "*(validating, then redirecting)* The rainbow IS beautiful up there! But guess what — those same colors live down here too. Red flowers, yellow leaves... let's find them!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, encouraging)* Look around your feet. See anything red? Or yellow? The rainbow's colors might be closer than you think!"
>
> **Screen**: The child's rainbow photo is centered with a soft shimmering glow around the arc. A faint dotted line curves downward from the rainbow to the ground, with tiny color dots (red, orange, yellow, green, blue) scattered along the bottom edge — hinting at colors to find below.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs a double rainbow with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(amazed gasp)* Wow — TWO rainbows! Look at all those colors stretching across the sky! The big one is so bright and the little one is like its shy twin. Those are some amazing colors up there. Do they make you feel happy or surprised?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Happy!" / "So pretty!" / "I love rainbows!" / "Surprised!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I see red and blue." / "Where does it end?" / "Can I touch it?"
> 3. (No response) Child stares at the rainbow quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, delighted)* Me too! Rainbows always make me feel sparkly inside. I wonder — those beautiful colors are way up in the sky. Can we find them down here too?"
> 2. "*(curious and warm)* Great eyes — red and blue are up there! You can't quite touch a rainbow, but you CAN find its colors right here on the ground. Want to try?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft wonder)* It's so beautiful, isn't it? Two rainbow smiles in the sky. I wonder if we can find those same colors hiding around us..."
>
> **Screen**: The double rainbow photo fills the screen with a gentle sparkling animation along both arcs. Soft pastel light radiates outward. A tiny animated raindrop falls through a beam of light and splits into color — a simple visual hint at how rainbows form.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, conspiratorial tone)* Here's a fun idea! You are now a Rainbow Color Scout! The rainbow put its colors in the sky. Your mission: find 3 things nearby that show rainbow colors! A red flower, a yellow leaf, anything colorful. Take a photo of each one. Then we'll make our OWN rainbow right here. Ready, Color Scout?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes!" / "I'm ready!" / "I see something red!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What if I can't find any?" / "What colors?" / "Can I find blue?"
> 3. (No response) Child hesitates or looks around slowly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* Go, Color Scout! Find anything colorful and take a photo. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue — any rainbow color counts!"
> 2. "*(helpful, upbeat)* Any rainbow color works! Red, orange, yellow, green, blue — pick your favorites. Look near flowers, leaves, or even puddles!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* Look near the ground. Flowers, leaves, even wet rocks can be colorful. Find something bright and take a photo!"
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears with "Rainbow Color Scout" in bold, gradient-colored lettering (red through blue). Four collection slots are shown in a curved arc shape — the first slot holds the rainbow photo, the other three are empty with dotted outlines in different rainbow colors (red, yellow, blue). Below, three numbered task icons: (1) magnifying glass, (2) camera, (3) rainbow arc.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3-4 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — First Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of something colorful nearby)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited discovery)* Ooh! You found one! What color is this? Is it a rainbow color?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's red!" / "Yellow!" / "I found a green leaf!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a rock." / "I don't know." / Names the object but not color.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* Red — just like the top of the rainbow! Great find, Color Scout! That's 1 down. 2 more to go!"
> 2. "*(validating, then guiding)* Nice find! And look at the color on it — I see some warm tones there. That matches the rainbow! 2 more to go!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm)* Let me look... I can see color in there! That could match the rainbow. Nice work, Color Scout! 2 more to go!"
>
> **Screen**: The new photo slides into the second slot with a gentle color-burst animation. A small "2/4" counter updates. The filled slot glows with the dominant color of the photographed item.

> **Round 2 — Second Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of another colorful item)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(impressed)* Another one! Is this the same color as your first find, or a new color?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A new color!" / "This one is orange!" / "Different — more yellow!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's the same!" / "I like this one better." / Describes the object.
> 3. (No response) Quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(fascinated)* A different color! Now you have two rainbow colors in your collection. The rainbow has so many — let's keep going! 1 more!"
> 2. "*(warm, encouraging)* It IS a good one! Every find adds to your rainbow collection. You're doing great! 1 more to go!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* Another beautiful color for your collection! Your ground rainbow is growing. Just 1 more!"
>
> **Screen**: Third slot fills with a color-burst animation. A faint gradient line briefly connects the filled slots, hinting at a rainbow forming. Counter shows "3/4."

> **Round 3 — Third Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a third colorful item)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(celebrating)* YES! Color Scout, you did it! Three rainbow colors found right here on the ground! Which one is your favorite find?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The red one!" / "I like the blue!" / Points to one.
> 2. (Unexpected) "All of them!" / "I want to find more!" / "The rainbow is gone now."
> 3. (No response) Looks at the collection on screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(admiring)* Great pick! That one really pops. Your collection is complete! Now let's do something fun with it..."
> 2. "*(warm, validating)* They're ALL great finds! And you know what — even if the sky rainbow fades, YOUR colors are right here. Collection complete! Now for the fun part..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm)* Look at all those colors! Your collection is complete. Let's see what we can do with them..."
>
> **Screen**: The final slot fills with a big celebration animation — all four slots pulse with color simultaneously. Tiny rainbow sparkles burst around the collection arc. Counter shows "4/4 — COMPLETE!"

> **STUCK BRANCH** (if child says "I can't find anything colorful" or pauses for a long time):
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful, upbeat)* Let me help! Look down at the ground near flowers or bushes. Petals, berries, and leaves can be super colorful! Even a puddle can show rainbow colors if the light hits it. Go peek near a flower!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Finds something) Takes a photo.
> 2. (Still stuck) "There's nothing colorful here."
> 3. (No response) Wanders without clear direction.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. *(Proceeds to the relevant round's reaction above)*
> 2. "*(reassuring)* That's okay! Try looking at leaves — even green is a rainbow color! Or check if there are any small flowers hiding. A yellow dandelion or a brown pinecone counts too!"
> 3. *(waits 3 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Walk over to the nearest plant. Look at its leaves and flowers. Any color there? Even green counts — green is right in the middle of the rainbow!"
>
> **Screen**: A gentle pulsing compass icon appears with illustrated arrows pointing toward flowers, puddles, and leaves on the ground. Hint text appears in a rainbow-gradient speech bubble: "Look near flowers, leaves, or puddles!"

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, building excitement)* Color Scout, look at your four treasures! The rainbow put its colors in the sky. And you found those SAME colors right here on the ground! Can you put them in rainbow order? Red first... then orange... yellow... green... blue! Which one goes first?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The red one!" / "This one is warmest!" / Names a color order.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know the order." / "They're all different." / Picks randomly.
> 3. (No response) Looks at the photos quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Yes! Red goes first, just like in the rainbow up there! Then the next warmest color... You're building your very own ground rainbow!"
> 2. "*(scaffolding gently)* Let's try together! Think of the rainbow in the sky — red is on top, then orange, yellow, green, blue. Which of your finds looks most red? That one goes first!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm, guiding)* Here's a trick — look at the rainbow in your first photo. See how red is on top? Find your reddest treasure and put it first!"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos are displayed in a curved arc shape at the center of the screen. The child's verbal sorting is reflected by photos gently sliding into new positions with smooth animation. A faint reference rainbow arc appears above as a guide. When the child completes the sort, a golden "GROUND RAINBOW" label arches over the arranged photos with sparkle effects.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* WOW! You built a rainbow on the ground! The sky rainbow was made by sun and rain. And YOUR ground rainbow was made by your sharp eyes! Why do you think there are so many colors out here after the rain?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Because the sun came out!" / "The rain made things wet and shiny!" / "Nature has lots of colors!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know!" / "Because flowers are pretty!" / "I like colors."
> 3. (No response) Smiles or looks at the collection.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(fascinated)* That's a wonderful idea! The sun IS shining after the rain — and sunlight has all the rainbow colors hiding inside it. When light hits raindrops, the colors come out! That's what CAUSED the rainbow up there!"
> 2. "*(validating)* Flowers ARE full of beautiful color! And you know what? The sun's light has all those colors inside it. When the light goes through raindrops, the colors spread out — and THAT is what makes the rainbow appear!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(reflective)* I think it's because sunlight holds many colors inside. When it goes through raindrops, those colors come out and spread across the sky. Sun plus rain equals rainbow!"
>
> **Screen**: The four photos are displayed in arc order with gentle animated lines connecting them. Above, the child's original rainbow photo is shown smaller, creating a visual parallel between sky rainbow and ground rainbow. Animated light beams pass through a raindrop icon and split into colors — a simple causation diagram. A warm golden light pulses behind the display.

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, proud)* Rainbow Color Scout, you were amazing today! You discovered **Causation** — WHY rainbows appear. Sun and rain work together to make those colors in the sky! And you saw **Change** happen — the rainbow faded and shifted right before your eyes, because weather changes so fast. But YOUR ground rainbow is here to stay! You earned your Color Scout badge. Go show someone your rainbow collection!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) "I will!" / "That was fun!" / "I want to find more colors!"
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or nods.
> 3. (No response) Child watches the badge animation quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm goodbye)* It WAS fun! Next time it rains and the sun peeks out, look for another rainbow. Bye, Color Scout!"
> 2. "*(gentle)* You found something really special today. Bye bye, Color Scout!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft)* Your rainbow collection is beautiful. Bye bye, Color Scout!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden badge animation appears — a shield shape with two curved rainbow arcs (mimicking the double rainbow). The text "Rainbow Color Scout" is displayed below in bold, gradient lettering (red through blue). The four collection photos appear as small insets around the badge. The words **"Causation"** and **"Change"** float upward in artistic lettering — "Causation" styled with a sun icon on one side and a raindrop icon on the other with a rainbow arc connecting them, "Change" with a fading-gradient effect (bright to faint). Rainbow-colored confetti drifts down. A triumphant chime plays.
