## Activity: The Warm Color Treasure Hunt

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Warm Color Treasure Hunt
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4-6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Form** (What is it like? — observing and comparing colors) & **Connection** (How is it connected to other things? — linking warm colors across different objects in nature)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Pattern, Discovery, Classification, Expression
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking — sorting and classifying), Self-Management Skills (organization — completing a multi-step mission)
- **Trigger Entity**: Autumn leaf (bright red)
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a bright red leaf on the ground in the park

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After the child photographs a bright red autumn leaf, the AI marvels at its fiery color and wonders if autumn is painting the whole park in warm colors. The child becomes a "Flame Finder" — an explorer on a mission to collect warm-colored treasures (reds, oranges, yellows) hiding around the park. After photographing 3 finds, the child sorts them from lightest yellow to deepest red, creating their own autumn color rainbow.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the names of warm colors (red, orange, yellow). Learn that autumn leaves change color. Learn the word "gradient" (lightest to darkest). Learn that many different things in nature can share the same color family.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that warm colors share a visual quality that connects very different objects — a leaf, a berry, a flower can all belong to the same color family (**Connection**). Understand that colors have a range — not just "red" but light red, deep red, orange-red — and this range is a kind of **Form**.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice careful observation by identifying warm colors in the environment (Research Skills — observation). Practice collecting and photographing items systematically (Research Skills — data collection). Practice sorting items by a visual property — color gradient (Thinking Skills — critical thinking).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Flame Finder" metaphor transforms color-hunting into a heroic quest — the child is rescuing tiny flames of color before winter arrives. The synthesis task (sorting by gradient) gives children a tangible, visual accomplishment: their very own autumn rainbow arranged from palest gold to deepest crimson.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a bright red leaf on the ground in the park → AI reacts to the fiery color → child becomes a "Flame Finder" collecting warm-colored treasures → photographs 3 more finds → sorts them into a color gradient → celebrates their autumn rainbow.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1 (ages 4-6)]

**Step 1: Transition Bridge**

> **AI says**: "*(amazed gasp)* Whoa! Look at that red! This leaf is glowing like a tiny flame! I bet autumn painted it. What do you think it looks like?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It looks like fire!" / "It's like a flame!" / "It's so red!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a leaf." / "I found it on the ground." / "It's pretty."
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* Yes! A tiny flame on the ground! Autumn is hiding warm colors everywhere. I wonder where else..."
> 2. "*(warm and curious)* It IS a leaf — and such a beautiful red one! It looks warm, like a little sunset. Don't you think?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle wonder)* I think it looks like a little piece of sunset. So warm and bright!"
>
> **Screen**: The red leaf photo fills the screen with a soft golden-orange glow radiating outward from the leaf. Tiny animated ember-like sparkles drift gently upward from the leaf's edges.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, conspiratorial tone)* I think autumn is hiding warm colors all over the park! Reds, oranges, yellows — like little flames. You could be a Flame Finder! Here's your mission: Find 3 more warm-colored things. Take a photo of each one. Then we'll make a color rainbow! Ready, Flame Finder?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes!" / "I'm ready!" / "Let's go!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What's warm-colored?" / "Like what?" / "I see one already!"
> 3. (No response) Child looks around or hesitates.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* Go, Flame Finder! Look for anything red, orange, or yellow. Take a photo when you find one!"
> 2. "*(helpful, encouraging)* Great question! Warm colors are red, orange, and yellow — like fire! Look around. Anything that color counts!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* Try looking around you. Any red, orange, or yellow things nearby? Those are warm colors!"
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears with the title "Flame Finder" in bold, warm-gradient lettering. Four collection slots are shown — the first slot holds the red leaf photo, the other three are empty with a dotted flame-shaped outline. Below, three numbered icons: (1) a magnifying glass, (2) a camera, (3) a rainbow arc.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3-4 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — First Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of something warm-colored)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited discovery)* Ooh! You found one! Tell me — what color is this one? Is it more red, orange, or yellow?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's orange!" / "Yellow!" / "It's like red and orange."
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a flower." / "I don't know." / Names something unrelated.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* Orange — like a warm sunset! Great find, Flame Finder! That's 2 in your collection. 2 more to go!"
> 2. "*(validating, then guiding)* A flower — nice! And look at that warm color on it. I think it's a bit orange. Another flame for your collection! 2 more to go!"
> 3. "*(warm)* Let me look... I see warm color in there! Looks like it could be a golden-yellow. Beautiful! 2 more to go!"
>
> **Screen**: The new photo slides into the second slot with a gentle flame burst animation. A small "2/4" counter updates. The filled slots glow softly.

> **Round 2 — Second Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of another warm-colored item)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(impressed)* Another one! You have sharp eyes! Does this one look the same color as your leaf, or different?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Different! This is lighter." / "It's more yellow." / "Same red!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I like this one!" / "It's pretty." / Describes the object, not color.
> 3. (No response) Quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(fascinated)* You're right — it IS different! See how warm colors can look so many ways? Flame Finder, you're getting good at this! 1 more to go!"
> 2. "*(agreeing warmly)* It IS pretty! And it adds a new shade to your collection. Every warm color is a little bit different. 1 more to go!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* This one is lovely! I think it's a different shade from your leaf. Your collection is growing! 1 more to go!"
>
> **Screen**: Third slot fills in. A subtle gradient animation briefly connects the filled slots, hinting at the color spectrum forming. Counter shows "3/4."

> **Round 3 — Third Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a third warm-colored item)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(celebrating)* YES! Flame Finder, you did it! Look at all those warm colors! Which one do you think is the warmest — the most fiery one?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The red one!" / "This one!" / Points to or names one.
> 2. (Unexpected) "All of them!" / "I want to find more!" / Describes something else.
> 3. (No response) Looks at the collection on screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(admiring)* Ooh, good choice! That one really does look like a flame. Your collection is complete! Time for the fun part..."
> 2. "*(laughing warmly)* They ARE all fiery! You built an amazing set. Your collection is complete! Now for something cool..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm)* I think they're all beautiful. Your collection is complete! Let's do something special with them..."
>
> **Screen**: The final slot fills with a big celebration animation — all four slots pulse with a warm glow simultaneously. Small firework-like sparkles in red, orange, and yellow burst around the collection. Counter shows "4/4 — COMPLETE!"

> **STUCK BRANCH** (if child says "I can't find anything" or takes a long pause between finds):
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful, upbeat)* Hmm, let me think! Try looking down at the ground — fallen leaves and petals hide there. Or check near bushes — berries and flowers love warm colors! Even tree bark can be orange-ish. Go take a peek!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Finds something) Takes a photo.
> 2. (Still stuck) "I still can't find any."
> 3. (No response) Wanders without direction.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. *(Proceeds to the relevant round's reaction above)*
> 2. "*(reassuring)* That's okay! Look up — do you see any leaves still on the trees? Some might be yellow or orange. Even a brown stick counts — brown is a warm color too!"
> 3. *(waits 3 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Try going closer to a tree. Look at the leaves, the ground around it. Any yellow or orange hiding there?"
>
> **Screen**: A gentle pulsing compass-like icon appears with arrows pointing toward illustrated bushes, ground, and tree bark. The hint text "Look near the ground, bushes, or tree bark!" appears briefly in a warm-colored speech bubble.

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, building excitement)* Flame Finder, look at your four treasures! Now — here's the big challenge. Can you put them in order? Lightest color first, darkest last. Like a rainbow going from yellow... to orange... to red! Which one goes first?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The yellow one first!" / "This one is lightest!" / Names an order.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know how." / "They're all the same." / Picks randomly.
> 3. (No response) Looks at the photos quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Yes! Start with the lightest one. Then a bit darker... and the deepest red at the end! You made an autumn rainbow! That's called a gradient — colors going from light to dark!"
> 2. "*(scaffolding gently)* Let's try together! Which one looks the palest — the most like sunshine? That goes first. And which one is the darkest red? That goes last. You decide the middle!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm, guiding)* Here's a trick — find the palest, most yellow one. That goes first! Then the deepest, darkest one goes last. What about the ones in between?"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos are displayed in a horizontal row at the center of the screen. The child's verbal sorting is reflected by the photos gently rearranging themselves with a smooth sliding animation. A gradient bar (yellow → orange → red) appears below the row as a visual reference. When the child completes the sort, a golden "AUTUMN RAINBOW" label arches above the arranged photos.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* WOW! You made a real autumn rainbow! Four different things — but they all share warm colors. Isn't that amazing? Why do you think autumn has so many warm colors?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Because the leaves change!" / "Because it's getting cold!" / "Autumn likes warm colors!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know!" / "Because it's pretty!" / "I like red."
> 3. (No response) Smiles or looks at collection.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(fascinated)* What a great idea! The leaves ARE changing. Nature makes its own art show before winter comes. You noticed that!"
> 2. "*(validating)* It IS pretty! I think nature puts on a color show every autumn — like painting the park in sunset colors. You found the evidence!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(reflective)* I think autumn is like nature's art show. All these warm colors appearing before winter. And YOU collected them!"
>
> **Screen**: The four photos are displayed in gradient order with gentle animated lines connecting them in an arc — forming a visual "rainbow." Animated leaves in red, orange, and yellow drift softly across the screen behind the collection. A warm golden light pulses behind the entire display.

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, proud)* Flame Finder, you were amazing today! You noticed the **Form** of each color — every warm shade was a little different, from pale yellow to deep red. And you found a **Connection** — leaves, flowers, berries, all sharing the same warm color family! You earned your Flame Finder badge. Go show your autumn rainbow to someone you love!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) "I will!" / "That was fun!" / "I want to find more!"
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or nods.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm goodbye)* It WAS fun! Keep looking for warm colors — they're hiding everywhere. Bye, Flame Finder!"
> 2. "*(gentle)* You did something really special today. Bye bye, Flame Finder!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden badge animation appears — a shield shape with a stylized flame icon in gradient colors (yellow-orange-red). The text "Flame Finder" is displayed below in bold, warm lettering. The four collection photos appear as small insets around the badge. The words **"Form"** and **"Connection"** float upward in artistic lettering — "Form" styled with a gradient fill from yellow to red, "Connection" with tiny dotted lines linking the letters together. Warm confetti drifts down. A triumphant chime plays.
