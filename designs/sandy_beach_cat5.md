## Activity: The Shore Detective Agency

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Shore Detective Agency
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Causation** (Why is it like this?) & **Connection** (How is it connected to other things?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Habitat (mapping), Diversity (mapping), Discovery (designed), Conservation (mapping)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking — cause-and-effect reasoning), Self-Management Skills (focus — sustained physical search across a beach setting)
- **Trigger Entity**: Sandy beach
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs the sandy shore at the beach.
- **Mapping Source**: nature_landscapes_sandy_beach
- **IB Theme**: Sharing the Planet (mapping: primary, weight=0.42)
- **Dimension Anchors**: appearance (physical), structure (physical), relationship (engagement)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: appearance — sand_grain_sparkle, wet_dry_color_change; structure — shell_bits

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After photographing the sandy shore, the AI notices the mix of tiny treasures hidden in the sand — sparkly grains, dark shell bits, and smooth pebble pieces — and wonders WHO or WHAT delivered them there. The child becomes a "Shore Detective" on a mission to find and photograph 3 beach clues (things the ocean left behind), figure out where each one came from, and then piece together the story of how the ocean delivers gifts to the shore.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn that sand has tiny shell pieces mixed in. Learn that wet sand looks darker than dry sand. Learn the words "shore," "clue," "washed up," and "tide." Learn that waves carry things from the ocean to the beach.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that things found on the beach got there because waves and tides carried them — each item has a CAUSE for being there (**Causation**). Understand that the ocean, sand, shells, and seaweed are all linked — they share a **Connection** as parts of one beach system.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice close observation by spotting small objects mixed into the sand (Research Skills — observation). Practice collecting evidence and describing where it came from (Research Skills — data collection). Practice cause-and-effect reasoning by explaining WHY each clue ended up on the shore (Thinking Skills — critical thinking).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Shore Detective" metaphor reframes beachcombing as a mystery investigation. Every shell, bit of seaweed, or smooth pebble is a "clue" the ocean left behind. The child is not just collecting — they are solving the mystery of how the ocean delivers things to the shore. The synthesis asks the child to connect ALL the clues into one ocean delivery story, turning science into narrative.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs the sandy shore → AI spots tiny treasures in the sand → child becomes a "Shore Detective" → finds and photographs 3 ocean clues → explains where each came from → tells the full story of the ocean's deliveries → celebrates as a Shore Detective.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the sandy beach.
> **Conversation anchor**: appearance — sand_grain_sparkle ("Tiny grains that can glitter in sunshine"); structure — shell_bits ("Mixed-in tiny shell pieces with sharp edges")
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* Remember how we saw those tiny sparkly grains in the sand? And those sharp little shell bits hiding in there too? I keep thinking — how did all those pieces GET here? What if we could solve that mystery?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The ocean brought them!" / "The waves did it!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know" / "Maybe someone put them there"
> 3. (No response) Child looks around or stays quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Yes! The waves carry things here! I bet there are more ocean clues hiding on this beach. Want to find them?"
> 2. "*(encouraging)* That is a good guess! I think the waves might be the answer. They wash things onto the shore. Want to look for clues together?"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, inviting)* Look down at the sand near your feet. See anything the ocean might have left behind?"
>
> **Screen**: Child's beach photo centered. Sparkly grain areas and shell bits subtly highlighted with a soft gold glow. A small "conversation recap" icon pulses near the highlighted features.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs the sandy beach with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted, amazed)* Wow, look at this sandy shore! I see so many tiny treasures hiding in the sand — sparkly bits, little shell pieces, smooth spots. It is like the ocean left presents on the beach! What do you see in the sand?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I see shells!" / "There are little shiny things!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It is just sand" / "I see water"
> 3. (No response) Child is looking around or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Great eyes! Those shells did not just appear. The ocean waves washed them right onto the sand. I wonder what else the ocean left here!"
> 2. "*(warm, curious)* Sand is pretty cool, right? But look really close. Wet sand looks darker than dry sand. And there are tiny pieces mixed in. The ocean hides its gifts well!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(playful whisper)* Psst — look really close at the sand. See any tiny bits that are not just sand grains?"
>
> **Screen**: Child's beach photo centered. Gentle wave animation along the bottom edge. Small sparkle effects appear on sand highlights in the photo.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(adventurous, confident)* I have a mission for you! You are now a Shore Detective! Your job is to solve the mystery of what the ocean left behind."
>
> "*(counting on fingers tone)* Here is your mission: First, find three things the ocean washed up on the beach. Second, take a photo of each one. Third, tell me WHERE you think it came from!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Okay!" / "I want to find clues!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What should I look for?" / "I do not know what to find"
> 3. (No response) Child seems unsure or hesitant.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(pumped up)* Let us go, Detective! Walk along the sand and look for shells, seaweed, smooth stones, or driftwood. When you spot a clue, take a photo!"
> 2. "*(helpful, warm)* Great question! Look for things that do not look like plain sand. Shells, seaweed, little sticks, or smooth pebbles. They are all ocean clues! Go explore!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, encouraging)* Try walking along the wet sand near the water. That is where the waves drop the best clues. You will find something!"
>
> **Screen**: Mission card appears with a detective badge labeled "Shore Detective." Four slots displayed in a row — the first slot filled with the child's original beach photo, three empty slots with question marks and magnifying glass icons. A numbered task list below reads: "1. Find 3 ocean clues, 2. Photograph each one, 3. Tell me where it came from."

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

> **Round 1** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of something they found — e.g., a seashell)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(thrilled, detective voice)* Clue number one! Ooh, that looks smooth and curved. What did you find, Detective? Tell me about it!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A shell!" / "It is a seashell! It is white."
> 2. (Unexpected) "A rock" / "I do not know what it is"
> 3. (No response) Child just took the photo silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* A shell, yes! Shells come from animals that live in the ocean. The waves washed this one right onto the sand. Where do you think it lived before? In the deep water or close to shore?"
> 2. "*(warm, validating)* Nice find! It does look rocky. I think it might be a piece of shell that the waves smoothed down. The ocean is like a big tumbler — it rolls things around until they get smooth. That is one clue solved!"
> 3. "*(encouraging)* I see something smooth and light colored. It looks like it has been tumbled by the waves! Is it hard or soft when you touch it?"
>
> **Screen**: Photo slides into slot 2. Counter updates to "1 of 3 clues found." A small celebration burst of bubbles animates around the new photo. A dotted line connects the beach photo to the new find.

> **Round 2**:
>
> *(Child takes a photo — e.g., a piece of seaweed)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(curious, sniffing tone)* Clue number two! Hmm, this one looks slippery and green. What is it?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Seaweed!" / "Slimy green stuff!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "A leaf" / "Grass"
> 3. (No response) Silent.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Seaweed! It grows under the water and waves rip it loose and push it onto the sand. The tide is like a delivery truck! Two clues down, one more to go!"
> 2. "*(validating)* It does look like a leaf! It is actually seaweed — a plant that grows in the ocean. The waves tore it off and carried it right here. Cool, right? Two clues down!"
> 3. "*(playful)* I see something green and wet looking. That is seaweed! It grew in the ocean and waves carried it to the shore. Two clues found, one more to go!"
>
> **Screen**: Photo slides into slot 3. Counter updates to "2 of 3 clues found." Bubble celebration. Connection line extends.

> **Round 3**:
>
> *(Child takes a photo — e.g., a smooth pebble or piece of driftwood)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited, building suspense)* Clue number three! This could be the final piece of the puzzle! What did you find this time?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A smooth rock!" / "A piece of wood!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "A bottle cap" / "Something weird"
> 3. (No response) Silent.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebratory)* A smooth rock! The ocean rolled it around for a long, long time. That is why it feels so smooth. The waves are like a big polishing machine! All three clues are in!"
> 2. "*(validating, thoughtful)* Oh, interesting find! Even that got here because the waves pushed it onto the sand. Everything on this beach has a story about how it arrived. That is three clues, Detective!"
> 3. "*(warm)* I see something interesting! It looks like the ocean carried it a long way to get here. That is our third clue. Great detective work!"
>
> **Screen**: Photo slides into slot 4. Counter shows "3 of 3 — Complete!" Confetti burst and magnifying glass sparkle animation. All four slots now filled.

> **STUCK BRANCH** (if child cannot find something):
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful, calm)* Hmm, beach clues can be tricky! Try walking to where the wet sand meets the dry sand. That line is where waves drop things. Look for anything that is not just plain sand — a shell, a stick, a smooth stone, or slimy seaweed. They like to hide right along that wet line!"
>
> **Screen**: Arrow animation points toward the tide line area in the original beach photo. Text reads "Look where wet sand meets dry sand!" with a small magnifying glass icon.

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, gathering-the-evidence tone)* All three clues are in! Look at them together, Detective. You found a [item 1], a [item 2], and a [item 3]. They all ended up right here on this beach."
>
> "*(thoughtful, mystery-solving tone)* Now here is the big question — can you tell me the whole story? How did the ocean bring all these things to the shore?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The waves pushed them!" / "The ocean carried them and dropped them on the sand!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "They were already here" / "I do not know" / offers a creative or fantastical explanation
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the collection silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(deeply impressed)* That is exactly right! The waves pick things up from the ocean floor, carry them in, and drop them on the sand when the tide goes out. Every single thing on this beach has a journey story!"
> 2. "*(warm, building)* I love that idea! Here is something cool — the waves are like delivery trucks. They pick up shells, seaweed, and stones from the ocean and push them onto the sand. The tide goes in and out, leaving presents behind every time!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, guiding)* Think about the waves. They come in and go out, right? Each time, they can carry something and leave it on the sand. That is how your clues got here — the ocean delivered them!"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos displayed in a connected timeline layout. Animated blue wave lines flow from left to right, connecting each item with a "delivery" arrow. The word "WHY?" floats above in a thought bubble, then morphs into "waves + tide = ocean delivery!" as the child answers.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, celebratory)* You are an amazing Shore Detective! You found three ocean clues and solved the mystery. Every shell, every piece of seaweed, every smooth pebble — the waves brought them all here."
>
> "*(curious, reflective)* Here is one more thing to wonder about — why do you think some things wash up near the water, and some end up farther up the beach?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Big waves push things farther!" / "The tide goes up high sometimes!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I do not know" / "Maybe someone moved them"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* You figured it out! Bigger waves push things farther up. And when the tide is high, water reaches way up the beach and leaves things there. You really think like a detective!"
> 2. "*(warm, encouraging)* That is a fun guess! The answer is the waves — sometimes they are big and strong and push things way up the sand. Sometimes they are small and only leave things near the water. The beach is always changing!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle)* Think about big waves and small waves. Big waves push things farther up the beach. Small waves leave things near the water. That is why clues end up in different spots!"
>
> **Screen**: Collection displayed as a beach scene illustration. Items are placed at different distances from an animated waterline. Dotted arrows show wave paths. Small animated connections appear between items, pulsing gently.

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, celebrating)* What an incredible Shore Detective you are! You found ocean clues, figured out where they came from, and solved the mystery of the beach."
>
> "*(warm, naming concepts naturally)* You discovered that everything on this beach has a reason — the waves CAUSED each shell and pebble and piece of seaweed to land right here. That is the power of Causation — understanding WHY things happen! And you saw how the ocean, the sand, the shells, and the seaweed are all connected — part of one big beach family. That is Connection — seeing how things belong together. You earned your Shore Detective badge!"
>
> **Screen**: A detective badge animation spins into the center, labeled "Shore Detective" with the child's collection photos as insets around it. Below the badge, the words "Causation" and "Connection" appear in stylized ocean-blue lettering, surrounded by small shell and wave icons. Gentle celebration sparkles cascade across the screen.
