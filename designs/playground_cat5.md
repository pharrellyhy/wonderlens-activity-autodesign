## Activity: The Playground Job Fair

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Playground Job Fair
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Function** (How does it work?) & **Perspective** (What are the points of view?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Community (mapping), Safety (mapping), Identity (mapping), Discovery (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking — comparing purposes), Communication Skills (expressing ideas and feelings)
- **Trigger Entity**: Playground (slide)
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs the slide at the neighborhood playground.
- **Mapping Source**: buildings_places_playground
- **IB Theme**: Who We Are (mapping: primary, weight=0.40)
- **Dimension Anchors**: appearance (physical), structure (physical), relationship (engagement)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: appearance — slide_surface_shine ("smooth and a little shiny in sunlight")

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After photographing the slide, the AI marvels at how smooth and shiny it is and wonders what JOB the slide does at the playground. The child becomes a "Playground Reporter" on a mission to find 3 more pieces of equipment, photograph each one, and figure out what special job it does — climbing, swinging, balancing, or something else. At the end, the child gives the whole playground a "team name" and reflects on how each piece has a different job but they all work together.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the vocabulary "smooth," "shiny," "bolts," "chain links," "balance." Learn that slides are smooth so you go fast, bars are at different heights for different climbers, and big bolts hold parts together like puzzle pieces.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that each piece of playground equipment has a specific **Function** — a job it does for children's bodies — and that different children may feel brave or nervous about the same equipment, showing different **Perspectives** on the same place.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice observing physical details of built structures (Research Skills — observation), compare the purposes of different equipment (Thinking Skills — critical thinking), and express personal feelings about playground features (Communication Skills — expressing ideas).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Playground Job Fair" metaphor treats each piece of equipment as an employee with a special job — the slide's job is "speed delivery," the climbing frame's job is "muscle training," the swing's job is "flying practice." This personification makes functional analysis feel like play. The twist comes when the child realizes every piece has a DIFFERENT job, but together they form a team — and different kids might pick a different "favorite coworker" depending on how brave or careful they feel.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs the slide → AI notices it is smooth and shiny → launches a "Playground Job Fair" to find 3 more pieces of equipment and name each one's job → child gives the playground team a group name and reflects on which job feels bravest.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1 (ages 4–6)]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the playground.
> **Conversation anchor**: appearance — slide_surface_shine ("smooth and a little shiny in sunlight")
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* Remember how we noticed the slide is smooth and shiny in the sun? You said it looks almost like a mirror! That got me thinking — the slide is smooth for a reason. It has a JOB. What if we found out what job EVERY piece of this playground does?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah! The slide makes you go fast!" / "What kind of jobs?"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to go on the swings!" / "Can I slide again?"
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the slide or around the playground.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Exactly — going fast is the slide's JOB! It's smooth so you zoom down. I bet every single thing here has its own special job. Want to be a Playground Reporter and find out?"
> 2. "*(enthusiastic)* The swings! I bet the swings have a really cool job too. What if we went around and figured out what EVERY piece does? Like a reporter figuring out everyone's job!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(curious, leaning in)* The slide is smooth so you can zoom down fast — that's its job! I wonder what job the other things here do. Should we go find out, like a reporter?"
>
> **Screen**: Child's slide photo centered with a soft golden glow around the shiny surface area. A small "conversation recap" sparkle effect traces the smooth surface. A speech bubble near the slide reads "My job is: Speed!" in playful letters.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start** (standalone)

> **Context**: Child photographs the slide with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted surprise)* Whoa — look at that slide! It's so smooth and shiny, like a silver river going down a hill. I bet if you touched it in the sun, it would feel warm like toast! What do you like best about this slide?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's really fast!" / "I like going down it!" / "It's so tall!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's hot!" / "My friend pushed me on it."
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or back at the slide.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Really fast — whoooosh! You know why? The slide is smooth on purpose. That's its JOB — to help you zoom! I wonder what jobs the OTHER playground things have. Want to find out?"
> 2. "*(validating warmly)* Oh, it can get warm in the sun! Metal heats up faster than plastic — the sun is like a little toaster for the slide. But you know what? Being smooth and warm is part of the slide's special job — helping you go FAST. I wonder what other jobs are hiding at this playground..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(inviting)* This slide is so smooth — feel how shiny it looks! That smoothness has a purpose. It helps you zoom down super fast! That's the slide's special JOB. Should we find out what jobs the other playground things do?"
>
> **Screen**: Child's slide photo centered with the shiny surface area highlighted by a soft glow. Tiny animated sparkle dots drift along the slide surface. A small sun icon warms the corner of the screen.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(adventurous, reporter tone)* Okay — you are now an official Playground Reporter! Your mission has three parts. Part one: find 3 more playground things and take a photo of each one. Part two: figure out what JOB each one does for kids. Part three: give the whole playground team a name! Ready, Reporter?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yeah!" / "I know what to find!"
> 2. (Uncertain) "What do you mean, a job?" / "Like what?"
> 3. (No response) Child looks around the playground.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* Reporter is ON the case! Go find a piece of playground equipment, take a photo, and tell me what it does. I'll be right here, Reporter!"
> 2. "*(explaining warmly)* A job means what it DOES for you! The slide's job is zoom — it makes you go fast. A ladder's job might be climb — it helps you go up. Every piece has a job! Go find one and take a photo, and we'll figure out its job together."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gently encouraging)* Here is a tip — look for something you can climb, swing on, or balance on. Take a photo of it and we will figure out its job together!"
>
> **Screen**: A reporter-themed mission card appears: a badge outline labeled "Playground Reporter" with a small notepad icon. Below it, 4 slots in a row — the first filled with the slide photo labeled "Job: Speed!" and 3 empty slots with question marks. A numbered task list with small icons (camera, magnifying glass, trophy) shows the three mission parts.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — First Find:**
>
> *(Child photographs something — e.g., the climbing frame or monkey bars)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(eager reporter voice)* Reporter report! Oh, what is that? I see bars at different heights — some low, some high! What job do you think this one does?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "You climb on it!" / "It helps you go up!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't like that one." / "It's scary."
> 3. (No response) Just takes the photo.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Climbing — YES! That's its job! Those bars are at different heights so smaller kids and bigger kids can all practice. It helps your arms get strong and teaches you to balance. Reporter Log: 'Climbing Frame — Job: Muscle Builder!' Two more to find, Reporter!"
> 2. "*(warm and understanding)* A little scary? That makes sense — it's tall! Some kids feel brave on it and some feel a little nervous. That's totally okay. Its job is still to help kids practice climbing and holding on tight. Different kids feel different ways about the same thing! Reporter Log: 'Climbing Frame — Job: Brave Builder!' Two more, Reporter!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(curious)* Look at all those bars — some low, some high! I think this one's job is climbing. It helps your body practice holding on tight and going up. What do YOU think — is climbing its job, or something else?"
>
> **Screen**: New photo slides into the second slot. A brief sparkle animation. The slot label updates to show the job name. Counter reads "2 of 4." A small height chart icon appears next to the bars photo showing "bars at different heights."

> **Round 2 — Second Find:**
>
> *(Child photographs something — e.g., the swings)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(playful)* Report number two! Ooh, I can see chain links — those tiny metal loops that connect together. What job does THIS one do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Swinging!" / "It makes you fly!" / "You go back and forth!"
> 2. (Stretch) "The chains are cold." / "My mom pushes me."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* Flying practice — that's the perfect job name! The chain links bend so you can swing back and forth. A stiff pole would not work — the chain bends and lets you soar! Reporter Log: 'Swings — Job: Flying Practice!' One more to find, Reporter!"
> 2. "*(warmly connecting)* Cold chains! Yep — metal can feel chilly. And your mom pushes you because the swing's job is to go back and forth, like flying! Those chains are made of tiny loops so they can bend. Reporter Log: 'Swings — Job: Flying Practice!' One more, Reporter!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enthusiastic)* I see chains made of tiny loops, connected like a necklace! Those chains let the seat swing back and forth — whoooosh! I think this one's job is flying practice. Does that sound right to you?"
>
> **Screen**: Third slot fills. Counter reads "3 of 4." A small chain-link animation briefly shows links connecting. The progress trail shows the reporter almost at the finish.

> **Round 3 — Third Find (with stuck branch):**
>
> **STUCK BRANCH** *(if child has been searching for more than a minute without finding something)*:
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful whisper)* Reporter tip! Look for something you can sit on, balance on, or spin. Maybe a seesaw, a bench, a balance beam, or even a sandbox! Those all have jobs too."
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I see the seesaw!" / Child spots something and moves toward it.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I already did everything." / "There's nothing else."
> 3. (No response) Child keeps looking around.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Great spotting! Go take a photo, Reporter — let's find out its job!"
> 2. "*(reassuring)* Look around one more time — even a bench has a job! Its job is resting. Or the fence — its job is keeping everyone safe. Anything at the playground counts!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* How about that over there — do you see anything with bolts holding it together? Big bolts mean it's strong and important. Even the ground has a job — it's soft and bouncy to keep you safe when you land!"
>
> *(Child photographs final find — e.g., a seesaw, sandbox, or bench)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(triumphant)* FINAL REPORT! What did you find, and what is its job?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A seesaw! You go up and down with a friend!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I took a picture of the ground."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* A seesaw — and you need a FRIEND to make it work! Its job is teamwork practice. One goes up, one goes down. You have to work together! Reporter Log: 'Seesaw — Job: Teamwork Trainer!' Collection COMPLETE, Reporter!"
> 2. "*(delighted)* The ground! That is a brilliant find. Feel how soft and bouncy it is? The ground's job is SAFETY — it cushions your landing so jumping doesn't hurt. Reporter Log: 'Ground — Job: Safe Landing Pad!' Collection COMPLETE, Reporter!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enthusiastic)* Let me look at your photo! I can see something interesting. Every playground thing has a job — this one's job might be resting, or balancing, or protecting. What do YOU think its job is?"
>
> **Screen**: Final slot fills with a celebration animation — all 4 photos light up with golden borders. A small fireworks burst. The reporter trail reaches the finish flag. Badge starts assembling.

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud and excited)* Playground Reporter, look at your collection! A slide for speed, a [first find] for [its job], a [second find] for [its job], and a [third find] for [its job]. Four different things — four different JOBS! Now the big question: what team name would you give this playground? If all these things worked together like a team, what would you call them?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The Super Playground Team!" / "The Fun Helpers!" / Any creative team name.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know a name." / "The playground."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebrating)* [Child's team name] — I LOVE that! Every member has a different job but they all work together to help kids play, move, and grow strong. Which team member is YOUR favorite? The one that feels the most fun to you?"
> 2. "*(playfully helping)* How about this — they all help your body do something. Speed, climbing, flying, [third job]. They could be 'The Body Helpers' or 'The Fun Crew!' Pick one, or make up your own!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gently)* What if we called them by what they do? They help kids zoom, climb, swing, and [verb]. I think they could be 'The Adventure Crew!' Or would you pick a different name?"
>
> **Screen**: All 4 photos displayed in a grid with their job labels underneath (Speed, Muscle Builder, Flying Practice, [third job]). A large blank banner at the top reads "Team Name: ___" waiting for the child's answer. When the child names the team, the banner fills in with the name in bold playful letters. Animated connection lines link all four photos to the banner, showing they are one team.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(amazed and warm)* Reporter, you discovered something really cool. Every single piece of this playground has a DIFFERENT job — but not everyone feels the same way about each one! Some kids love the tall climbing frame because it feels brave. Other kids love the swings because flying feels free. Which one did YOU like best, and why?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The swings! Because I like flying!" / "The climbing one because I'm strong!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "All of them!" / "None, I like the sandbox."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* That tells me something special about YOU! You picked [choice] because of how it makes you FEEL. Another kid might pick a totally different one — and that's what's cool. The same playground feels different to every kid who plays here!"
> 2. "*(warmly)* All of them — you love the whole team! That makes you an all-around playground fan. And you know what? If your friend was here, they might pick a different favorite. Everyone sees the playground a little differently!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(sharing gently)* I bet you have a favorite — the one that makes your tummy feel excited. Is it the fast one? The tall one? The flying one? Every kid picks differently, and that's what makes a playground so special!"
>
> **Screen**: The four photos are arranged around a central "star" icon with the team name above. Each photo has a small emoji-style face below it showing different feelings (brave face, happy face, excited face, calm face). Gentle animated sparkles connect the photos to the center.

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* Congratulations, Playground Reporter! You did something amazing today. You discovered the **Function** of every piece — what job it does, from zooming to climbing to flying to [third job]. And you found out something even bigger — every kid has a different **Perspective** on the same playground. One kid feels brave on the climbing frame. Another feels free on the swings. Same playground, different feelings! You earned your Playground Reporter Badge!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) Cheers, talks about wanting to visit other playgrounds, or asks about the badge.
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or says nothing.
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Next time you visit ANY playground, try being a reporter again — every playground has different equipment with different jobs! See you on the next report, Reporter!"
> 2. "*(warm)* Your badge is saved! Whenever you see playground equipment, think about its job. Bye for now, Reporter!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft)* Your Reporter Badge is glowing. Great job today, Reporter!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden "Playground Reporter Badge" appears — shaped like a notepad, with the slide photo at the center and the 3 other collection photos as small insets around the edges. The words **"Function"** and **"Perspective"** float up artistically — "Function" styled with bold, mechanical-looking letters and small gear icons, "Perspective" styled with letters in different colors viewed from slightly different angles. A soft chime plays. The team name banner glows softly above the badge.
