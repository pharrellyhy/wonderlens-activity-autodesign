## Activity: The Banana Taste Test Show (香蕉品鉴会)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Banana Taste Test Show (香蕉品鉴会)
- **Activity Category**: Category 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device, Indoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: Perspective, Responsibility
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Nutrition (mapping), Wellbeing (mapping), Preferences (designed), Discovery (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Thinking Skills (critical thinking, creative thinking), Communication Skills (expressing opinions, listening to others' views), Self-Management Skills (making healthy choices)
- **Trigger Entity**: Banana
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a banana on the kitchen table
- **Mapping Source**: food_banana
- **IB Theme**: Who We Are (mapping: primary, weight=0.39)
- **Dimension Anchors**: emotions (engagement), relationship (engagement), senses (physical)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: emotions → preferences about bananas; senses → how it feels and tastes

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: The child becomes a "Snack Show Host" who runs a pretend taste-test show about bananas. The AI plays the role of different imaginary friends who each have very different opinions about bananas — one loves them mushy, one only likes them green, one thinks the stringy bits are the best part. The child must listen to each friend's perspective, decide if they agree or disagree, and explain why. At the end, the child creates a "Banana Recommendation" for their show.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: (1) Bananas get softer and squishier as they ripen, (2) the peel feels waxy and a bit slippery, (3) thin stringy bits cling when you peel, (4) a banana gives your body energy for running and playing, (5) "preference" means what you like best
  - **U (Understand)**: (1) Different people can have different feelings about the same food, and all preferences are valid (Perspective), (2) Choosing what and how much to eat is a way of taking care of yourself (Responsibility)
  - **D (Do)**: (1) Express and justify personal opinions about sensory experiences (critical thinking), (2) Consider viewpoints different from their own (perspective-taking), (3) Make a recommendation based on their own experience (decision-making)

- **③ Design Highlight**: Instead of learning ABOUT the banana, the child runs a show where opinions CLASH. Each imaginary friend has a strong, funny take on bananas — grounded in real sensory attributes from the mapping. The child isn't quizzed; they're the authority. They host, judge, and deliver the final verdict. The humor comes from how seriously the imaginary friends take their banana opinions.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: A child in the kitchen photographs a banana and becomes the host of a pretend show where imaginary friends share wildly different banana opinions, and the child decides who they agree with.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

---

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the banana.
> **Conversation anchor**: emotions — "Do you like bananas, or do you not like them? Why?" / senses — "gets softer and squishier as it ripens"

> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier tone)* You know what I loved? When you told me how you feel about bananas — whether you like them mushy or not mushy. That got me thinking… I bet not EVERYONE agrees with you! What if we found out? I have some friends who have VERY strong banana opinions…"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "What do they think?" / "Who are they?" / "I want to hear!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I already told you what I think!" / "Bananas are just bananas."
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet or looking around.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited, conspiratorial tone)* Oh, you're going to love this. They get SO worked up about bananas. But they need someone to run the show — like a host. Want to be the host?"
> 2. "*(playful, validating tone)* You did, and your opinion was great! But my friends disagree with each other SO much, they need someone smart — like you — to sort it out. You in?"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(gentle, enticing tone)* One of my friends thinks the stringy bits on a banana are the BEST part. Can you believe that? Want to hear more?"

> **Screen**: The child's banana photo is centered with a soft golden glow. Three small, colorful silhouette icons (representing the imaginary friends) peek in from the edges of the screen, each with a thought bubble containing a tiny banana. A "conversation recap" shimmer highlights the banana's texture.

---

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start** (standalone)

> **Context**: Child photographs the banana with no prior conversation.

> **AI says**: "*(delighted, curious tone)* Ooh, a banana! Look at it sitting there all yellow and curvy. You know what's funny? Not everyone feels the same way about bananas. Some people LOVE them, some people think they're too squishy. What about you — do you like bananas?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes, I love them!" / "They're okay." / "I don't like the mushy ones!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to eat it!" / "It's yellow." / "I like apples better."
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, interested tone)* Ooh, interesting! I have some friends who feel VERY differently about bananas. They argue about it all the time!"
> 2. "*(playful, validating tone)* That's a great answer! You know what, I have some friends who have really strong opinions about bananas — even stronger than yours!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(conspiratorial tone)* Well, I have a friend who thinks the stringy bits on bananas are the BEST part. Can you imagine? Want to hear what else they think?"

> **Screen**: The child's banana photo is centered on screen with a warm ambient glow. Small animated question marks float around it. Three colorful silhouette icons peek in from the edges — curious faces with thought bubbles.

---

**Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo — "The Snack Show Host"**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, showbiz tone)* Okay, here's the deal. You are now the HOST of The Banana Show! I'm going to introduce you to three friends. Each one has a totally different opinion about bananas. Your job is to listen, and then tell us — do you AGREE or DISAGREE? And why! Here, I'll show you. My first friend is Momo. Momo says: 'I only like bananas when they have lots of brown spots — the spottier the better, because they're SO sweet and soft!' Host, do you agree or disagree with Momo?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I agree, spotty ones are sweet!" / "I disagree, I like yellow ones!" / "Eww, brown spots!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Who is Momo?" / "I don't know." / "What's a host?"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed tone)* Great hosting! You {agreed/disagreed} AND told us why — that's exactly what a show host does! Did you know that bananas get sweeter when those spots show up? That's why Momo likes them. Ready for the next friend?"
> 2. "*(warm, explaining tone)* Momo is a pretend friend who loves bananas. And a host is the person who runs the show — like you! All you have to do is say if you agree or disagree with what they think. Want to try?"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(encouraging tone)* Momo really loves the spotty, soft bananas. Do you think that sounds yummy, or not so yummy? Either answer is great!"

> **Screen**: A colorful "BANANA SHOW" banner appears at the top. A cheerful character icon labeled "Momo" appears on the left with a speech bubble showing a spotted banana with heart eyes. On the right, two large buttons glow softly: a green thumbs-up ("AGREE") and an orange thumbs-down ("DISAGREE"). A small "HOST" badge sits at the bottom with the child's name placeholder.

---

**Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction (3–5 rounds)**

**Round 1 — Guest: "Pip" (senses focus)**
*Target: Sensory opinion. Build confidence with a concrete, relatable preference.*

> **AI says**: "*(introducing a new voice)* Next up is Pip! Pip says: 'I love peeling bananas. The peel feels all waxy and slippery, and it makes a fun rip sound when you pull it open! That's the best part — even better than eating it!' Host, what do you think — agree or disagree?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I disagree, eating is the best part!" / "I agree, peeling is fun!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I peel it a different way." / "My mom peels it for me." / "Rip!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted tone)* Ha! You {agreed/disagreed} — and you're right, {child's reasoning}! Pip really does love that waxy, slippery peel. You know, banana peels feel so slippery because they have a special waxy coating. Does that sound cool or weird?"
> 2. "*(warm, curious tone)* Oh interesting! Everyone peels their banana differently. But what about Pip's idea — is peeling really MORE fun than eating? What does the host think?"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(playful prompt)* Pip thinks the rrrip sound when you peel is the best thing ever! Better than eating it! Does that sound right to you, or kind of silly?"

> **Screen**: "Pip" character icon appears — a bouncy, energetic character. Speech bubble shows an illustrated hand peeling a banana with "RRIIPP!" sound effect text. The AGREE/DISAGREE buttons pulse gently. When the child responds, a small scoreboard appears: "Host's Verdict: AGREE/DISAGREE" with a checkmark animation.

---

**Round 2 — Guest: "Sunny" (emotions + relationship focus)**
*Target: Comparative opinion. Slightly more complex — requires relating banana to other things.*

> **AI says**: "*(thoughtful, introducing tone)* Our third guest is Sunny. Sunny says: 'A banana is like a smile! It's curved just right. And when I eat one before I go play, I feel ready for anything — like my body has all the energy it needs. A banana is the BEST snack for playing!' Host, agree or disagree?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I agree, it gives you energy!" / "I disagree, apples are better!" / "It IS like a smile!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I eat cookies before playing." / "I don't like playing." / "It looks like a moon too!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(enthusiastic tone)* What a great call, Host! You know, Sunny is right that bananas give your body energy for running and playing. That's why some people grab a banana before they go outside! Do you eat a snack before you play?"
> 2. "*(validating, warm tone)* Ooh, everyone has their own favorite snack — that's what makes this show fun! Sunny thinks a banana beats everything because it gives you quick energy. What do you think is the best snack for playing? Is it a banana, or something else?"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Sunny thinks eating a banana makes you ready to run and jump. Does that sound right? Or do you feel ready to play from a different snack?"

> **Screen**: "Sunny" character icon appears — a warm, bright character holding a banana like a trophy. Speech bubble shows a curved banana with a smiley face and a running child icon. Small energy bolt icons animate around the banana. The scoreboard updates with the running tally of AGREE/DISAGREE verdicts.

---

**Round 3 — Guest: "Roo" (perspective challenge)**
*Target: Confronting a surprising viewpoint. Peak engagement — funny and unexpected.*

> **AI says**: "*(dramatic, announcer tone)* And now… our most CONTROVERSIAL guest… ROO! Roo says: 'I don't like bananas AT ALL. They get too mushy, the stringy bits stick to everything, and they smell too sweet. I think bananas are OVERRATED!' Whoa! Host, what do you say to THAT?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I disagree, bananas are great!" / "I kind of agree, mushy is yucky." / "The strings ARE annoying!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Roo is mean!" / "I agree with everything!" / Child laughs.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed, fair tone)* Wow, the host has spoken! You know what's really cool? You and Roo don't have to agree. Some people think mushy bananas are the sweetest, and some people don't like the texture. Both are okay! That's what makes people interesting — everyone feels different about the same thing."
> 2. "*(warm, normalizing tone)* You know, it's totally fine to agree with Roo! Not everyone likes the same foods. That's actually the whole point of this show — finding out that people have different feelings about the same banana. And every feeling is okay!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(playful, inviting tone)* Roo really doesn't like mushy bananas or those thin stringy bits! Some people feel that way. What about you — do the stringy bits bother you, or do you not mind them?"

> **Screen**: "Roo" character icon appears — a skeptical character with arms crossed. Speech bubble shows a banana with X marks over "mushy" texture, stringy bits, and smell waves. Dramatic spotlights animate. When the child gives their verdict, the scoreboard updates and a "CONTROVERSIAL!" stamp animates if the child agrees with Roo.

---

**Round 4 (if child is engaged) — The Host's Own Opinion**
*Target: Open-ended. Child synthesizes their own perspective.*

> **AI says**: "*(warm, turning to the child)* Okay, Host, you've heard from everyone — Momo loves spotty sweet bananas, Pip loves peeling them, Sunny says they're the best energy snack, and Roo thinks they're overrated! Now it's YOUR turn. If you could tell everyone ONE thing about bananas — your BEST banana opinion — what would it be?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I think bananas are yummy but not when they're brown!" / "Peeling is the best part!" / "They're good for you!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I like all food!" / "I want to eat this one now!" / Child says something unrelated.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebratory tone)* '{child's answer}' — what a perfect host opinion! That's YOUR perspective, and nobody else has exactly the same one. That's what makes you a great host!"
> 2. "*(warm, delighted tone)* I love it! That's your very own banana opinion, and it's just as important as anyone else's. Every person's perspective matters!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(gentle, encouraging tone)* You know, even not saying anything is an opinion! Maybe you're still thinking about it. That's okay — a good host takes their time."

> **Screen**: The spotlight turns to the "HOST" position. A microphone icon glows in the center. When the child speaks, their words appear in a large, decorated speech bubble — the biggest one on screen. A "HOST'S PICK" golden frame appears around their opinion. All four guest icons watch from the sides.

---

**Step 4: Celebration + Role Title**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, grand tone)* And that's a wrap on The Banana Show! You were an AMAZING Snack Show Host! You listened to every friend's opinion — even when it was really different from yours — and you shared your own. That takes a lot of thinking and a lot of courage!"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child cheers, claps, or says "I'm a Snack Show Host!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can we do apples next?" / "That was funny!"
> 3. (No response) Child watches the screen.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming tone)* The best Snack Show Host ever! Your friends were lucky to have you listen so carefully."
> 2. "*(enthusiastic tone)* Wasn't it? You made it so fun! And yes, every food could have its own show. You're officially a Snack Show Host!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(warm tone)* Look at that — Snack Show Host! The audience loved you."

> **Screen**: A large animated badge fills the center: "SNACK SHOW HOST" in bold, playful lettering with a microphone and banana icon. Confetti bursts in the background. The four guest character icons applaud around the edges. A cheerful fanfare sound plays. The child's banana photo appears as a small inset on the badge.

---

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective tone)* You know what was really special? Every friend had a different feeling about the same banana — Momo loved the spots, Pip loved the peel, Sunny loved the energy, and Roo didn't like it at all! And every single opinion was okay. That's the magic of Perspective — seeing that people can feel differently about the same thing. And when you chose your own opinion about what's good to eat, that's Responsibility — making your own smart choices about food. You're not just a Snack Show Host — you're a thinker!"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah, everyone is different!" / "I liked the show!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What's perspective?" / "I want to play again!"
> 3. (No response) Child looks at screen.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm closing tone)* It really is! Next time you eat anything, you can think about how someone else might feel about it. See you next time, Snack Show Host!"
> 2. "*(encouraging tone)* Perspective means seeing how other people think and feel — and you did that perfectly today! See you next time, Host!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(gentle closing tone)* Everyone has their own perspective — their own way of seeing things. And you explored that beautifully today. Bye, Snack Show Host!"

> **Screen**: The words "Perspective" and "Responsibility" appear in large, colorful, hand-drawn-style lettering. "Perspective" is accompanied by four small character icons each with a different expression looking at the same banana. "Responsibility" is accompanied by a child icon making a thoughtful choice with a small heart. The Snack Show Host badge sits below. After 3 seconds, "See you next time, Snack Show Host!" fades in with a gentle wave animation.
