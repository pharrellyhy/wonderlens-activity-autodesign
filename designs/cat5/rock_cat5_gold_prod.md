### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After photographing a rock, the AI marvels at how hard and tough it is — and reveals the rock's "superpower": STRENGTH. The child becomes a Nature Inventor and goes on a mission to collect 3 more natural items, each of which the AI assigns a unique superpower based on its material properties (e.g., leaf = flexibility, feather = lightness, stick = length). At synthesis, the child picks two superpowers to combine and invents something that has never existed before — naming it, describing what it does, and hearing the AI bring the invention to life in vivid words.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the vocabulary "strength," "flexibility," "lightness," "property," and "material." Learn that different natural objects have different physical properties — rocks are hard and strong, leaves can bend without breaking, feathers are light enough to float, sticks are long and reach far.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that every material has a distinct Form — specific physical properties like hardness, bendability, or weight — and that those properties determine Function — what the material can do or be used for. When you combine properties from different materials, you can imagine entirely new functions.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice close observation of material properties through touch and sight, combine distinct properties into novel inventions, and describe imagined inventions with reasoning about what they can do.

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Nature Inventor's Lab" frames the outdoors as a laboratory and every natural object as an ingredient with a hidden superpower. The key mechanism is superpower discovery + creative combination: instead of simply collecting items, the AI reveals each find's material superpower (strength, flexibility, lightness, length), turning ordinary nature objects into extraordinary building blocks. At synthesis, the child becomes the inventor — choosing two superpowers to fuse into something impossible but wonderful. The invention is the child's own creation, and the AI's elaborate description makes it feel real.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a rock in the park → AI reveals the rock's superpower of STRENGTH → child becomes a Nature Inventor → collects 3 more items (leaf, feather, stick) → each gets a superpower (flexibility, lightness, length) → child picks two superpowers to combine → invents and names something new → AI brings the invention to life with vivid description → child earns Nature Inventor Badge.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1 (ages 4-6)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (amazed, hefting-weight) "Whoa — look at this rock! Feel how HARD it is. It's like a tiny mountain you can hold in your hand! I bet if you squeezed it as hard as you could, it wouldn't even budge. Have you ever tried to break a rock?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "No, it's too hard!" / "I tried once, I couldn't!"
2. (Unexpected) "I threw it in the water!" / "It's just a rock." / "I like shiny ones!"
3. (No response) Child looks at the rock or the screen silently.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (impressed) "It IS super hard! You know what? This rock has a secret SUPERPOWER. Want to know what it is? Its superpower is... STRENGTH! It's tough, solid, and nothing can squish it. This rock is the strongest thing around here!"
2. (playful) "Ooh, shiny rocks are the best! And you know what makes ALL rocks amazing? They're SO hard and SO tough. This one has a secret superpower — want to hear it? Its superpower is... STRENGTH! Nothing can squish this rock!"
3. (wait 2s) "Can I tell you something cool about this rock? Feel how hard it is — go on, give it a squeeze. It won't budge! That's because this rock has a superpower. Its superpower is... STRENGTH!"

**Screen:** Rock photo centered with bold "STRENGTH" pulsing in block letters, animated cracks radiating outward, and a small shield icon.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (scientist-explorer) "Okay, here's the thing — EVERY natural thing out here has a hidden superpower. Rocks have strength. But what about leaves? Feathers? Sticks? You are now a Nature Inventor! Your mission: find 3 more things in this park. I'll tell you each one's superpower. Then — here's the best part — you'll COMBINE two superpowers to invent something totally new! Ready, Inventor?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yes! Let's go!" / "I want to find a leaf!"
2. (Uncertain) "What do I look for?" / "What's a superpower?"
3. (No response) Child glances around the park.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (cheering) "The lab is OPEN! Go find something from nature — a leaf, a feather, a stick, a flower petal, anything! Take a photo and I'll reveal its superpower. Go, Inventor!"
2. (encouraging) "A superpower is the special thing each object can DO because of what it's made of! Like your rock is super strong because it's so hard. Go find something else from nature — a leaf, a stick, anything! Take a photo and I'll show you its superpower!"
3. (wait 2s) "Try looking near the ground — there are leaves, sticks, feathers, and all kinds of things hiding in the grass! Pick up something interesting, take a photo, and I'll tell you its secret superpower!"

**Screen:** Lab-themed mission card with "Nature Inventor" badge, 4 ingredient slots (first filled with rock photo labeled "STRENGTH"), 3 glowing question marks, and a 3-step task list: Collect, Discover, Invent.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1 — First Find (e.g., a leaf):**

**AI says:** (excited scientist) "Ooh, ingredient number two! Let me analyze this... I see it's thin, and look — you can bend it without it snapping! Try bending it gently. Does it bend?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes! It bends a lot!" / "It's bendy!" / "I can fold it!"
2. (Unexpected) "It's green." / "It fell from a tree." / "I don't know."
3. (No response) Child looks at the leaf or the screen.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (triumphant) "YES! This leaf's superpower is... FLEXIBILITY! It can bend and twist and curve without breaking! Imagine if YOU could bend like that — you'd be like a rubber band! FLEXIBILITY is locked in. Two more ingredients to go, Inventor!"
2. (curious, redirecting) "It IS green — and look how thin it is! Give it a little bend — does it curl? Does it snap, or does it stay in one piece? I think this leaf can bend without breaking — its superpower is FLEXIBILITY! Two more to find!"
3. (wait 2s) "Let me check this one out. See how thin it is? I bet it can bend and twist without snapping. You know what that means? Its superpower is... FLEXIBILITY! It can bend any way it wants! Two more ingredients, Inventor!"

**Screen:** Leaf photo slides into second slot with green sparkles. "FLEXIBILITY" in curvy, wiggling letters. Counter updates to "2/4."

**Round 2 — Second Find (e.g., a feather):** AI asks child to hold it up and let go gently, notices how slowly it falls. Reveals superpower: LIGHTNESS — "it weighs almost nothing, it can drift through the air like a cloud!" Counter updates to 3/4.

**Round 3 — Third Find (e.g., a stick), with stuck branch:** AI provides a concrete stuck hint: "Look at the base of a tree — there are usually sticks, seed pods, or bark pieces." When child photographs the stick, AI asks what they can reach with it that they can't reach with their hand. Reveals superpower: LENGTH — "it reaches farther than anything else in your collection!" All four slots fill and "INVENTION TIME!" banner appears.

#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (grand, dramatic inventor) "Inventor — your lab is LOADED! Look at your four superpowers: the STRENGTH of your rock, the FLEXIBILITY of your leaf, the LIGHTNESS of your feather, and the LENGTH of your stick! Now comes the BEST part. Pick any TWO superpowers — and imagine: if you combined them into ONE thing, what amazing invention would you create? What would you build with... say... the STRENGTH of rock and the FLEXIBILITY of leaf?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "A bendy shield!" / "A rubber wall!" / "Stretchy armor!"
2. (Unexpected) "I don't know." / "A rock-leaf." / "Can I pick different ones?"

**AI follow-up:**

1. (mind-blown) "A BENDY SHIELD! Imagine this — a shield that's as STRONG as a rock, so nothing can break through it — but it can BEND and CURVE around corners because it has the flexibility of a leaf! You could wrap it around yourself like a blanket, but it's as tough as stone! The Bendy Shield — invented by YOU, Nature Inventor! What would you use it for?" Then: "That is BRILLIANT! No one in the whole world has ever invented a Bendy Shield before. Only YOU!"
2. (encouraging) "That's okay — let me give you a spark! What if something was as STRONG as your rock... but could BEND like your leaf? What would that be? A bendy wall? A curvy sword? A twisty shield? What do YOU think it could be?" If child asks for different ones: "YES! Pick any two you want! How about LIGHTNESS and LENGTH — something super light but super long?"

**AI says:** (proud, reflecting) "Nature Inventor, look at what you did today! You started with one rock — just a rock! — and you discovered that it has the superpower of STRENGTH. Then you found a leaf with FLEXIBILITY, a feather with LIGHTNESS, and a stick with LENGTH. Every single thing out here has its own special power! Why do you think different things in nature have different superpowers?"

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted) "That's it EXACTLY! Rocks are hard so they can last a long time and protect things. Leaves are bendy so the wind doesn't snap them off trees. Feathers are light so birds can fly! Every superpower helps that thing do its JOB in nature."
2. (warmly) "Nature IS super cool! Here's a secret — each superpower is like a tool. Rocks are strong so they can protect things. Feathers are light so birds can fly! Everything is made just right for what it needs to do."

**Screen:** Four ingredient photos in a row with glowing superpower labels. Invention Workshop area below with "+" symbol; chosen superpowers merge with energy arcs and sparkles, invention name appears in bold golden letters with "Created by [child]" stamp.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (warm celebration) "Congratulations, Nature Inventor! You did something incredible today. You explored the **Form** of things in nature — what they feel like, how hard or soft or light they are. And you discovered their **Function** — the special job each superpower lets them do! Then you went even further — you COMBINED superpowers to invent something totally new. That's the magic of being an inventor — seeing what things CAN DO, and imagining what they COULD DO! You earned your Nature Inventor Badge!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Engaged) Cheers, talks about their invention, or wants to invent more.
2. (Quiet) Smiles or says nothing.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (encouraging) "Next time you pick up ANYTHING — a pinecone, a pebble, a flower — ask yourself: what's YOUR superpower? And what could I invent if I combined it with something else? See you in the lab, Inventor!"
2. (warm) "Your badge is saved! Remember, Inventor — everything around you has a hidden superpower. You just have to look! Bye for now!"

**Screen:** Golden "Nature Inventor Badge" with beaker-and-leaf silhouette, 4 collection photos with superpower labels, invention name in golden banner. "Form" in textured stone/leaf/feather/wood letters, "Function" in bold letters with lightning accents, light bulb icon between them.
