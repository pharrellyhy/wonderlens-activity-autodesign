## Activity: The Shimmer Spotter Safari (闪光侦察之旅)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Shimmer Spotter Safari (闪光侦察之旅)
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Form**, **Connection**
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Habitat (mapping), Adaptation (mapping), Discovery (designed), Pattern (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking, transfer), Communication Skills (expressing ideas)
- **Game Style**: comparison_chart
- **Trigger Entity**: Goldfish
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a goldfish in a park pond or aquarium tank and goes on a hunt for other water creatures and shimmery things nearby
- **Mapping Source**: animals_goldfish
- **IB Theme**: How the World Works (mapping: primary, weight=0.36)
- **Dimension Anchors**: appearance (physical), structure (physical), relationship (engagement)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: appearance — fin_shape, tail_style; structure — scales

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: The child spots a goldfish in a pond or aquarium tank and becomes a "Shimmer Spotter" on a mission to find other water creatures and shimmery things nearby. They photograph 3 finds, compare each one to the goldfish — does it have fins, scales, or a shiny surface? — and sort their collection into a simple chart of "like the goldfish" and "different from the goldfish."

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Goldfish have floaty fins, fan-shaped tails, and tiny overlapping scales; other water creatures move and look different from goldfish; shiny or shimmery surfaces appear on many things in nature and around ponds; scales protect a fish like a jacket protects you
  - **U (Understand)**: Living things that share a habitat can look very different from each other — their Form tells us how they survive (Form); objects that seem unrelated can share surprising features like shimmer, shape, or movement (Connection)
  - **D (Do)**: Observe and describe visible features of water creatures and shimmery objects (Research — observation); compare and sort finds by shared and different properties (Thinking — critical thinking); explain why a find is similar to or different from the goldfish (Communication — expressing ideas)

- **③ Design Highlight**: The collection criterion is "water creatures and shimmery things" — a two-pronged hunt that lets the child find both living things (frogs, ducks, bugs on water) and non-living shimmery objects (wet rocks, reflections, water surface itself). The comparison chart asks the child to sort each find: "Does it have fins? Scales? Shimmer?" — turning physical observation into a simple classification exercise grounded in the goldfish's real body features.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: A child at a park pond or aquarium photographs a goldfish, then hunts for 3 more water creatures or shimmery things nearby, comparing each find's features to the goldfish's fins, scales, and shimmer.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the goldfish.
> **Conversation anchor**: appearance — tail_style ("fan-shaped tail that swishes side to side")
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* You noticed the goldfish's tail fans out like a little flag! It swishes side to side to swim. I bet there are more shimmery things near this water. Want to go look?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah! Let's find shiny things!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to watch the fish more."
> 3. (No response) Child gazes at the water quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Great! Your eyes are super sharp. Let's see what shimmery things are hiding nearby!"
> 2. "*(warm, validating)* The goldfish is fun to watch! But first, quick adventure — let's find things that shimmer like that fish. Then we can come back!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, encouraging)* See how the goldfish glows in the water? What if we found more glowing, shimmery things out here? Want to try?"
>
> **Screen**: Goldfish photo centered with a soft golden glow highlighting the fan-shaped tail. A small "conversation recap" badge shows a magnifying glass icon over the tail detail.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start** (standalone)

> **Context**: Child photographs a goldfish in a park pond or aquarium tank with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted wonder)* Wow, a goldfish! Look at those shiny scales. It sparkles like a tiny treasure in the water. What do you like best about it?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's so shiny!" / "I like its tail!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's orange!" / "Can I touch it?"
> 3. (No response) Child watches the goldfish swim.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* So shiny, like little mirrors on its body! This goldfish is a real shimmer star. I wonder what else around here shimmers like that."
> 2. "*(warm, playful)* It IS orange — bright orange with sparkly scales! It looks like a little jewel swimming around. I bet more shimmery things are hiding near this water."
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(playful, coaxing)* Look at it glide! Those fins wave like tiny flags. This goldfish is full of shimmer. Want to find more shimmery things nearby?"
>
> **Screen**: Goldfish photo centered with a soft aqua-blue glow. Tiny sparkle animations float around the fish to highlight its shiny scales.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing — Shimmer Spotter**

> **AI says**: "*(adventurous, rallying)* You are now a Shimmer Spotter! Your mission has three parts. First, find three water creatures or shimmery things nearby. Second, take a photo of each one. Third, we will sort them — like the goldfish or different. Ready, spotter?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Let's go!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What is a shimmery thing?"
> 3. (No response) Child looks around uncertainly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(pumped up)* Let's go! Look near the water for bugs, birds, shiny rocks, or anything that glows. Take a photo when you spot one!"
> 2. "*(warm, explaining)* Shimmery means shiny and sparkly — like the goldfish's scales! Look for wet rocks, water sparkles, or shiny bugs. Anything that catches your eye!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging, pointing)* Try looking at the water's edge first. See any bugs or shiny rocks? Take a photo of whatever you spot!"
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears with a "Shimmer Spotter" badge at the top — a magnifying glass with sparkles. Four collection slots are shown in a row — the first slot is filled with the goldfish photo. Three empty slots glow softly, each marked with a question mark. A numbered checklist reads: 1) Find 3 shimmery things. 2) Photograph each one. 3) Sort: like the goldfish or different?

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

> **Round 1** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a shiny wet rock near the water)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited discovery)* Ooh, a shiny rock! It gleams near the water. Does this rock have scales like the goldfish, or is it smooth?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's smooth!" / "No scales, just shiny."
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's just a rock." / "I don't know."
> 3. (No response) Child waits silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* Smooth and shiny — no scales, but it shimmers just like the goldfish! Great eye, spotter. Two more finds to go!"
> 2. "*(warm, extending)* A rock is a great find! It is shiny like the goldfish but has no fins or tail. Same shimmer, different shape! Two more to go!"
> 3. "*(playful)* I see something shiny and wet! This rock gleams like the goldfish's scales, but it cannot swim. Nice spotting! Two more to find!"
>
> **Screen**: Rock photo slides into second collection slot with sparkle animation; counter shows "1 of 3 found."
>
> **Round 2** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a duck or small water bug on the pond)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(curious excitement)* Oh wow, look at that! Does this creature live in the water like the goldfish? Does it have fins?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It has feathers, not fins!" / "It swims but no scales."
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a duck!" / "Quack quack!"
> 3. (No response) Child watches the creature.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Feathers, not fins! But it still swims. So it lives in water like the goldfish but looks totally different. Amazing comparison, spotter! One more to go!"
> 2. "*(warm, playful)* A duck — great find! Ducks paddle with webbed feet, not fins like a goldfish. Same water, different style! One more treasure to find!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* This one lives near water too! But look — no scales, no fins like the goldfish. It moves a different way. Cool find! One more to go!"
>
> **Screen**: New photo slides into third collection slot with a little splash animation; counter shows "2 of 3 found."
>
> **Round 3** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a dragonfly, water reflection, sparkly leaf, or another shimmery item)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(thrilled)* You found another one! Does this shimmer like the goldfish? What makes it sparkly?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's wet so it's shiny!" / "Its wings shimmer!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's pretty." / Child names the object but doesn't compare.
> 3. (No response) Child waits quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(awed)* Wet things shimmer — just like the goldfish in the water! You spotted the pattern. Your collection is complete, spotter!"
> 2. "*(warm, validating)* It IS pretty! And look — it catches the light, kind of like the goldfish's scales. Your collection is all done!"
> 3. "*(cheerful)* I see something sparkly! It catches light like the goldfish's shiny scales. Your third treasure is in the bag. Collection complete!"
>
> **Screen**: Final photo slides into the fourth collection slot with a celebration burst. All four slots now filled. A "Collection Complete!" banner flashes briefly.
>
> **Stuck Branch** (if child cannot find a third item):
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful, conspiratorial)* Shimmery things like to hide! Try looking at the water surface — see any sparkles? Or check wet leaves on the ground. Even a puddle can shimmer like a goldfish!"
>
> **Screen**: A soft pulsing hint arrow appears pointing toward the water's edge. The text "Try: wet rocks, water sparkles, shiny leaves" appears in a small bubble.

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, curious)* Look at all your finds together! The goldfish, and three new treasures. Now let's sort them. Which ones shimmer like the goldfish? And which ones are really different? Tell me!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child groups finds: "The rock shimmers like the fish, but the duck is different because it has feathers."
> 2. (Unexpected) Child picks one find without sorting: "I like the rock best." / Child is unsure how to sort.
> 3. (No response) Child stares at the collection display.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed, extending)* The rock shimmers but has no fins. The duck swims but has feathers, not scales. Great sorting, spotter! You found what is the same and what is different."
> 2. "*(scaffolding warmly)* Good pick! Now think — does the rock have fins like the goldfish? No! But does it shimmer? Yes! That is one way to sort them. What about your other finds?"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gently guiding)* Look at the pictures. Which one is shiny like the goldfish? Point to it! And which one looks really different? You decide!"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos displayed in a row. Two labeled zones appear below: "Shimmers like goldfish" and "Different from goldfish." Photos can be mentally grouped. Dotted comparison lines connect similar features between images.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* Wow, Shimmer Spotter! You found four things near the water. Some shimmer, some swim, some have scales and some do not. Which one is your favorite find? And why?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The rock because it's super shiny!" / "The duck because it quacks!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "The goldfish is still my favorite."
> 3. (No response) Child thinks quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(awed)* Great choice! Every find has its own special look. That is what makes exploring so fun — everything near the water is different!"
> 2. "*(warm, affirming)* The goldfish started it all! It showed you what shimmer looks like. And then you found so many other treasures. That is amazing!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle)* They are all so cool! Point to the one that made you smile the most. That is your star treasure!"
>
> **Screen**: Collection photos displayed with a golden star appearing on the child's chosen favorite. Animated water ripple effects connect all four photos. A banner reads: "4 treasures found near the water!"

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* You are an amazing Shimmer Spotter! You found shiny things and water creatures and compared every one to the goldfish. You discovered Form — every treasure has its own shape, scales, or shimmer. And you found Connection — things near the water share surprises, like shimmer and movement, even when they look totally different. You earned your Shimmer Spotter badge!"
>
> **Screen**: "Shimmer Spotter" badge — a golden magnifying glass with a goldfish silhouette inside and three small treasure icons orbiting it. "Form" and "Connection" appear in artistic aqua-blue and gold lettering with a wave icon and a chain-link icon. Four collection photos as small insets around the badge border.
