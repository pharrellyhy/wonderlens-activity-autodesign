## Activity: The Warm Color Treasure Hunt

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Warm Color Treasure Hunt
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4-6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Change** (How is it changing? — leaves transforming color as seasons shift) & **Connection** (How is it connected to other things? — linking warm colors across different objects in nature)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Form, Pattern, Discovery, Classification
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (creative thinking — interpreting warm colors imaginatively), Communication Skills (expressing — narrative co-creation), Self-Management Skills (organization — completing a multi-step mission)
- **Trigger Entity**: Autumn leaf (bright red)
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a bright red leaf on the ground in the park
- **Game Style**: naming_story
- **Design Version**: 2.0 — naming_story redesign
- **Last Updated**: 2026-03-24

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After the child photographs a bright red autumn leaf, the AI marvels at its fiery color and names it the first member of the "Flame Team." The child becomes a "Flame Finder" on a mission to find 3 more warm-colored things in the park, discover what each one reminds them of, give each a character name based on their imaginative response, and co-create the story of the Flame Team's autumn adventure — warming up the park before winter arrives.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the names of warm colors (red, orange, yellow). Learn that autumn leaves change color. Learn vocabulary like "cozy," "glow," "flame," "warmth." Learn that many different things in nature can share the same color family.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that warm colors share a visual quality that connects very different objects — a leaf, a berry, a flower can all belong to the same color family (**Connection**). Understand that autumn brings a transformation — leaves **Change** from green to brilliant warm colors as the season shifts. Understand that the same warm-colored object can remind different people of different things, and that personal interpretation drives creative storytelling.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice careful observation by identifying warm colors in the environment (Research Skills — observation). Practice collecting and photographing items systematically (Research Skills — data collection). Practice creative interpretation by describing what warm-colored things remind them of (Thinking Skills — creative thinking). Practice co-creating a narrative where each character's role reflects the child's own observations (Communication Skills — expressing).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Flame Finder" metaphor transforms color-hunting into a heroic quest — the child is recruiting a team of warm-colored characters to warm up the park before winter. The key mechanism is detail-harvesting: instead of sorting finds by color gradient, the AI asks "What does this warm thing remind you of?" for each find, and the child's answer becomes the seed for a character name and personality. An orange berry that reminds the child of "a tiny sunset" becomes "Sunset Berry"; a yellow leaf that "looks like sunshine on the ground" becomes "Sunshine Dancer." At synthesis, these detail-driven characters go on an autumn adventure together — warming the park in ways connected to their specific details.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a bright red leaf on the ground in the park → AI reacts to the fiery color, names it "Little Ember" with the child → launches a Flame Finder mission to find 3 more warm-colored things → per find, AI asks what it reminds the child of, then they name it together → child co-creates a story where the Flame Team warms up the park before winter, each character contributing in a way shaped by their unique detail.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1 (ages 4-6)]

**Step 1: Transition Bridge**

> **AI says**: "*(amazed gasp)* Whoa! Look at that red! This leaf is glowing like a tiny flame! I bet autumn painted it. What do you think it looks like?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It looks like fire!" / "It's like a flame!" / "It's so red!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a leaf." / "I found it on the ground." / "It's pretty."
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* Yes! A tiny flame on the ground! It's warm and bright, like a little ember glowing in the grass. Let's call it... Little Ember! I think Little Ember needs a team. I have an idea..."
> 2. "*(warm and curious)* It IS a leaf — and such a beautiful red one! It looks warm, like a little ember glowing on the ground. Can we call it Little Ember? I think Little Ember has a mission..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle wonder)* I think it looks like a tiny ember — warm and bright and glowing! Let's call this leaf Little Ember. And I have a fun idea for Little Ember..."
>
> **Screen**: The red leaf photo fills the screen with a soft golden-orange glow radiating outward from the leaf. Tiny animated ember-like sparkles drift gently upward from the leaf's edges. A small name tag reading "Little Ember" appears below the photo.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, conspiratorial tone)* Little Ember has a big job — autumn wants the Flame Team to warm up the park before winter comes! But Little Ember needs teammates. Here's your mission, Flame Finder: Find 3 warm-colored things — reds, oranges, yellows. Tell me what each one reminds you of, and we'll give it a character name. Then we'll tell the story of the Flame Team's autumn adventure! Ready?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes!" / "I'm ready!" / "Let's go!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What's warm-colored?" / "Like what?" / "I see one already!"
> 3. (No response) Child looks around or hesitates.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* Go, Flame Finder! Look for anything red, orange, or yellow. Take a photo when you find one, and tell me what it reminds you of!"
> 2. "*(helpful, encouraging)* Great question! Warm colors are red, orange, and yellow — like fire! Look around. Anything that color counts! Take a photo and tell me what it reminds you of!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* Try looking around you. Any red, orange, or yellow things nearby? Those are warm colors! Take a photo when you spot one!"
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears with the title "Flame Finder" in bold, warm-gradient lettering. Four character slots are shown — the first slot holds the red leaf photo with its "Little Ember" name tag, the other three are empty with a dotted flame-shaped outline. Below, three numbered icons: (1) a magnifying glass, (2) a speech bubble, (3) an open book. A task list shows: Find, Name, Story.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3-4 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — First Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of something warm-colored — e.g., an orange berry)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited discovery)* Ooh! You found one! Look at that warm color! What does this remind you of? A sunset? A campfire? Something cozy?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It looks like a tiny sunset!" / "Like a little campfire!" / "Like a warm marble!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a berry." / "I don't know." / "It's orange."
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* A tiny sunset — I love that! It really DOES glow like a little sunset! Let's call this character... Sunset Berry! Welcome to the Flame Team, Sunset Berry! Two more teammates to find, Flame Finder!"
> 2. "*(validating, then guiding)* A berry — nice find! And look at that warm orange color. Does it remind you of anything cozy? Maybe a little lantern? A warm marble? A tiny sun?" *(waits for child's detail, then names accordingly — e.g., "A tiny sun? Then let's call it Sun Drop!")*
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm)* Let me look... what a beautiful warm color! It reminds ME of a little lantern glowing in the dark. What does it remind YOU of? A lantern? A warm marble? A little sunset?"
>
> **Screen**: The new photo slides into the second slot with a gentle flame burst animation. A name tag appears under the photo with the character name. Counter updates to "2 of 4." The filled slots glow softly.

> **Round 2 — Second Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of another warm-colored item — e.g., a yellow leaf)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(impressed)* Another warm treasure! Look at that glow! What does this one remind you of?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Like sunshine on the ground!" / "It's like a golden feather!" / "Like a piece of honey!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a leaf." / "It's pretty." / "Yellow!"
> 3. (No response) Quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(fascinated)* Sunshine on the ground — YES! It really does look like a little piece of sunshine that fell from the sky! Let's call it... Sunshine Dancer! The Flame Team is growing! One more teammate to go!"
> 2. "*(agreeing warmly)* It IS a leaf — a beautiful yellow one! Does it remind you of anything warm? Maybe a golden coin? A piece of butter? A little star?" *(waits for child's detail, then names accordingly — e.g., "A little star? Then it's Star Leaf!")*
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* This one is so bright! It reminds ME of a piece of golden treasure. What does it remind YOU of? A gold coin? A little sunbeam? Something else warm?"
>
> **Screen**: Third slot fills in with name tag. Counter shows "3 of 4." A brief warm glow connects the filled character slots.

> **Round 3 — Third Find:**
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a third warm-colored item — e.g., a red-orange flower)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(celebrating)* YES! Flame Finder, you found the last teammate! Ooh, look at that warm color! What does it remind you of?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Like a little campfire!" / "It's like a warm hug!" / "Like a torch!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "A flower." / "I want to find more!" / "Red!"
> 3. (No response) Looks at the collection on screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* A little campfire — perfect! All those warm petals flickering like flames! Let's call this one... Campfire Bloom! The Flame Team is COMPLETE!"
> 2. "*(warmly)* A flower — and what a fiery one! Does it remind you of anything warm and cozy? A little torch? A warm blanket? A dancing flame?" *(waits for child's answer, then names accordingly — e.g., "A dancing flame? Then it's Flame Dancer!")*
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enthusiastic)* What a beautiful find! Look at those warm colors! It reminds ME of a little bonfire. What does it remind YOU of? A bonfire? A warm hug? A fiery dancer?"
>
> **Screen**: The final slot fills with a big celebration animation — all four slots pulse with a warm glow simultaneously. Small firework-like sparkles in red, orange, and yellow burst around the collection. Each character's name tag is displayed proudly. A "Story Time!" banner starts pulsing. Counter shows "4/4 — TEAM COMPLETE!"

> **STUCK BRANCH** (if child says "I can't find anything" or takes a long pause between finds):
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful, upbeat)* Hmm, let me think! Try looking down at the ground — fallen leaves and petals hide there. Or check near bushes — berries and flowers love warm colors! Even tree bark can be orange-ish. Go take a peek!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Finds something) Takes a photo.
> 2. (Still stuck) "I still can't find any."
> 3. (No response) Wanders without direction.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. *(Proceeds to the relevant round's reaction above)*
> 2. "*(reassuring)* That's okay! Look up — do you see any leaves still on the trees? Some might be yellow or orange. Even a brown stick counts — brown is a warm color too!"
> 3. *(waits 3 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Try going closer to a tree. Look at the leaves, the ground around it. Any yellow or orange hiding there?"
>
> **Screen**: A gentle pulsing compass-like icon appears with arrows pointing toward illustrated bushes, ground, and tree bark. The hint text "Look near the ground, bushes, or tree bark!" appears briefly in a warm-colored speech bubble.

**Step 4: Story Co-Creation**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, building excitement)* Flame Finder, your Flame Team is ready! Little Ember the red leaf, [Name 1] the [detail], [Name 2] the [detail], and [Name 3] the [detail]. Autumn sent them on a mission — warm up the park before winter comes! What does each one do to make the park warmer?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Sunset Berry lights up the path!" / "Sunshine Dancer makes the ground warm!" / Child starts assigning roles.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know." / "They can't warm up a park!" / "They just sit there."
> 3. (No response) Looks at the photos quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(narrating with wonder)* Once upon a time, autumn said: 'Winter is coming! I need the Flame Team!' Little Ember glowed bright to lead the way. Then [Name 1] the [detail] said, 'I can [action connected to their detail]!' And [Name 2] the [detail] said, 'I'll [action connected to their detail]!' And [Name 3] the [detail] danced around, [action connected to their detail]! Together, the Flame Team made the whole park glow with warmth. What happened when winter finally arrived?"
> 2. "*(playfully scaffolding)* Okay, let me help start! Little Ember is a glowing red leaf — so Little Ember lights up the darkest corners of the park! Now — [Name 1] reminds you of a [detail]. What could [Name 1] do to warm the park? Maybe [action suggestion based on detail]?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm, storytelling voice)* I'll start the story! One autumn day, Little Ember said: 'Team, winter is coming! We need to warm this park!' [Name 1] the [detail] said, 'I'll [action based on detail]!' And [Name 2] the [detail] joined in. What did [Name 3] the [detail] do?"
>
> **Screen**: All 4 photos displayed in a storybook-style layout with character name tags below each. A soft open-book animation frames the photos. As the AI narrates, gentle spotlight effects highlight each character when mentioned. Speech bubbles appear briefly near each character. A golden autumn-leaf border frames the story. Tiny animated leaves drift across the background.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* WOW! What an adventure! The Flame Team warmed up the whole park! Little Ember, [Name 1], [Name 2], and [Name 3] — all different, but all warm and glowing. [Name 1] reminded you of a [detail], and [Name 2] was like a [detail]. Why do you think autumn has so many warm colors?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Because the leaves change!" / "Because it's getting cold!" / "To keep the park warm before winter!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know!" / "Because it's pretty!" / "I like red."
> 3. (No response) Smiles or looks at collection.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(fascinated)* What a great idea! The leaves ARE changing — nature puts on a warm color show before winter. And YOUR Flame Team showed exactly how those warm colors make the park feel cozy!"
> 2. "*(validating)* It IS pretty! I think nature puts on a color show every autumn — like painting the park in sunset colors. And your Flame Team turned those colors into a real story!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(reflective)* I think autumn is nature's way of warming us up with colors before winter. And YOUR characters — [Name 1], [Name 2], [Name 3] — they each brought their own kind of warmth to the park!"
>
> **Screen**: The storybook layout remains with the four characters displayed. Gentle animated lines connect the characters. Animated leaves in red, orange, and yellow drift softly across the screen. Small callouts near each character show their name and the detail that inspired it.

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, proud)* Flame Finder, you were amazing today! You saw autumn's magic — leaves **Change** from green to red, orange, yellow! Nature transforms before your eyes. And you found a **Connection** — Little Ember, [Name 1], [Name 2], and [Name 3] are all different things, but they all share warm colors AND they all came together as the Flame Team to warm up the park! You earned your Flame Finder badge. Go tell someone the story of YOUR Flame Team!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) "I will!" / "That was fun!" / "I want to find more!"
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or nods.
> 3. (No response) Child watches the badge animation quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm goodbye)* It WAS fun! Keep looking for warm colors — every one could be a new character for the Flame Team! Bye, Flame Finder!"
> 2. "*(gentle)* Your Flame Team's story was really special. Bye bye, Flame Finder!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft)* The Flame Team will always be your autumn friends. Bye bye, Flame Finder!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden badge animation appears — a shield shape with a stylized flame icon in gradient colors (yellow-orange-red). The text "Flame Finder" is displayed below in bold, warm lettering. The four collection photos appear as small insets around the badge, each with its character name tag. A storybook icon nestles between the concept words. The words **"Change"** and **"Connection"** float upward in artistic lettering — "Change" styled with a gradient fill from green to yellow to red, "Connection" with tiny dotted lines linking the letters together. Warm confetti drifts down. A triumphant chime plays.
