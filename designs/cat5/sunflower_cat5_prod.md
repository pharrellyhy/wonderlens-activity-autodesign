### B. Activity Overview

**① Brief Description**

After photographing a sunflower, the AI marvels at its many different parts — petals like tiny sun rays, a bumpy seed center, a thick strong stem — and wonders what each part DOES for the plant. The child becomes a "Plant Parts Inspector" on a mission to find 3 more things in the garden that have parts with a special job. Each find gets logged with its "part" and "job," and at the end the child picks which find changes the most over time — connecting Function (parts have jobs) to Change (things grow and transform).

**② Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Learn the vocabulary "petals," "stem," "seeds," "nectar," "job." Learn that sunflower petals attract bees, the thick stem holds the heavy head up, and the seeds inside the center can grow into new plants.
- **U (Understand):** Understand that every part of a living thing has a Function — a special job that helps the whole plant or creature. Understand that living things Change over time as their parts grow, open, or make new things like seeds.
- **D (Do):** Practice close observation of plant and nature parts (Research Skills — observation), compare what different parts do for their owners (Thinking Skills — critical thinking), and track multiple finds across a garden space (Self-Management Skills — organization).

**③ Design Highlight**

The "Plant Parts Inspector" role reframes a nature walk as a detective mission — the child is not just finding things but investigating what each PART does. The metaphor of "every part has a job" is concrete enough for T1 and connects naturally to both Function (what does this part do?) and Change (how do parts grow and transform?). The synthesis twist asks the child to pick which find changes the most, linking observation to temporal thinking.

**④ Typical Scenario**

Child photographs a sunflower in the garden → AI points out how many parts it has, each with a special job → launches a "Plant Parts Inspector" mission to find 3 more things with parts that have jobs → child picks which find changes the most over time.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1 (ages 4–6)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (amazed wonder) "Wow — look at this sunflower! It is like a tiny sun growing right out of the ground! Those petals, that big round middle, that tall strong stem — every part looks so different. I wonder... does each part have its own special job?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "The petals are pretty!" / "The stem holds it up!"
2. (Unexpected) "I want to pick it!" / "It is yellow!"
3. (No response) Child gazes at the sunflower or screen.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted) "Yes! The petals wave at bees to come visit. And you know what? The stem holds the big heavy head up high! Every part has a job. I bet there are MORE things out here with parts that have special jobs..."
2. (warm, validating) "It IS so bright and yellow! Those yellow petals have a job — they tell the bees 'come get some nectar here!' Every part does something. I wonder what other things have parts with jobs..."
3. (wait 2s) (soft, inviting) "See those long petals? They wave at bees! And the thick stem? It holds up the heavy head. Every part has a job! What if we found more things like that?"

**Screen:** Close-up of sunflower photo with soft glow and gentle animated sparkles tracing along petals, stem, and seed center, each briefly labeled with a tiny icon.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (adventurous, inspector) "Alright — you are now an official Plant Parts Inspector! Your patrol mission has three parts. Part one: find 3 more things nearby that have a part with a special job. Part two: take a photo of each one. Part three: at the end, you pick which one CHANGES the most over time! Ready to start your patrol, Inspector?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yes!" / "Let's go!"
2. (Uncertain) "What do you mean a special job?" / "What should I look for?"
3. (No response) Looks around the garden.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (cheering) "Patrol is GO! Find something with a part that does a job, snap a photo, and report back!"
2. (encouraging) "A special job means the part DOES something for the thing it belongs to. Like a flower petal waves at bees. Or a thorn keeps animals away. Look at plants, bugs, even rocks with moss — what part stands out?"
3. (wait 2s) (gently) "Here is your first clue — look at any plant near you. See any leaves, thorns, or berries? Each one does something special! Take a photo when you spot one."

**Screen:** Mission card with an "Inspector" badge (magnifying glass over a leaf), 3 empty collection slots plus sunflower photo with golden check, and a numbered task list with icons.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1 — First Find:**

*(Child photographs something — e.g., a leaf on a bush)*

**AI says:** (eager) "Inspector report incoming! What part do you see, and what do you think its job is?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "A leaf! It catches the sun!" / "This leaf helps the plant eat!"
2. (Unexpected) "A green thing." (minimal description)
3. (No response) Just takes the photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (thrilled) "A leaf that catches sunlight — YES! That is exactly its job. The leaf soaks up sun and helps the plant make food. Patrol Log: 'Green Leaf — job: catching sunshine!' Great first find, Inspector. Two more to go!"
2. (looking closely) "A green thing! Look at how flat and wide it is. That flat shape helps it catch as much sunlight as possible — that is its job! Patrol Log: 'Flat Green Catcher — job: soaking up sun!' Two more, Inspector!"
3. (wait 2s) (enthusiastic) "Oooh, let me see! I see something flat and green. That flat shape has a job — it catches sunlight to help the plant grow. What do YOU think this part does?"

**Screen:** New photo slides into the first empty slot with a sparkle burst; label appears below: "Part: ? Job: ?" filling in based on conversation. Counter: "2 of 4."

**Round 2 — "Second Find":** Child photographs something (e.g., a berry or flower bud); AI identifies the part's job (e.g., berry protects seeds until ready, bud is waiting to open). Patrol Log updated. Counter: "3 of 4."

**Round 3 — "Final Find":** Child photographs a third item (e.g., a thorn or tree bark); AI identifies it as nature's armor or protector. Patrol Log updated. Counter: "4 of 4 — Collection complete!"

**STUCK BRANCH:** AI suggests looking close to the ground for thorns on stems (job: keeping the plant safe) or bright petals (job: waving at butterflies); provides bark on a tree trunk as a fallback.

#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (proud and excited) "Okay, Plant Parts Inspector — look at your whole collection! A sunflower with petals that wave at bees, a [first find] that [job], a [second find] that [job], and a [third find] that [job]. Every part has a job! Now here is the big question — which one of these do you think CHANGES the most over time? Which one grows, opens, or turns into something new?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "The flower bud! It opens up!" / "The seeds change into new plants!" / "The sunflower grew really tall!"
2. (Unexpected) "They all change!" / "I do not know."
3. (No response)

**AI follow-up:**

1. (mind-blown) "YES! The sunflower started as a tiny seed, grew into a tall stem, and opened into this giant flower — and now the seeds in the middle will fall out and grow into NEW sunflowers! That is so much change from one little seed!"
2. (playfully) "You know what? You are RIGHT — they ALL change! The leaf grows bigger, the berry gets ripe, the bark gets thicker. But the sunflower might change the MOST — from a tiny seed to this giant flower with hundreds of new seeds inside. One little seed becomes a whole sun!"
3. (wait 2s) (guiding gently) "Here is a clue — think about the sunflower. It started as a teeny tiny seed in the dirt. Then it grew a stem, then leaves, then this BIG flower full of new seeds. Does anything else change that much?"

**Screen:** All 4 photos in a row at top; below, a timeline arrow labeled "Tiny Seed → Sprout → Tall Stem → Big Flower → New Seeds" with animated growth lines connecting stages.

**AI says:** (amazed and warm) "Inspector, you found FOUR different things and every single one has parts with special jobs! Why do you think nature gives every part a job to do?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "So everything can help!" / "So the plant can grow!"
2. (Unexpected) "I do not know."
3. (No response) Child looks at the collection quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted) "That is such a smart thought! Every part helps the whole plant stay alive and grow. The petals bring bees, the stem stands tall, the seeds make new flowers. When every part does its job, the whole plant works!"
2. (warmly) "It IS a big wonder! If the stem did not hold the flower up, bees could not find it. If the petals were not bright, bees would fly past. Every part helps, and together they keep the plant going and growing!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, guiding) "Think about the stem, the petals, and the seeds. They all do something different, but they all help the sunflower live. That is why each part needs its own job."

**Screen:** Collection displayed with each photo labeled "Part: Job," gentle animated lines connecting parts to jobs, soft sun animation, and tiny animated bees visiting the sunflower photo.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (warm celebration) "Congratulations, Plant Parts Inspector! You did something really special today. You discovered the Function of so many parts — petals wave at bees, stems hold flowers up, bark protects trees, and seeds grow new plants. Every part has a job! And you noticed something even bigger — the sunflower is always Changing. It started as a tiny seed, grew into this tall flower, and now it is making new seeds to start all over again. You earned your Plant Parts Inspector Badge!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Engaged) Cheers, talks about wanting to find more parts, or asks about the badge.
2. (Quiet) Smiles or says nothing.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (encouraging) "Next time you are in the garden, keep your inspector eyes open — every plant, every bug, every tree has parts with secret jobs! See you on the next patrol, Inspector!"
2. (warm) "Your badge is saved! Remember — every part you see has a job to do. Bye for now, Inspector!"

**Screen:** Golden "Plant Parts Inspector Badge" shaped like a magnifying glass with the sunflower at center and collection photos as insets; "Function" in gear-like letters with tool icons, "Change" with letters morphing from seed to flower shape; soft chime and animated seeds sprouting.
