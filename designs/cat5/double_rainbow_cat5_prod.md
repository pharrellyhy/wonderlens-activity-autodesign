### B. Activity Overview

**① Brief Description**

After the child photographs a double rainbow, the AI marvels at the two colorful arcs in the sky and wonders where else those bright colors might be hiding down on the ground. The child becomes a "Rainbow Color Scout" — searching the nearby area for 3 colorful things. For each find, the AI asks what the color reminds the child of and what feeling it gives — and the child's answer drives a character name (e.g., a red flower that feels "warm like fire" becomes "Fire Blossom," yellow leaves that feel "sunny and happy" become "Sunny Giggles"). At the end, the AI narrates a story where the rainbow sent these color characters down to paint the world, and each one paints things that match the child's specific color-feeling.

**② Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Learn that sun and rain together make a rainbow. Learn that the outer rainbow is fainter than the inner one. Learn that rainbow colors appear in order (red, orange, yellow, green, blue). Learn that light from the sun holds many colors inside it. Learn the word "faint" (lighter, harder to see).
- **U (Understand):** Understand that rainbows are caused by sunlight passing through raindrops — sun and rain working together is the Causation (why rainbows appear). Understand that rainbows Change quickly — they fade as clouds move or rain stops, showing how weather scenes transform in minutes.
- **D (Do):** Practice careful outdoor observation by spotting colorful things near the rainbow (Research Skills — observation). Practice describing what colors remind them of and how colors make them feel (Thinking Skills — critical thinking). Practice giving detail-driven character names to found objects and hearing them in a personalized story about painting the world with color.

**③ Design Highlight**

Since a child cannot collect more rainbows, the mission cleverly flips the task: bring the rainbow DOWN to earth by finding colorful objects on the ground. Each find gets a detail-harvesting question ("What does this color remind you of? What feeling does it give you?") whose answer drives the character name and the synthesis story. If a child finds a red flower and says "warm like fire," it becomes "Fire Blossom" who paints warm things in the story. A different child who finds the same red flower but says "sweet like a cherry" gets "Cherry Sweetie" who paints sweet-smelling things. The story is always unique to the child's color interpretations.

**④ Typical Scenario**

Child photographs a double rainbow after a rain shower → AI reacts with wonder at the two arcs → child becomes a "Rainbow Color Scout" searching for colorful things nearby → photographs 3 colorful finds → for each find, AI asks what the color reminds them of → child's answer drives the character name → AI narrates a story where the rainbow sent these color characters to paint the world → celebrates with a Color Scout badge.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1 (ages 4–6)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (amazed gasp) "Wow — TWO rainbows! Look at all those colors stretching across the sky! The big one is so bright and the little one is like its shy twin. Those are some amazing colors up there. Do they make you feel happy or surprised?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Happy!" / "So pretty!" / "I love rainbows!" / "Surprised!"
2. (Unexpected) "I see red and blue." / "Where does it end?" / "Can I touch it?"
3. (No response) Child stares at the rainbow quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (warm, delighted) "Me too! Rainbows always make me feel sparkly inside. I wonder — those beautiful colors are way up in the sky. Can we find them down here too?"
2. (curious, warm) "Great eyes — red and blue are up there! You can't quite touch a rainbow, but you CAN find its colors right here on the ground. Want to try?"
3. (wait 2s) (soft wonder) "It's so beautiful, isn't it? Two rainbow smiles in the sky. I wonder if we can find those same colors hiding around us..."

**Screen:** Double rainbow photo with sparkling animation along both arcs and a tiny animated raindrop splitting into color through a beam of light.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (excited, conspiratorial) "Here's a fun idea! You are now a Rainbow Color Scout! The rainbow sent its colors down to paint the world — but it needs YOUR help finding them! Your mission: find 3 colorful things nearby, discover what each color reminds you of, give each one a special character name, and tell the story of how they paint the world with color! Ready, Color Scout?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes!" / "I'm ready!" / "I see something red!"
2. (Unexpected) "What if I can't find any?" / "What colors?" / "Can I find blue?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates or looks around slowly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (cheering) "Go, Color Scout! Find anything colorful and take a photo. We'll discover what each color reminds you of!"
2. (helpful, upbeat) "Any colorful thing works! Red, orange, yellow, green, blue — pick your favorites. Look near flowers, leaves, or even puddles!"
3. (wait 2s) (encouraging) "Look near the ground. Flowers, leaves, even wet rocks can be colorful. Find something bright and take a photo!"

**Screen:** Mission card with "Rainbow Color Scout" in gradient-colored lettering, four arc-shaped collection slots (first holding the rainbow photo, three empty), and three numbered task icons.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1 — First Find:**

*(Child takes a photo of something colorful nearby)*

**AI says:** (excited discovery) "Ooh! Look at that color! What does this color remind you of? What feeling does it give you?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "It's warm like fire!" / "It feels sunny and happy!" / "It's like the ocean — calm!"
2. (Unexpected) "It's red." / "A flower." / Names the color or object but not a feeling.
3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted, naming) "[Uses child's color-feeling to build name] Warm like fire? Then this is FIRE BLOSSOM! Hello, Fire Blossom! You paint the world with warmth! That's 1 color character — 2 more to go!"
2. (warm, scaffolding) "A red flower — beautiful! Red is a strong color. Does it feel warm like a hug, or bright like a siren? ... [child answers] ... [Uses answer to name] Then this is [detail-driven name]! 2 more to go!"
3. (wait 2s) (warm, offering) "I think this color feels bright and bold! Let's call it Bold Spark! Hello, Bold Spark! 2 more to go!"

**Screen:** Photo slides into slot with a color-burst animation; a name tag appears below showing the detail-driven character name; counter updates to "1 of 3 found."

**Round 2 — Second Find:**

*(Child photographs another colorful item)*

**AI says:** (excited) "Another color for our team! Look at that! What does THIS color remind you of? How does it make you feel?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Sunny and happy!" / "Cool like water!" / "It's soft like a cloud!"
2. (Unexpected) "It's yellow!" / "A leaf!" / describes what it is, not the feeling
3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted, naming) "[Uses child's color-feeling] Sunny and happy? This is SUNNY GIGGLES! Hello, Sunny Giggles! You paint everything with happiness! 1 more to go!"
2. (warm, scaffolding) "A yellow leaf! Yellow is such a cheerful color. Does it feel warm like sunshine, or light like a butterfly? ... [child answers] ... [Uses answer to name] Then this is [detail-driven name]! 1 more to go!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, offering) "This color feels cheerful to me! Let's call it Happy Glow! 1 more to go!"

**Screen:** Photo slides into slot with color-burst animation; detail-driven name tag appears; counter updates to "2 of 3 found."

**Round 3 — Third Find:**

*(Child photographs a third colorful item)*

**AI says:** (thrilled) "One more color character! What does THIS color remind you of? What feeling does it give you?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Cool and smooth like the sky!" / "Fresh like grass!" / "Sparkly like a jewel!"
2. (Unexpected) "It's blue!" / "A puddle!" / names color or object
3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (thrilled, naming) "[Uses child's color-feeling] Cool and smooth like the sky? This is SKY BREEZE! Hello, Sky Breeze! You paint the world with coolness! Team complete!"
2. (warm, scaffolding) "A blue puddle! Blue is so calming. Does it feel cool like water, or deep like the night? ... [child answers] ... [Uses answer to name] Then this is [detail-driven name]! Team complete!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, offering) "This color feels calm and peaceful! Let's call it Gentle Wave! Team complete!"

**Screen:** Photo slides into slot with color-burst animation; detail-driven name tag appears; counter updates to "3 of 3 found — Team complete!" All slots glow with rainbow shimmer.

**STUCK BRANCH:** AI suggests looking near flowers, bushes, or puddles for colorful items; provides two rounds of specific location hints. "Look near the ground — any bright color counts! A red berry, a green leaf, even a shiny puddle!"

#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (proud, storytelling voice) "Your color characters are ready! The rainbow looked down and said — I need helpers to paint the world! Are you ready for the story?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yeah!" / "Tell the story!" / "What happens?"
2. (Unexpected) "I like my colors!" / "Paint!"
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen with anticipation.

**AI follow-up (detail-driven story — each character's action comes from their specific color-feeling):**

1. (narrating, using details from all three finds) "The rainbow looked down from the sky and called out — I need painters! Down came [Name 1], [Name 2], and [Name 3]! [Name 1] flew to the ground and painted all the [child's detail-driven things — e.g., 'warm things — campfires, cozy blankets, and hugs!'] Then [Name 2] swooshed over and painted the [e.g., 'happy things — smiles, laughter, and sunny mornings!'] And [Name 3] floated down and painted the [e.g., 'cool things — breezes, rivers, and the wide open sky!'] Together they painted the WHOLE WORLD with color!"
2. (warm, scaffolding with details) "Whoooosh! The rainbow sent its painters! [Name 1] said 'I'm [child's feeling]! I'll paint the [detail-driven things]!' [Name 2] said 'I'm [child's feeling]! I'll paint the [detail-driven things]!' And [Name 3] said 'I'm [child's feeling]! I'll paint the [detail-driven things] too!' The world was so colorful!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, using details) "Here's the story! The rainbow needed painters. [Name 1] painted [detail-driven action]. [Name 2] painted [detail-driven action]. [Name 3] painted [detail-driven action]. The whole world was full of color! Do you like that story?"

**Screen:** All 4 photos in a curved arc formation; each character flies down from the rainbow with paint-splash trails in their color; character name tags beneath each; painted scenes bloom outward from each character matching the child's descriptions; "Rainbow Painters" banner.

**AI says:** (warm, wondering) "Wow! Each of your color characters painted something different! Why do you think the rainbow has SO many different colors?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Because the sun came out!" / "The rain made things wet and shiny!" / "Because the world needs lots of colors!"
2. (Unexpected) "I don't know!" / "Because flowers are pretty!" / "I like colors."
3. (No response) Child looks at the collection quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (fascinated) "That's a wonderful idea! The sun IS shining after the rain — and sunlight has all the rainbow colors hiding inside it. When light hits raindrops, the colors come out! That's what CAUSED the rainbow up there — and your color characters brought those colors to everything around us!"
2. (validating) "The world DOES need lots of colors! And you know what? The sun's light has all those colors inside it. When the light goes through raindrops, the colors spread out — and THAT is what makes the rainbow appear! Your characters showed us how each color makes the world feel different!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, guiding) "Think about what happened first — rain fell, then the sun came out, and then you saw all those colors. What do rain and sunshine do together? They make a rainbow — and your characters showed us what each color feels like!"

**Screen:** Four photos in arc order with animated lines connecting them; original rainbow photo shown smaller above for visual parallel; animated light beams pass through a raindrop icon and split into colors; each color beam connects to its corresponding character below.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (warm, proud) "Rainbow Color Scout, you were amazing today! You discovered Causation — WHY rainbows appear. Sun and rain work together to make those colors in the sky! And you saw Change happen — the rainbow faded and shifted right before your eyes, because weather changes so fast. But YOUR color characters — [Name 1], [Name 2], and [Name 3] — they'll keep painting the world forever! You earned your Color Scout badge. Go tell someone the story of your rainbow painters!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Engaged) "I will!" / "That was fun!" / "I want to find more colors!"
2. (Quiet) Smiles or nods.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (warm goodbye) "It WAS fun! Next time it rains and the sun peeks out, look for another rainbow — and more color characters! Bye, Color Scout!"
2. (gentle) "Your rainbow painters made the world beautiful today. Bye bye, Color Scout!"

**Screen:** Golden badge with two curved rainbow arcs (mimicking the double rainbow), "Rainbow Color Scout" in gradient lettering, collection photos as small insets with character name tags, and "Causation" and "Change" floating upward in artistic lettering with sun/raindrop icons and paint-splash effects. Rainbow confetti drifts down.
