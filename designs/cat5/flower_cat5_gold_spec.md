## Activity: The Garden Rescue Squad (花园救援队)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Garden Rescue Squad (花园救援队)
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Responsibility**, **Connection**
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Care (designed), Interdependence (designed), Empathy (designed), Conservation (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Social Skills (empathy, cooperation), Thinking Skills (creative thinking, transfer), Communication Skills (expressing ideas)
- **Experience Pillar**: Nurture
- **Game Style**: rescue_team
- **Design Version**: 1.0
- **Last Updated**: 2026-04-01
- **Trigger Entity**: Flower
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a flower in the garden or park
- **Mapping Source**: none
- **IB Theme**: Sharing the Planet

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: The child photographs a flower and notices it looks a little droopy. The AI frames the child as a "Rescue Captain" leading a Garden Rescue Squad. The child explores the garden to find 3 more things that need help — a dry leaf, a fallen twig, a lonely pebble — and proposes care for each. At synthesis, the rescued items help EACH OTHER: the leaf shades the pebble, the twig props up a wilting petal, the pebble holds down the leaf in the wind. The magic moment is seeing the rescued team form a mutual aid circle.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Plants and garden things can show signs of needing help (drooping, drying, falling); water, shade, and support are ways to help living things; different items in a garden have different needs; things that receive help can also give help to others
  - **U (Understand)**: When we notice something needs help, we can take Responsibility by thinking of a solution; things that seem separate in a garden are actually Connected — they can support each other in surprising ways
  - **D (Do)**: Observe signs of need in natural objects (Research — observation); propose creative care solutions for garden items (Thinking — creative thinking); explain how rescued items could help each other (Communication — expressing ideas)

- **③ Design Highlight**: The mutual aid synthesis is the star mechanic. After the child rescues 3–4 items by proposing care, the AI flips the script: "Now your rescue team is complete — but guess what? They can help EACH OTHER!" The child discovers that the leaf they watered can shade the pebble, the twig they propped up can support a drooping petal, and the pebble can anchor the leaf in the wind. This transforms a one-way caregiving exercise into a web of interdependence — the "I helped!" feeling deepens into "They help each other!"

- **④ Typical Scenario**: A child in a garden photographs a droopy flower, then searches for 3 more nearby things that need help — proposing care for each — and discovers at the end that all the rescued items can help one another.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs a flower in the garden or park with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(tender, concerned tone)* Oh, look at this flower! Its petals are drooping down a little, like it is feeling tired. I think it might need some help. What do you think it needs?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It needs water!" / "It looks thirsty!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's pretty!" / "I like the color!"
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the flower quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, impressed)* Water — great idea! A little drink would perk those petals right up. You already know how to help! I bet other things in this garden need help too."
> 2. "*(warm, agreeing)* It IS pretty! Even pretty flowers get tired sometimes. See how the petals droop? Maybe it needs a little water or some shade. You could be its helper!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, coaxing)* See how its petals hang down? It looks a little sleepy. Maybe it needs water, or a friend nearby. What do you think would help?"
>
> **Screen**: Flower photo centered with a soft warm glow. The drooping petals are subtly highlighted with a gentle pulsing circle. A tiny water droplet icon and sun icon float nearby, suggesting possible needs.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing — Rescue Captain**

> **AI says**: "*(heroic, rallying tone)* You are now the Rescue Captain of the Garden Rescue Squad! Your mission has three parts. First, find three things nearby that need help. Second, tell me what each one needs. Third, we will see how they can help each other! Ready, captain?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Let's rescue things!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What kind of things need help?"
> 3. (No response) Child looks around the garden uncertainly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited, encouraging)* Let's go, captain! Look around the garden — on the ground, near bushes, by the path. Find something that looks dry, fallen, or lonely. Take a photo when you spot it!"
> 2. "*(warm, helpful)* Great question! Look for things that are dry, fallen over, cracked, or all alone. Like a leaf that is curling up, or a stick that fell down. Anything that looks like it could use a friend!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, guiding)* Try looking near the ground first. See any dry leaves? Fallen sticks? A little stone all by itself? Take a photo of anything that looks like it needs help!"
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears with a "Rescue Captain" badge at the top — a shield with a green leaf and a tiny heart. Four collection slots are shown in a row — the first slot is filled with the flower photo and labeled "Flower — needs water." Three empty slots glow softly, each marked with a red cross icon. A numbered checklist reads: 1) Find 3 things that need help. 2) Tell me what each one needs. 3) See how they help each other!

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

> **Round 1** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a dry, curled leaf on the ground)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(concerned, caring tone)* Oh no, look at this leaf! It is all dry and curled up at the edges. It looks like it has been in the sun too long. What do you think it needs, captain?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Water!" / "It needs a drink!" / "Shade!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's crunchy!" / "I can step on it!"
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the leaf silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, celebrating)* Yes! A little water would help it uncurl. *Imagine the leaf slowly opening up, getting softer and greener.* It is already feeling better thanks to you! Two more rescues to go, captain!"
> 2. "*(playful, redirecting)* It IS crunchy! That means it is really dry. Crunchy leaves are thirsty leaves! If we gave it some water, it might uncurl and get soft again. You just figured out what it needs! Two more to find!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, prompting)* Feel how dry it looks? When leaves curl up like that, they are thirsty. What if we gave it some water? *The leaf starts to uncurl a tiny bit.* It is getting better! Two more rescues to go!"
>
> **Screen**: Leaf photo slides into the second collection slot. The slot label updates to "Leaf — needs water." A tiny animation shows the curled leaf icon gently uncurling. Counter shows "1 of 3 rescued."
>
> **Round 2** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a fallen twig or small stick on the ground)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(sympathetic, gentle tone)* Aww, this little twig has fallen down! It is lying flat on the ground. It used to stand up tall on a branch. What do you think it needs, captain?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It needs to stand up!" / "Prop it up!" / "Put it against something!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a stick!" / "I can throw it!"
> 3. (No response) Child picks up the twig and looks at it.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted, encouraging)* Yes! If we prop it up against a rock or another plant, it can stand tall again. *Imagine the twig leaning up, reaching toward the sky.* Look at that — it is standing again, thanks to you! One more rescue to go!"
> 2. "*(warm, redirecting)* It IS a stick — a fallen one! It used to be up high on a tree. What if we helped it stand up again by leaning it against something? *The little twig leans up proudly.* You helped it stand! One more rescue!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, encouraging)* This twig fell from its tree. It looks like it wants to stand up again. What if we propped it against a rock? *The twig tilts upward, reaching toward the sun.* There it goes! One more to find!"
>
> **Screen**: Twig photo slides into the third collection slot. The slot label updates to "Twig — needs support." A tiny animation shows a stick icon tilting from horizontal to a proud leaning angle. Counter shows "2 of 3 rescued."
>
> **Round 3** (full detail):
>
> *(Child takes a photo of a small pebble sitting alone on the path)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(soft, empathetic tone)* Oh, look at this little pebble. It is sitting all alone on the path. All the other pebbles are way over there. It looks a bit lonely. What do you think it needs, captain?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Friends!" / "It needs other rocks nearby!" / "Move it closer to the others!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's just a rock." / "I can kick it!"
> 3. (No response) Child holds the pebble and looks at it.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(touched, warm)* Friends nearby — what a kind idea! *Imagine the pebble scooting closer to the other rocks. It wiggles happily.* It has company now, thanks to you! Your rescue team is complete, captain!"
> 2. "*(playful, redirecting)* It IS a rock — a lonely one! See how it is far from the other pebbles? What if we moved it closer so it had friends? *The pebble wiggles with joy next to its new neighbors.* You helped! Rescue team complete!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(warm, gently prompting)* See how it is all by itself? The other pebbles are in a group over there. Maybe it needs to be closer to friends! *The pebble slides over and snuggles next to the others.* It looks happy now! All three rescues done!"
>
> **Screen**: Pebble photo slides into the fourth collection slot. The slot label updates to "Pebble — needs friends." A tiny animation shows a small stone icon moving toward a cluster of stones. Counter shows "3 of 3 rescued!" with a brief sparkle celebration burst.
>
> **Round 4 (optional)** (abbreviated — if child is especially engaged):
>
> If the child finds a fourth item (e.g., a wilting petal, a cracked seed pod, a muddy bug), AI identifies its need (sunshine, protection, a bath), child proposes care, and the item joins the rescue team as a bonus member. AI says: "*(amazed)* A bonus rescue! Your squad is even bigger now!"
>
> **Stuck Branch** (if child cannot find something that needs help):
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful, conspiratorial)* Things that need help like to hide! Try looking on the ground near bushes — dry leaves curl up there. Or check the path for little stones all alone. Even a twig lying flat on the dirt needs help standing up!"
>
> **Screen**: A soft pulsing hint arrow appears pointing toward the ground near a bush. The text "Try: dry leaves, fallen twigs, lonely stones" appears in a small bubble.

**Step 4: Synthesis — The Magic Moment (Mutual Aid)**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, building suspense)* Captain, your Garden Rescue Squad is complete! You helped the leaf, the twig, and the pebble. But guess what? Now THEY can help each other! Watch this — the leaf you watered is big and green now. It can give shade to the little pebble when the sun gets too hot! And the twig you propped up? It can lean over and hold up a droopy petal so it catches more sunlight! But here is the big question — how would the pebble help the leaf?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The pebble can hold the leaf down!" / "It can sit on the leaf so the wind does not blow it away!" / "It keeps the leaf safe!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know." / "The pebble is too small to help." / Child names a different kind of help.
> 3. (No response) Child thinks quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed, celebrating)* YES! The pebble can sit on the leaf and hold it down when the wind blows! The leaf shades the pebble, the twig holds up the petal, and the pebble protects the leaf. They are ALL helping each other! That is what a real rescue team does, captain!"
> 2. "*(warm, scaffolding)* Think about it — the leaf is light, and the wind might blow it away. But the pebble is heavy! What if the pebble sat on the leaf to hold it down? *The pebble settles gently on the leaf's edge.* See? Even the smallest member of the team can help! They are a real rescue team now!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, revealing)* Here is a hint — the wind is blowing! The leaf might fly away. But the pebble is heavy and strong. What if the pebble sat on the leaf to keep it safe? *The pebble gently rests on the leaf.* Now they are ALL helping each other. Your rescue team is the best!"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos displayed in a circle (flower at top, leaf at right, twig at bottom, pebble at left). Animated arrows appear between them showing mutual aid connections: leaf → pebble (shade arrow with sun icon), twig → flower petal (support arrow with upward icon), pebble → leaf (anchor arrow with wind icon). A glowing heart appears in the center of the circle. A banner reads: "Rescue Team — Helping Each Other!" Each item's "before" state (droopy, dry, fallen, alone) fades into a "better" state (perky, soft, standing, together).

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, reflective)* Captain, look at what you did! You found four things that needed help. You gave each one exactly what it needed. And then something amazing happened — they started helping EACH OTHER. Which rescue was your favorite? Which one made you feel the best?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The pebble because it was lonely!" / "The leaf because it was so dry!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I liked finding all of them!" / "Can I rescue more?"
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the display thoughtfully.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(touched)* That one really needed you! And now it is part of a team. Every member of your rescue squad matters — even the smallest one can do something big for someone else."
> 2. "*(warm, celebrating)* You loved ALL of them — that is what makes you such a great captain! And yes, there are always more things that need help. You can be a Rescue Captain every time you go outside!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle)* They all needed your help, and you gave it. Point to the one that makes you smile the most. That is your star rescue!"
>
> **Screen**: Collection photos displayed with a golden star appearing on the child's chosen favorite. Animated green vines gently connect all four photos. A banner reads: "4 rescues complete — the squad helps each other!"

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* You are an incredible Rescue Captain! You found things that needed help and gave each one care. That is Responsibility — when you notice something needs help and you do something about it. And then your rescue team helped each other — the leaf shaded the pebble, the twig propped up the petal, the pebble held down the leaf. That is Connection — everything in a garden is linked together, helping one another. You earned your Rescue Captain badge!"
>
> **Screen**: "Rescue Captain" badge — a green shield with a golden heart in the center and four small icons around it (flower, leaf, twig, pebble). "Responsibility" and "Connection" appear in artistic green and gold lettering with a caring-hands icon and a linked-chain icon. Four collection photos as small insets around the badge border. Gentle leaf and petal animations float across the screen.
