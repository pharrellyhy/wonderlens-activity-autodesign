## The Rain Guard Patrol

### A. Basic Info

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Activity Name | The Rain Guard Patrol |
| Activity Category | Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device) |
| Recommended Tier | T0 (ages 2–4) |
| Core IB Key Concepts | Connection, Function |
| Related Concepts | Identity, Expression, Discovery, Creativity |
| ATL Skills Focus | Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking) |
| Game Style | naming_story |

### B. Activity Overview

**① Brief Description**

After the child photographs their raincoat outside, the AI makes a big "swish-swish!" and marvels at how the raincoat keeps the child dry and cozy. The AI wonders — what else outside protects things from rain? The child becomes a "Rain Guard" — searching the yard for 2 things that cover, shelter, or protect (a big leaf, an umbrella, a roof edge, a bush). Each find gets a character name, and at the end the child tells a tiny story about the Rain Guard team protecting things from the rain together.

**② Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Learn that a raincoat has a hood and is long. Learn that a raincoat feels smooth and slippery. Learn that a raincoat makes a swishy sound. Learn the word "protect" (keep safe, cover up).
- **U (Understand):** Understand that a raincoat and other things that block rain share a Connection — they all cover or protect something from water. Understand that the Function of a raincoat is to keep you dry, and other things have a similar function of sheltering or protecting.
- **D (Do):** Practice finding things that share a function — "protecting from rain." Practice giving names to found objects and creating a story. Practice photographing finds and describing them.

**③ Design Highlight**

The child's own raincoat becomes the "captain" of a Rain Guard Patrol — a team of protectors. The naming_story synthesis turns the collection into a cast of characters: the raincoat, a big leaf, and a shelter all become "Rain Guards" with names the child invents. The final story moment asks the tiny team to protect something together, making the concept of "function = protection" tangible through narrative play. The "swish-swish" sound thread ties every round together with sensory fun.

**④ Typical Scenario**

Child photographs their raincoat outside → AI goes "swish-swish!" and notices how it protects → child becomes a "Rain Guard" → finds 2 things that cover or protect → names each as a character → tells a tiny story about the Rain Guard team → celebrates with a Rain Guard badge.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T0 (ages 2–4)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (delighted, playful) "Swish-swish! A raincoat! It keeps you SO cozy! Do you feel brave in it?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yeah!" / "I'm brave!" / "Swish-swish!" / child swishes
2. (Unexpected) "It's yellow!" / "It has a hood!" / "I like rain!"
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (thrilled) "So brave! Raincoat protects you! I wonder — what else protects things from rain?"
2. (warm, validating) "Yellow and cozy! With a big hood! Your raincoat keeps rain away. What ELSE keeps rain away?"
3. (wait 2s) (soft, playful) "Swish-swish! Your coat is like a little roof for your body! What else is like a roof?"

**Screen:** Raincoat photo centered with animated raindrops bouncing off the coat surface; small "swish" sound-wave icons on either side.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (excited, playful) "You are a Rain Guard! Find 2 things that protect from rain. Take a photo! Go!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay!" / "I see one!" / child starts looking
2. (Unexpected) "What protects?" / "Like what?"
3. (No response) Child looks around slowly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (cheering) "Go go, Rain Guard! Find things that cover and protect!"
2. (helpful, warm) "Things that block rain! A big leaf? A roof? An umbrella? Go look!"
3. (wait 2s) (encouraging) "Look up! A roof? Look down! A big leaf? Find something that stops rain!"

**Screen:** Mission card with "Rain Guard" badge (raindrop shield icon), 3 slots (first filled with raincoat photo, 2 empty with raindrop-splash placeholders), and a "Find 2!" counter.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1 — First Rain Guard Find:**

*(Child takes a photo of something that covers or protects — e.g., a big leaf, an umbrella, a tree canopy, a roof overhang)*

**AI says:** (excited discovery) "Ooh! Does it stop rain? Tell me!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "A leaf!" / "Big tree!" / "It covers!"
2. (Unexpected) "Pretty!" / "It's green!" / describes the color, not the function
3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted) "A big leaf! It covers things, just like your raincoat! Let's give it a name. What's its name?"
2. (warm, scaffolding) "Green and big! And look — it could cover something from rain! It's a rain protector! What should we call this friend?"
3. (wait 2s) (warm) "I see something that could block rain! A new Rain Guard! What name should we give it?"

**Child names the find** (or AI suggests): "Leafy! / Big Cover! / Shade!"

**AI response to naming:** (celebrating) "Hello, [name]! You're on the Rain Guard team! 1 more to go!"

**Screen:** Photo slides into slot 2 with a raindrop-shield animation; a small name tag appears below; counter updates to "1 of 2 found."

**Round 2 — Second Rain Guard Find:** Child photographs another protector (e.g., a bench overhang, a bush, a hat); AI celebrates the find, child names it, and the Rain Guard team is complete.

**STUCK BRANCH:** AI suggests looking up for a roof or tree, or looking for big leaves. If still stuck, AI points toward the door or the biggest leaf in sight.

#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (proud, storytelling voice) "Look! Your Rain Guard team! Your raincoat, [Name 1], and [Name 2]. They all protect! What do they do when rain comes?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "They cover!" / "They keep dry!" / "They hide from rain!" / child makes up a tiny story
2. (Unexpected) "I don't know" / "Rain goes splash!" / "Swish-swish!"
3. (No response) Child looks at the photos with a smile.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (amazed, narrating) "Yes! When rain falls — drip drop — raincoat goes swish-swish, [Name 1] covers up, and [Name 2] blocks the rain! The best Rain Guard team!"
2. (warm, scaffolding) "Splash splash! Rain is coming! But your raincoat says 'swish-swish, I'll keep you dry!' And [Name 1] says 'I'll cover too!' They protect together!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, playful) "I think when rain falls, raincoat says 'I got you!' And [Name 1] says 'Me too!' They ALL protect together. Do you like that story?"

**Screen:** All 3 photos in a triangle formation with animated rain falling from above; a transparent shield effect over all three; character name tags beneath each; "Rain Guard Team" banner.

**AI says:** (warm, wondering) "Wow! So many things block rain! Why do things need to stay dry?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "So we don't get wet!" / "To stay warm!" / "Rain is cold!"
2. (Unexpected) "I like rain!" / "Splash!" / unrelated answer
3. (No response) Child smiles at the screen.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (fascinated) "Yes! Staying dry keeps us warm and cozy! That's why we have raincoats and roofs and leaves!"
2. (warm, connecting) "Rain IS fun! But sometimes we need to stay dry. That's what Rain Guards are for — keeping us cozy!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle) "Being dry feels warm and cozy! That's why raincoats and big leaves are so helpful!"

**Screen:** Collection photos with animated rain bouncing off each item; small "dry zone" circles glow beneath each protector; warm golden light from the center.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (warm, proud) "Rain Guard, great job! You found Connection — raincoat, leaves, and roofs all keep things dry! And you saw their Function — they protect! Swish-swish! You earned your Rain Guard badge!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Engaged) "Swish-swish!" / "Yay!" / "Rain Guard!"
2. (Quiet) Child smiles or waves.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (celebration) "Swish-swish! Great job! Bye, Rain Guard!"
2. (gentle) "You kept everyone dry today. Bye bye, Rain Guard!"

**Screen:** Badge spinning into center labeled "Rain Guard" with raindrop-shield and hood motif; collection photos as small insets; "Connection" and "Function" in blue-and-gold lettering; blue raindrop confetti drifts down.
