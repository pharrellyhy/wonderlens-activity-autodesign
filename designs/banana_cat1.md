## Activity: The Banana's Big Adventure (香蕉历险记)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Banana's Big Adventure (香蕉历险记)
- **Activity Category**: Category 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device, Indoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: Change, Connection
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Journey, Growth, Systems, Discovery
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Thinking Skills (creative thinking, transfer), Communication Skills (expressing ideas, listening), Research Skills (observation)
- **Trigger Entity**: Banana
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a banana on the kitchen table

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: The child becomes a "Travel Reporter" who interviews a banana about its life journey. The AI voices the banana as a shy traveler who needs the child's help to remember and tell the story of where it came from, how it traveled, and how it ended up on the kitchen table. Each round covers a different chapter of the banana's life — from a tiny green bud on a faraway tree, to a long boat ride, to arriving at the store, to sitting on the table right now.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: (1) Bananas grow on tall plants in warm places, (2) bananas start green and turn yellow, (3) bananas travel a long way to reach us, (4) "tropical" means a warm, sunny place, (5) bananas grow in bunches called "hands"
  - **U (Understand)**: (1) Food changes as it moves from where it grows to where we eat it (Change), (2) Many people and steps connect a fruit on a faraway plant to the food on our table (Connection)
  - **D (Do)**: (1) Narrate a sequence of events in order (creative thinking), (2) Express ideas about a familiar object's hidden history (communication), (3) Make observations about an everyday object's features (observation)

- **③ Design Highlight**: The banana is cast as a "shy world traveler" who has had an amazing journey but is too bashful to tell its own story. The child becomes its Travel Reporter — asking questions and filling in the chapters. This flips the typical dynamic: instead of the AI quizzing the child, the child is the interviewer helping the banana share its tale. The metaphor of a passport with stamps for each life chapter makes the journey tangible.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: A child in the kitchen photographs a banana, then helps the banana tell its life story chapter by chapter — from tropical plant to kitchen table.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

---

**Step 1: Transition Bridge**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, surprised tone)* Oh wow, a banana! Look how bright and yellow it is — like a little sunshine sitting on your table! I bet this banana has been on a really long trip to get here. Where do you think it came from?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "From a tree!" / "From the store!" / "From far away!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "From my mom!" / "I don't know." / "I want to eat it!"
> 3. (No response) Child is silent or looking at the screen.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted tone)* Yes! This banana came from SO far away. It had a big adventure before it got to your table!"
> 2. "*(playful, validating tone)* That's true, someone did bring it home! But before that, this banana went on a really long trip. A big adventure!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(gentle, coaxing tone)* This banana is pretty quiet too. But I think it has a secret — it went on a big adventure!"

> **Screen**: The child's banana photo is centered on screen with a soft golden glow radiating outward. Tiny animated sparkles drift around the banana like travel dust. A faint dotted line — like a journey path — begins to trace from the bottom-right corner.

---

**Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo — "The Travel Reporter"**

> **AI says**: "*(conspiratorial, fun tone)* Here's a secret — this banana is actually a world traveler! It went on an amazing journey. But it's a little shy. It needs YOUR help to tell its story. You will be its Travel Reporter! I'll start each chapter, and you tell me what happened. Ready? Like this — Chapter One: the banana was a tiny green bud on a big, tall plant. It was SO hot and sunny. I think the banana said… 'Wow, it's warm up here!' What do you think it said next?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It said 'I'm so small!'" / "It said 'I like the sun!'"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Bananas can't talk!" / "I want a different game."
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(enthusiastic tone)* Ha, I love that! The little banana bud, looking around at the big sunny world. Great reporting! Let's keep going with the next chapters."
> 2. "*(playful, warm tone)* You're right, bananas are pretty quiet! But that's why they need a reporter — someone to imagine their story. You'd be great at it. Let's try!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(encouraging tone)* Maybe the little banana said 'Hello, sunshine!' What do you think? You can say anything!"

> **Screen**: The banana photo shrinks to the top-right corner. A large illustrated "passport" book opens on screen. Page 1 shows a simple cartoon of a green banana bud on a tall plant under a bright sun. A "Chapter 1" stamp appears. A small microphone icon with "Travel Reporter" text and a press badge graphic appears at the bottom.

---

**Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction (3–5 rounds)**

**Round 1 — Chapter: "Growing Up Green"**
*Target: Simple sensory description. Build confidence.*

> **AI says**: "*(storyteller tone)* Chapter Two! The banana grew bigger every day, hanging in a big bunch with its banana brothers and sisters. It was still green! Imagine — you're this green banana, hanging high up on a plant, and you can see the whole jungle below. Travel Reporter, what does the banana see from up there?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It sees birds!" / "Trees!" / "Other bananas!" / "Rain!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It sees my house!" / "A dinosaur!" / "I like green."
> 3. (No response) Child is silent.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited tone)* Ooh, yes! The banana looks down and sees {child's answer}. That's amazing reporting! Did you know bananas grow in bunches called 'hands'? This banana had lots of friends up there."
> 2. "*(delighted, go-with-it tone)* Whoa, {child's answer}! This banana has a wild imagination — just like you! It's hanging way up high with all its banana friends, looking at everything below."
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(helpful tone)* Hmm, I think from way up there, the banana might see big green leaves and colorful birds. What else could be down there?"

> **Screen**: The passport turns to Page 2. Illustration shows a bunch of green bananas hanging from a plant. Animated leaves sway gently. When the child responds, small icons of what they describe (birds, trees, etc.) pop up around the illustration. A "Chapter 2" stamp appears.

---

**Round 2 — Chapter: "The Big Boat Ride"**
*Target: Sequence and cause-and-effect. Slightly more complex.*

> **AI says**: "*(adventurous tone)* Chapter Three! One day, someone picked the banana — still green! — and put it in a big box with lots of other bananas. Then the box went on a huge boat across the ocean! Travel Reporter, the banana is on a boat for the first time. What does it feel?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's scared!" / "It feels the waves!" / "It's excited!" / "It's cold!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Boats go fast!" / "I went on a boat!" / "It's yellow now."
> 3. (No response) Child is silent.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed tone)* Great reporting! The banana feels {child's answer} on its very first boat ride! The ocean is so big. And guess what — during the boat ride, the banana slowly starts to turn yellow. The trip changes it!"
> 2. "*(warm, connecting tone)* Oh, you know about boats! This banana was on a really big one. And something cool happened — during the long boat ride, the banana started turning from green… to yellow! The trip changed it."
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(gentle, inviting tone)* I think the banana might feel the waves going whoooosh. Maybe a little bumpy! And during the ride, it started turning yellow. Isn't that cool?"

> **Screen**: Passport Page 3. Illustration of a cargo ship on blue ocean waves with boxes of bananas. Gentle rocking animation. When "turning yellow" is mentioned, the banana in the illustration slowly shifts from green to yellow with a sparkle effect. "Chapter 3" stamp appears.

---

**Round 3 — Chapter: "Arriving at the Store"**
*Target: Perspective-taking and surprise. Peak engagement.*

> **AI says**: "*(dramatic, playful tone)* Chapter Four! The boat arrives! The banana — yellow now — gets put on a shelf in a big, bright store. It's never seen so many people before! Travel Reporter, imagine you're the banana on that shelf. You see someone walking toward you. What do you hope they say?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Pick me!" / "I hope they say 'that one looks yummy!'" / "Take me home!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't want to be eaten!" / "Buy apples instead!" / Child makes a funny voice.
> 3. (No response) Child is silent.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(laughing, warm tone)* Ha! '{child's answer}' — I bet that's exactly what it thought! And guess who came along and picked it? Someone in YOUR family! That's how it got to your kitchen table."
> 2. "*(delighted tone)* Oh that's so funny! The banana on the shelf, thinking '{child's answer}.' What a character! Well, somebody DID pick it up — someone from your family brought it all the way home to your table!"
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(playful whisper)* I think the banana might be hoping… 'Pick me, pick me! I came so far!' And someone did pick it — someone from your home!"

> **Screen**: Passport Page 4. Illustration of a bright store shelf with colorful fruits. The banana is front and center with a tiny hopeful face drawn on it. When the child responds, a speech bubble appears over the banana with a simplified version of their words. "Chapter 4" stamp appears. All four stamps are now visible at the top of the passport.

---

**Round 4 (if child is engaged) — Chapter: "Right Here, Right Now"**
*Target: Open-ended reflection. Child leads.*

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective tone)* Chapter Five — the final chapter! The banana made it all the way to your table. From a tiny bud in a sunny jungle, to a big boat, to a store, to right here with you. Travel Reporter, what do you think the banana would say to you right now, after such a long trip?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Thank you for finding me!" / "I'm happy to be here!" / "That was a long trip!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Eat me!" / "I'm tired!" / Child giggles.
> 3. (No response) Child is silent.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(moved, warm tone)* '{child's answer}' — what a beautiful ending to the story. You really are an amazing Travel Reporter."
> 2. "*(laughing warmly)* Ha! After that huge adventure, I bet the banana IS {child's answer}! What a journey. You told its whole story so well."
> 3. *(Wait 3 seconds)* "*(soft tone)* Maybe the banana would say 'I'm so glad I'm here with you.' It came such a long way, didn't it?"

> **Screen**: Passport Page 5. Illustration shows the banana on a table (matching the real photo) with a small heart. The child's banana photo reappears, now framed inside the passport. A "Final Chapter" golden stamp appears. The full passport is shown with all stamps visible.

---

**Step 4: Celebration + Role Title**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, celebratory tone)* You did it! You just told the banana's whole life story — from a tiny green bud to your kitchen table! You are officially a Travel Reporter! You helped a shy banana share its big adventure with the world."

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child cheers, laughs, or says "I'm a Travel Reporter!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can I do another one?" / "I want to eat it now!"
> 3. (No response) Child watches the screen.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming tone)* The best Travel Reporter I know! That banana is lucky to have you."
> 2. "*(playful tone)* Yes! And now you know the amazing story of what you're eating. Every banana has a journey! You're an awesome Travel Reporter."
> 3. "*(warm tone)* Look at that badge — Travel Reporter! The banana says thank you for telling its story."

> **Screen**: A large animated badge/medal fills the center of the screen reading "Travel Reporter" with a small banana and passport icon. Gold confetti animation bursts across the screen. A cheerful chime sound plays. The child's original banana photo appears as a small inset in the badge.

---

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective tone)* You know what's amazing? That banana changed so much on its trip — it went from a tiny green bud to the bright yellow banana on your table. That's the magic of Change! And it took so many people and places to bring it here — the sunny jungle, the big boat, the store, your family. Everything is connected! That's the power of Connection. Pretty cool for one little banana, right?"

> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah!" / "Bananas are cool!" / Child nods enthusiastically.
> 2. (Unexpected) "What about apples?" / "I want to do it again!"
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen.

> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm closing tone)* It really is! Next time you see a piece of fruit, you can be its Travel Reporter too. Bye for now, Reporter!"
> 2. "*(encouraging tone)* Great idea! Every piece of food has its own adventure. You can be a Travel Reporter for anything. See you next time!"
> 3. "*(gentle closing tone)* Every banana — every piece of fruit — has a story like this. You helped this one share it. See you next time, Travel Reporter!"

> **Screen**: The words "Change" and "Connection" appear in large, colorful, hand-drawn-style lettering. "Change" is accompanied by a small animation of a banana shifting from green to yellow. "Connection" is accompanied by a dotted line connecting icons: sun → plant → boat → store → table. The travel passport sits below with all its stamps. After 3 seconds, a soft wave animation and "See you next time, Travel Reporter!" text fades in.
