## Activity: Wheels and Feelings (车轮心声)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: Wheels and Feelings (车轮心声)
- **Activity Category**: 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Function** (How does it work?) & **Connection** (How is it connected to other things?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Rules (mapping), Community (mapping), Change Over Time (mapping), Discovery (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Communication Skills (expressing ideas, listening), Thinking Skills (creative thinking), Self-Management Skills (emotional regulation)
- **Game Style**: voice_acting
- **Trigger Entity**: Bicycle
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs their bicycle in the garage and voices what the bike feels on different adventures
- **Mapping Source**: vehicles_bicycle
- **IB Theme**: How We Organize Ourselves (mapping: primary, weight=0.48)
- **Dimension Anchors**: emotions (engagement), relationship (engagement), senses (physical)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: emotions — "Do bicycles feel fun or a little scary to you? Why?"; senses — wind_on_face ("cool wind whooshing on cheeks when riding")

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After the child photographs their bicycle in the garage, the AI marvels at the shiny wheels and strong frame. The child becomes a "Bike Whisperer" — someone who can hear what the bicycle feels and says on different adventures. AI presents scenarios — riding fast downhill, getting a flat tire, waiting in the rain, discovering a new path — and the child speaks AS the bicycle, voicing its feelings and thoughts. Each scenario reveals how the bike's parts work (Function) and how the bike connects the rider to places and people (Connection).

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: A bicycle has a triangle-shaped frame like a strong stick puzzle; thin spokes spread out like a wheel spiderweb; pedaling makes the bike move forward; squeezing brakes slows the bike down with hand levers that pull cables; the chain makes a soft clicking sound as it turns
  - **U (Understand)**: Every part of a bicycle has a special job — pedals for pushing, brakes for stopping, wheels for rolling. That is **Function** (how things work). A bicycle connects the rider to the park, the school, and the neighborhood — linking people and places together. That is **Connection** (how things are linked).
  - **D (Do)**: Practice expressing emotions and ideas through voice acting (Communication Skills — expressing ideas). Practice imagining how something non-human might feel (Thinking Skills — creative thinking). Practice recognizing and naming different feelings (Self-Management Skills — emotional regulation).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Bike Whisperer" metaphor transforms a parked bicycle into a character with feelings and a voice. The child does not answer questions ABOUT the bike — they speak AS the bike, giving it personality, emotions, and thoughts. Each scenario puts the bicycle in a different situation that naturally reveals how its parts work and what it connects the rider to. The child earns the title by showing they can truly understand and voice a bicycle's inner world.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs their bicycle in the garage → AI notices the wheels and frame → child becomes a Bike Whisperer who voices what the bicycle feels during different adventures.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_1 conversation about the bicycle.
> **Conversation anchor**: senses — wind_on_face ("cool wind whooshing on cheeks when riding"); emotions — "Do bicycles feel fun or a little scary to you?"
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* You told me riding your bike feels like cool wind whooshing on your cheeks — that sounds amazing! I keep wondering... what does the BIKE feel when you ride? What if your bike could talk?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It would say wheee!" / "It would be happy!" / "Yeah, let's try!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Bikes can't talk." / "My bike is blue." / "I ride fast."
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited, conspiratorial)* Wheee — yes! I bet that is EXACTLY what it says! What if we played a game where YOU are the bike's voice? Like a Bike Whisperer!"
> 2. "*(encouraging)* You are right — bikes do not really talk. But what if we PRETENDED? You know your bike so well. I bet you can imagine what it would say. Want to try?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, playful whisper)* Hey — what if you had a magic power to hear what your bike is thinking? And you told ME what it says? Like a secret translator!"
>
> **Screen**: Bicycle photo centered with a soft blue-silver glow around the wheels, subtle "conversation recap" shimmer around the spokes. A small speech-bubble icon fades in near the handlebars.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs their bicycle in the garage with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted gasp)* Ohhh wow, a bicycle! Look at those shiny wheels and all those thin spokes — they look like a spiderweb! I bet this bike goes on so many adventures. If your bike could talk right now, what do you think it would say?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Let's go ride!" / "I'm fast!" / "Take me outside!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's my bike!" / "The tire is flat." / "I can ride it!"
> 3. (No response) Child watches the screen silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, impressed)* Ha! 'Let's go ride!' I love it! Your bike sounds like it is ready for action. You really understand what it is thinking. I bet you can hear even MORE of what it says."
> 2. "*(enthusiastic agreement)* It IS your bike! And you know it so well. I bet if your bike could talk, it would have SO much to say about all the places you have been together. Want to find out?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft, wondering tone)* This bike looks like it has great stories to tell. What if you had a magic power to hear it talk? Want to be a Bike Whisperer with me?"
>
> **Screen**: Bicycle photo fills the center of the screen. Gentle silver sparkle animation radiates outward from the wheel spokes. Tiny motion lines pulse behind the rear wheel, suggesting readiness to ride.

**Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo — "The Bike Whisperer Game"**

> **AI says**: "*(playful, conspiratorial tone)* Okay, here is how the Bike Whisperer game works! I tell you something that happens to your bike. And YOU talk like the bike — you say what it FEELS and what it SAYS! Watch — I will show you! If I say... 'your bike is parked in the garage all day, waiting' — hmm, what would the bike say? I think the bike would say... 'I am SO bored in here! When is my rider coming? I want to feel the wind in my spokes!' See? Now YOUR turn. Ready, Whisperer?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yeah!" / "Let's go!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "My bike is in the garage!" / Child giggles / "That's funny!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet, possibly processing.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited countdown)* Okay, Bike Whisperer — here comes the first adventure! Listen carefully..."
> 2. "*(delighted)* It IS funny — a talking bike! And you are the only one who can hear it. Here comes the first adventure!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging, gentle)* It is easy — I say what happens, and you talk like the bike. Say whatever you want! Here is the first one..."
>
> **Screen**: The bicycle photo shifts to the left side of the screen. On the right, a cartoon speech-bubble icon appears with the text "Bike Whisperer" in playful letters. A simple animation shows: the bike in a garage with a thought bubble ("I'm bored!"), demonstrating the voice-acting pattern. A "Ride 1" badge glows softly below.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction (3–5 rounds)**

**Round 1: Zooming Downhill** *(Simple, obvious emotion — build confidence)*

> **AI says**: "*(bright narrator tone)* Adventure number one! Imagine... you hop on your bike and ride down a big hill. The wheels spin faster and faster. The wind rushes past. What does the bike say? Talk like the bike!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Wheee! I'm going so fast!" / "My wheels are spinning!" / "This is fun!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Vroom vroom!" / "I like going fast!" (speaks as self, not bike) / "Zoom!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet or looking at the photo.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* 'Wheee!' — I KNEW it! The bike is loving that speed! Those wheels are spinning so fast, and the chain goes click-click-click. Going downhill makes the bike feel FREE and excited! You are a great Bike Whisperer!"
> 2. "*(warm validation)* Vroom vroom — yes! The bike is zooming! And I bet the bike is saying, 'My wheels are spinning so fast! I love going downhill — click click click goes my chain!' The hill makes the bike feel excited. Great job!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(helpful, encouraging)* The bike is flying downhill — wind rushing past, wheels spinning fast! Is the bike feeling happy or scared? What would it shout?"
>
> **Screen**: Animation shows the bicycle rolling down a gentle green hill. Wind lines stream past. The spokes blur with speed. When the child responds, a big speech bubble appears over the bike with the child's words (or a cheerful face icon). A small "RIDE 1 ✓" stamp appears in the corner. A golden star sparkles beside it.

**Round 2: A Flat Tire** *(Contrasting emotion — introduces surprise and frustration)*

> **AI says**: "*(slightly dramatic whisper)* Adventure number two! Oh no... you are riding along and suddenly — psssshh! The front tire goes flat. The bike wobbles and slows down. What does the bike say now?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ouch! My tire!" / "Oh no, I can't roll!" / "Help me, I'm hurt!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "That happened to me!" / "Get a pump!" / "It's broken!" (speaks as self)
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet, possibly unsure.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(empathetic, impressed)* 'Ouch, my tire!' Aww, poor bike! When the tire goes flat, the bike cannot roll. The tire's job — its Function — is to be round and full of air so it can spin. Without air, the bike feels stuck and sad. You really feel what the bike feels!"
> 2. "*(gentle, validating)* A flat tire IS no fun! And I bet the bike would say, 'Oh no, my tire! I cannot roll anymore. I need air!' The tire has a special job — to stay round and bouncy. That is its Function. When it goes flat, the bike needs help."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(playful hint)* Psssshh — the air is coming out! The bike was rolling along and now it is wobbling. Is the bike feeling happy... or is it saying 'uh oh!'?"
>
> **Screen**: The bicycle illustration is on a path. One tire deflates with a "psssshh" animation — air whooshes out. The bike wobbles and tilts. When the child responds, a sad-face speech bubble appears over the bike. A small diagram highlights the tire with the word "air" and an arrow. "RIDE 2 ✓" stamp appears.

**Round 3: Discovering a New Path** *(Surprising, exciting — peak engagement)*

> **AI says**: "*(curious, playful tone)* Adventure number three — this one is exciting! You are riding your usual way to the park, but today you spot a path you have NEVER taken before. It goes through some trees and around a little pond. What does the bike say about the new path?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Let's go there! A new adventure!" / "I'm a little scared but excited!" / "Ooh what's down there?"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I go to the park!" / "There are trees!" / "I like ponds!"
> 3. (No response) Child is thinking or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* 'A new adventure!' — the bike is SO curious! It has never rolled on this path before. The pedals push forward, the wheels crunch on new ground, and suddenly the bike connects you to a whole new place you have never seen! That is what bikes do — they connect you to new places. Amazing whispering!"
> 2. "*(agreeing warmly)* Trees and ponds — sounds beautiful! And the bike would say, 'Ooh, new ground under my wheels! Where are we going?' Every new path the bike takes connects the rider to a brand new part of the neighborhood. That is pretty magical!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enticing whisper)* A path the bike has never taken! Is the bike feeling curious and brave... or is it saying, 'Hmm, let's turn back'? What do YOU think?"
>
> **Screen**: The bicycle illustration is at a fork in a path. One way goes to a familiar park, the other winds through illustrated trees toward a small pond. When the child responds, the bike rolls down the new path. Trees part to reveal the pond, and a discovery sparkle animation plays. A speech bubble with the child's words appears. "RIDE 3 ✓" stamp appears with an extra sparkle effect.

**Round 4 (if child is engaged): Waiting in the Rain** *(Mixed emotion — child leads)*

> **AI says**: "*(soft, wondering tone)* Bonus adventure! What if... it starts to rain. Big drops are falling. You have to run inside. The bike is parked outside, all alone, getting wet. What does the bike say?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Come back! I'm getting wet!" / "I'm cold and lonely." / "The rain is dripping on me!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Put it inside!" / "I don't like rain!" (speaks as self) / "Splash splash!"
> 3. (No response) Child may be getting tired or losing focus.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(gentle, impressed)* 'I'm getting wet!' Aww, the bike sounds lonely! The rain drips on its frame and the chain gets a little rusty. But maybe the bike also feels a tiny bit brave — 'I can handle the rain! My rider will come back.' You gave the bike MIXED feelings — lonely AND brave. That is really special whispering."
> 2. "*(validating warmly)* That is so thoughtful — putting it inside! And the bike would say, 'Thank you! My metal frame gets cold in the rain, and my chain does not like getting too wet.' The bike is a little sad but also knows its rider cares about it. That is Connection — the bike and its rider look out for each other!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(cozy whisper)* The rain is falling... drip drip on the handlebars. Is the bike feeling lonely? Or brave? Or both? What would it whisper to itself?"
>
> **Screen**: The bicycle illustration is outside in gentle rain. Blue raindrops fall on the frame and handlebars. A small puddle forms below. When the child responds, a thought bubble appears over the bike with the child's words. The scene is soft and moody — blue tones. "BONUS RIDE ✓" stamp glows faintly in gold.

**Step 4: Celebration + Role Title**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* WOW! You heard your bike on every single adventure! You felt its excitement going downhill, its ouchie with a flat tire, its curiosity on a new path, and its loneliness in the rain. You are officially a... BIKE WHISPERER! You gave your bike a voice, and that takes real heart!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yay!" / "I'm a Bike Whisperer!" / Giggles or cheers.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can we ride for real?" / "I want more adventures!" / "My bike is the best."
> 3. (No response) Child smiles or looks pleased.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming)* The BEST Bike Whisperer! Your bike is so lucky to have someone who really listens to it."
> 2. "*(delighted)* You want more adventures? That means you REALLY love your bike! We can play again another day. For now, let me tell you something amazing about what you just did..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm whisper)* You know what? I think your bike felt all those feelings with you. You really understand it."
>
> **Screen**: A golden badge animation fills the center of the screen with the text "BIKE WHISPERER" in bold, playful letters. The bicycle photo appears inside the badge like a portrait. Confetti bursts outward in silver and blue. A cheerful chime sound plays.

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective pride)* You know what you did today? You showed me that every part of your bike has a job — the wheels roll, the pedals push, the brakes stop, the chain clicks. That is called Function — how each part works! And you showed me that your bike takes you to the park, down new paths, and through the neighborhood — it connects you to all those places and people. That is called Connection — how things are linked together! You are not just a whisperer. You are a true bike friend."
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Function!" / "Connection!" / "I'm a bike friend!" / Repeats a word happily.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can I go ride now?" / "Bye bike!"
> 3. (No response) Child listens or looks at the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebrating)* That is right — Function and Connection! You earned your badge, Bike Whisperer. Next time you ride, listen close — maybe you will hear what your bike is saying!"
> 2. "*(warm, laughing)* Go ride! Your bike has been waiting all day. And now you know all its secrets. See you next time, Bike Whisperer!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle closing)* Great job today, Whisperer. Your bike has a really good friend. See you next time!"
>
> **Screen**: The Bike Whisperer badge remains centered. Below it, the words **"Function"** and **"Connection"** appear one at a time in elegant, silver-blue lettering — each word accompanied by a small icon (a gear for Function, a chain-link for Connection). The bicycle photo glows warmly behind the text. Soft sparkle animations surround the concept words. After 3 seconds, a gentle "The End" ribbon scrolls across the bottom.
