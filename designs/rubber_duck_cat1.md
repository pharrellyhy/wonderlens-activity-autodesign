## Activity: Ducky's Big Splash (小鸭大冒险)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: Ducky's Big Splash (小鸭大冒险)
- **Activity Category**: 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device, Indoor, Quiet)
- **Recommended Tier**: T0 (ages 2–4)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Perspective** (What are the points of view?) & **Connection** (How is it connected to other things?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Expression, Imagination, Environment, Role
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Communication Skills (listening, expressing), Thinking Skills (creative thinking), Self-Management Skills (emotional regulation)
- **Trigger Entity**: Rubber duck
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a rubber duck sitting in the bathtub during bath time.

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After photographing a rubber duck in the bathtub, the AI gasps with delight and imagines the duck wants to go on water adventures. The child becomes the duck's "voice" — making duck sounds and saying what the duck feels as the AI describes different water places (bathtub, rain puddle, ocean wave). Each round is a new splash-filled mini-scene where the child speaks for the duck.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the words "splash," "puddle," "float," "wave," and "rain." Learn that ducks float on water.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that the same duck can feel DIFFERENT things in different places — that's **Perspective**. Understand that water **Connects** many different places in the world (bathtub, puddle, ocean, rain).
  - **D (Do)**: Practice expressive vocalization and imaginative role-play (Communication Skills — expressing), generate creative ideas about what a character feels (Thinking Skills — creative thinking), and stay engaged across multiple imaginative scenarios (Self-Management Skills — focus).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Ducky's Voice" metaphor turns bath time into a magical adventure. The child doesn't just describe — they BECOME the duck's voice, making splashes, quacks, and exclamations. Each round transports the duck to a new water world (warm bath → rainy puddle → big ocean wave), making familiar water feel fresh and surprising. The escalation from cozy to wild keeps T0 children giggling and engaged.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a rubber duck in the bathtub → AI imagines the duck can talk → child becomes the duck's voice across water adventures.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T0 (ages 2–4)]

**Step 1: Transition Bridge**

> **AI says**: "*(delighted gasp)* QUACK QUACK! A duck! A little yellow duck! Is ducky having a bath?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes!" / "Ducky bath!" / "It's swimming!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's yellow!" / "My duck!" / "Quack!"
> 3. (No response) Looks at the screen or splashes in the tub.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm and amazed)* Ducky loves baths! SPLASH SPLASH! I think ducky wants to talk. Can YOU be ducky's voice?"
> 2. "*(agreeing happily)* Yes! YOUR duck! QUACK QUACK! Ducky looks so happy. I think ducky wants to talk. Can YOU say what ducky says?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(playful whisper)* I think ducky said... QUACK! Can you quack like ducky?"
>
> **Screen**: The rubber duck photo is centered on screen. Gentle blue water ripple animations radiate outward from behind the duck. Small bubble animations float upward around the edges.

**Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, playful)* Let's play! I tell a story. You be ducky's voice! Like this — ducky sees warm water... I say OOOH NICE! SPLASH! Now you try! What does ducky say?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "SPLASH!" / "Quack quack!" / "Ooh warm!" / Makes a sound.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know." / "Ducky?" / Repeats a different word.
> 3. (No response) Watches the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* YES! SPLASH! You sound just like ducky! Okay — ducky's first adventure is here!"
> 2. "*(encouraging gently)* That's okay! Just go QUACK! Or SPLASH! Any sound ducky makes! Let's try together — QUACK QUACK!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(silly voice)* QUAAACK! Can you do that? QUACK! Just try! Any ducky sound!"
>
> **Screen**: A simple icon sequence appears: a speech bubble with "SPLASH!" next to the duck, then a microphone icon with a sparkle, showing it's the child's turn. The duck photo gently bobs up and down like floating on water.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction (2 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — Warm Bath (simple, cozy, confidence-builder):**
>
> **AI says**: "*(cozy, warm voice)* Ducky is in warm water. So warm! So nice! What does ducky say?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ahhh!" / "Nice!" / "QUACK!" / "Warm!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Ducky swim!" / "Bubble!" / Something off-topic.
> 3. (No response) Silent or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cozy)* AAAHH! Ducky says AAAHH! So cozy! Ducky loves warm water. SPLASH SPLASH! Okay — next place!"
> 2. "*(validating)* Ducky swims! Yes! SPLISH SPLOSH! Ducky is SO happy in warm water! Great ducky voice! Next place!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(softly)* Maybe ducky says... AAAHH! So warm! Can you say AAAHH for ducky?"
>
> **Screen**: The background shifts to a warm orange-yellow glow around the duck. Steam wisps animate gently above the water. Small heart icons float up when the child responds.

> **Round 2 — Rainy Puddle (contrasting, playful):**
>
> **AI says**: "*(surprised voice)* OH! Now ducky is outside! In a puddle! Rain goes DRIP DROP! What does ducky say?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Drip drop!" / "Wet!" / "QUACK!" / "Cold!" / "Wheee!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "No rain!" / "Ducky go home!" / Something unrelated.
> 3. (No response) Silent.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(giggling)* DRIP DROP! Ducky says DRIP DROP! Rain tickles ducky's head! Hee hee! That's SO funny! One more place!"
> 2. "*(playfully)* Ducky wants to go home? Oh! Maybe ducky says BRRR! IT'S WET! Silly rain! That's a great ducky voice! One more place!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(making rain sounds)* DRIP DROP DRIP! Does ducky say BRRR? Or WHEEE? You pick!"
>
> **Screen**: Background shifts to grey-blue with animated raindrops falling. The duck photo has tiny animated rain splashes around it. Puddle ripple effects spread across the lower part of the screen.

> **Round 3 (optional, if child is engaged) — Big Ocean Wave (exciting, peak fun):**
>
> **AI says**: "*(big, thrilled voice)* WHOOOOOSH! A BIG wave! Ducky's on the ocean! UP and DOWN! What does ducky say?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "WHOOOOSH!" / "Wheee!" / "Big wave!" / "Help!" / Giggles.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Ducky scared!" / "Shark!" / Something surprising.
> 3. (No response) Watches with wide eyes.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* WHEEEEE! Ducky says WHEEEEE! UP on the wave! SPLAAASH down! Ducky is SO brave! What a ride!"
> 2. "*(validating with wonder)* Ducky is scared? AAAH! BIG WAVE! That's okay — even brave duckies get scared sometimes! HOLD ON DUCKY! Great voice!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(dramatic)* The wave goes WHOOOOSH! Maybe ducky says WHEEE! Or AAAH! Can you be ducky? WHOOOOSH!"
>
> **Screen**: Background becomes deep blue with a large animated wave that lifts the duck photo up and down in a gentle rocking motion. White foam animations splash at the wave crest. Stars and water sparkle effects play when the child responds.

> **Rounds 4–5 (optional, if child is engaged):**
>
> Round 4 — Gentle Pond: "*(peaceful voice)* Shhhh. Now ducky is on a quiet pond. So still. Shhh. What does ducky whisper?" (Targets: soft voice, calm emotion — contrast with ocean excitement)
>
> Round 5 — Magical Waterfall: "*(amazed voice)* WOW! A waterfall! Water goes ROAAAAR! What does ducky shout?" (Targets: loud expression, exhilaration — child-led open ending)

**Step 4: Celebration + Role Title**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* YAY! You did it! You are Ducky's Best Voice! You made ducky talk! QUACK QUACK HOORAY!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Laughs, says "Quack!", claps, or cheers.
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or says nothing.
> 3. (Wants more) "Again!" / "More ducky!"
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebrating)* QUACK QUACK HOORAY! Best ducky voice ever! You get a special badge!"
> 2. "*(warmly)* You were SO good! Ducky is lucky to have YOUR voice! Here's your badge!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(delighted)* You love being ducky's voice! Next time, ducky can go to even MORE places! But first — your badge!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden badge animation assembles on screen: a rubber duck wearing a tiny crown, with the text "Ducky's Best Voice" curving beneath it. Confetti bursts from both sides. A cheerful celebration sound plays.

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, gentle pride)* Ducky felt so many things! Happy, silly, brave! One ducky, many feelings. That's **Perspective**! And water is everywhere — bath, puddle, ocean! Water **connects** them all! QUACK QUACK bye bye!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) "Bye ducky!" / "Quack quack!" / Waves.
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or looks at the badge.
> 3. (No response) Child watches the ripple animation quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(happy)* Bye bye, Ducky's Best Voice! See you next splash time!"
> 2. "*(softly)* Your badge is saved! Bye bye, little duck friend!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle)* Bye bye, little duck friend. See you next splash time!"
>
> **Screen**: The badge shrinks to the top corner. The words **"Perspective"** and **"Connection"** appear in the center — "Perspective" is styled with the letters each tinted a different pastel color (representing different feelings), and "Connection" is styled with tiny animated water droplets linking the letters together. The rubber duck photo fades gently with a final ripple animation. Soft closing chime plays.
