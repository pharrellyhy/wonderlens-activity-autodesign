## Activity: The Wish Puff Safari

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Wish Puff Safari
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Form** (What is it like?) & **Connection** (How is it connected to other things?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Structure, Diversity, Nature, Discovery
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, collecting and recording data), Thinking Skills (creative thinking — comparing and classifying), Self-Management Skills (focus — sustained search across a space)
- **Trigger Entity**: Dandelion
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a dandelion in the grass at the park.

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After photographing a dandelion, the AI marvels at its puffy, round seed head and wonders what OTHER soft, round, or fluffy things might be hiding in the park. The child becomes a "Wish Puff Scout" on a safari to find 3 things that share the dandelion's softness or roundness. Each find gets a poetic "Safari Log" name, and at the end the child sorts the whole collection into two camps — "the Round Team" and "the Fluffy Team" — discovering that some things belong to both.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the vocabulary "fluffy," "round," "seed head," "puff," "texture." Learn that a dandelion's white ball is made of tiny seeds, each attached to its own parachute for flying on the wind.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that the same visual **Form** (round or fluffy shape) shows up across very different things in nature, and that sorting objects by shared features reveals surprising **Connections** between unrelated things.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice close observation of shape and texture in nature (Research Skills — observation), compare and classify objects by visual properties (Thinking Skills — creative thinking), and maintain focused exploration across a physical space (Self-Management Skills — focus).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Wish Puff Safari" metaphor frames the dandelion's seed head as a magical "wish puff" — something a child already associates with blowing wishes. This emotional anchor transforms a classification exercise into a treasure hunt for more "wish-worthy" things. The twist comes at synthesis: when the child sorts finds into Round vs. Fluffy, they discover that some things are BOTH — the dandelion itself lives in the overlap, just like in nature, where categories are never perfectly neat.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a dandelion puff ball in the park grass → AI notices how round and fluffy it is → launches a "Wish Puff Safari" to find 3 more round or fluffy things → child sorts the whole set into Round vs. Fluffy teams.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1 (ages 4–6)]

**Step 1: Transition Bridge**

> **AI says**: "*(breathless wonder)* Ohhh — a dandelion puff! Look at that — it's like a tiny cloud sitting on a stick! If you blew on it, all those little seeds would fly away like wishes. Have you ever blown on one of these?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes! They fly everywhere!" / "I blew one before!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's a flower!" / "I want to pick it!"
> 3. (No response) Looks at the dandelion or the screen silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* They really DO fly everywhere — whoooosh! Each little floaty piece is a tiny seed with its own parachute. This puff ball is so round and so soft. I wonder... is it the only fluffy, round thing hiding in this park?"
> 2. "*(warmly agreeing)* It IS a flower — a very special one! When it's done being yellow, it turns into this amazing puff ball full of tiny flying seeds. Feel how round and soft it looks! I wonder if there are OTHER round, soft things nearby..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft, inviting whisper)* See how round and fluffy it is? Like a teeny tiny cloud. Each piece is a seed that can fly! I have a really fun idea — what if we went looking for MORE soft, round things like this?"
>
> **Screen**: Close-up of the dandelion photo, with the seed head area surrounded by a gentle glowing halo. Tiny animated seed wisps drift slowly off the edges. A small "Puff Counter" icon (showing "1") appears in the bottom corner.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(adventurous, explorer tone)* Alright — you are now an official Wish Puff Scout! Your safari mission has three parts. Part one: find 3 more things in this park that are round OR fluffy — like the dandelion puff. Part two: take a photo of each one. Part three: at the end, we sort them all into teams — the Round Team and the Fluffy Team! Ready to start your safari, Scout?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yes!" / "Let's go!"
> 2. (Uncertain) "What do you mean fluffy?" / "Where should I look?"
> 3. (No response) Glances around the park.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* Safari is GO! Find something round or soft, take a photo, and report back to me. I'll be right here, Scout!"
> 2. "*(encouraging)* Fluffy means soft and puffy — like the dandelion, or like a fuzzy caterpillar, or a cottony bit on a plant. Try looking down in the grass or up close at bushes. Soft, round things are great at hiding!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gently)* Here's your first clue — look low, near the ground. Grass, clover, little mushrooms — round things love hiding close to the dirt! Go explore and snap a photo when you spot one!"
>
> **Screen**: A safari-themed "mission card" appears: a badge outline labeled "Wish Puff Scout" with a dandelion silhouette. Below it, 3 empty circle-shaped slots (to be filled as the child finds things). The dandelion photo sits above the slots with a golden check. A numbered task list with small icons (magnifying glass, camera, sorting arrows) shows the three parts.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — First Find:**
>
> *(Child photographs something — e.g., a round clover flower head)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(eager)* Safari log incoming! Oh, what is THAT? Tell me — is it round, fluffy, or both?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's a clover! It's round and kind of fluffy!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "A plant." (minimal description)
> 3. (No response) Just takes the photo.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* A clover flower — round AND a little fluffy! Look at all those tiny petals bunched together into a ball. Safari Log: 'Clover Pom-Pom — round and soft!' Amazing first find, Scout. Two more to go!"
> 2. "*(looking closely)* A plant! And look at the top part — see how it makes a little ball shape? All those tiny pieces poking out make it look puffy too! Safari Log: 'Mystery Puff Plant!' Great eye, Scout. Two more to find!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enthusiastic)* Oooh, let me look! I see something with a round shape at the top — it looks soft and bunched up. What does it feel like? Is it smooth or fuzzy? Tell me what YOU notice!"
>
> **Screen**: The new photo slides into the first empty slot on the mission card. A brief burst of golden sparkles. The slot gets a check. Counter updates to "2 of 4." A tiny label appears under the photo: awaiting the child's name or the AI's Safari Log name.

> **Round 2 — Second Find:**
>
> *(Child photographs something — e.g., a fuzzy caterpillar or a round mushroom cap)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(detective voice)* Report number two! What did your scout eyes spot this time?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A mushroom! It's really round on top!"
> 2. (Stretch) "I found a stick." (item doesn't clearly match round or fluffy)
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* A mushroom — like a little umbrella! That cap is perfectly round. And if you touched the top, it might feel smooth or a tiny bit velvety. Safari Log: 'Umbrella Cap — smooth and round!' One more find, Scout — you're almost there!"
> 2. "*(thoughtful)* A stick! Hmm, let me look closer... does it have any fuzzy bits on it? Sometimes sticks have soft moss or round bumps growing on them. Even a tiny round knot counts! Safari Log: 'Knobbly Stick — secret round bits!' One more to go!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(curious)* I see your photo! I think I can spot something interesting. Is any part of it round? Or does any part feel soft? You're the scout — tell me what you notice about its shape!"
>
> **Screen**: Second slot fills in. Counter updates to "3 of 4." A progress path (dotted safari trail) animation shows the scout almost at the finish flag.

> **Round 3 — Third Find (with stuck branch):**
>
> **STUCK BRANCH** *(if child has been searching for more than a minute without finding something)*:
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful whisper)* Scout tip! Try looking RIGHT at your feet in the grass — clover heads, tiny mushrooms, and seed puffs love hiding in short grass. Or peek at a bush — sometimes the berries or buds are perfectly round! Even a round pebble counts!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I see one!" / Child spots something near the grass or bush.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I still can't find anything." / "There's nothing here."
> 3. (No response) Child keeps wandering without choosing a spot.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Great spotting! Go take a photo of it, Scout — let's see your final safari find!"
> 2. "*(reassuring)* That's okay! Try the edge of the path or right under a bush. Tiny round pebbles and fluffy grass tips count too!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Let's slow down and look low. Do you see anything round near your shoes, or anything soft near the grass?"
>
> *(Child photographs final find — e.g., a round seed pod or a fluffy grass plume)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(triumphant)* FINAL SAFARI FIND! Tell me about this one — what makes it round or fluffy?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's a fuzzy grass thing! It's really soft!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know what it is."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* A fuzzy grass plume — nature's own feather duster! That is SO fluffy. Safari Log: 'Grass Feather — extra fluffy!' Scout, your safari collection is COMPLETE!"
> 2. "*(excited anyway)* That's okay — scouts find mystery things all the time! Whatever it is, I can see it has a soft, feathery look. Safari Log: 'Mystery Fluff!' Your collection is done, Scout!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enthusiastic)* Look at that — I can see something soft and light in your photo! It reminds me of a little feather. Safari Log: 'Park Fluff — soft and wispy!' Collection complete, Scout!"
>
> **Screen**: Final slot fills in with a celebration animation — all 4 photos light up with golden borders. A small fireworks burst. The safari trail reaches the finish flag. Badge starts assembling.

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud and excited)* Okay, Wish Puff Scout — look at everything you found! A dandelion puff, a [first find], a [second find], and a [third find]. Now here's the fun part — let's sort them! Which ones go on the Round Team, and which ones go on the Fluffy Team? Can any of them be on BOTH teams?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The mushroom is round! The grass is fluffy! The dandelion is both!" / Child sorts them.
> 2. (Unexpected) "They're all fluffy!" / "I don't know which team."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(mind-blown)* Wait — the dandelion is on BOTH teams? You're right! It's round AND fluffy at the same time! That's such a smart discovery. Some things don't fit in just one group — they surprise you by being both!"
> 2. "*(playfully)* All fluffy? That's one way to see it! But look at the [mushroom/round item] — is it more ROUND like a ball, or more SOFT like a pillow? Sometimes things are round but not fluffy. And the dandelion — it's round AND fluffy! It's a double agent on BOTH teams!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(guiding gently)* Here, let me help! If something is shaped like a ball — that's the Round Team. If something is soft and wispy — that's the Fluffy Team. And guess what? Some things can play for BOTH teams! Which one do you think does that?"
>
> **Screen**: All 4 photos displayed in a row at the top. Below, two labeled zones appear: "Round Team" (with a circle icon) on the left, "Fluffy Team" (with a cloud icon) on the right, and a small overlapping zone in the middle labeled "Both!" with a star. Animated arrows suggest sorting. As the AI discusses each item, the corresponding photo gently bounces. After the conversation, photos slide into their team zones, with the dandelion landing in the overlapping center.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(amazed and warm)* Scout, you found FOUR different things in this park — a puff ball, a flower, a [item], and a [item] — and they ALL share something: round shapes or soft textures! But they're all SO different from each other. Why do you think so many different things in nature end up round or fluffy?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Because it's a good shape!" / "So the wind can blow them!" / Any creative reasoning.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* That's such a great thought! You're onto something — round shapes help things roll, bounce, and spread. And fluffy things can catch the wind and travel far, just like dandelion seeds! Nature figured out that round and fluffy are really useful shapes!"
> 2. "*(warmly)* It IS a big mystery! Here's a clue — the dandelion is fluffy so the wind can carry its seeds far away to grow new flowers. And round shapes help mushrooms push up through the dirt. Nature picks shapes that HELP things grow and move!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(sharing a secret)* I'll give you one clue — the dandelion's fluff catches the wind, so its seeds fly away and plant new flowers everywhere. Fluffy is like having a tiny parachute! Nature is really clever with shapes."
>
> **Screen**: The sorted collection is displayed beautifully — Round Team on the left, Fluffy Team on the right, "Both" items centered with a glowing star. Gentle animated lines connect items that share features. A soft wind animation blows tiny seed wisps across the screen.

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* Congratulations, Wish Puff Scout! You did something really special today. You looked closely at the **Form** of things — their shapes, their textures, whether they're round or fluffy or both. And you discovered an amazing **Connection** — that a tiny dandelion puff, a flower, and a [find] can all be part of the same team, even though they look nothing alike at first! You earned your Wish Puff Safari Badge!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) Cheers, talks about wanting to find more, or asks about the badge.
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or says nothing.
> 3. (No response) Child watches the drifting seeds quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Next time you're outside, keep your scout eyes ON — round and fluffy things are hiding everywhere! Under bushes, in flower beds, even on top of fences. See you on the next safari, Scout!"
> 2. "*(warm)* Your badge is saved! And remember — whenever you spot a new round or fluffy thing, you can always tell me about it. Bye for now, Scout!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft)* Your scout badge is shining. Bye for now, Scout!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden "Wish Puff Safari Badge" appears — circular, with a dandelion puff silhouette at the center and the 4 collection photos as small insets around the edges. The words **"Form"** and **"Connection"** float up artistically — "Form" styled with round, bubbly letters filled with a fluffy texture, "Connection" with gentle dotted lines linking the letters. A soft chime plays. Tiny animated seeds drift across the screen and settle.
