## The Shore Detective Agency

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Activity Name | The Shore Detective Agency |

### B. Activity Overview

**① Brief Description**

After photographing the sandy shore, the AI notices the mix of tiny treasures hidden in the sand — sparkly grains, dark shell bits, and smooth pebble pieces — and wonders WHO or WHAT delivered them there. The child becomes a "Shore Detective" on a mission to find and photograph 3 beach clues (things the ocean left behind), figure out where each one came from, and then piece together the story of how the ocean delivers gifts to the shore.

**② Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Learn that sand has tiny shell pieces mixed in. Learn that wet sand looks darker than dry sand. Learn the words "shore," "clue," "washed up," and "tide." Learn that waves carry things from the ocean to the beach.
- **U (Understand):** Understand that things found on the beach got there because waves and tides carried them — each item has a CAUSE for being there (Causation). Understand that the ocean, sand, shells, and seaweed are all linked — they share a Connection as parts of one beach system.
- **D (Do):** Practice close observation by spotting small objects mixed into the sand (Research Skills — observation). Practice collecting evidence and describing where it came from (Research Skills — data collection). Practice cause-and-effect reasoning by explaining WHY each clue ended up on the shore (Thinking Skills — critical thinking).

**③ Design Highlight**

The "Shore Detective" metaphor reframes beachcombing as a mystery investigation. Every shell, bit of seaweed, or smooth pebble is a "clue" the ocean left behind. The child is not just collecting — they are solving the mystery of how the ocean delivers things to the shore. The synthesis asks the child to connect ALL the clues into one ocean delivery story, turning science into narrative.

**④ Typical Scenario**

Child photographs the sandy shore → AI spots tiny treasures in the sand → child becomes a "Shore Detective" → finds and photographs 3 ocean clues → explains where each came from → tells the full story of the ocean's deliveries → celebrates as a Shore Detective.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1 (ages 4–6)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (delighted, amazed) "Wow, look at this sandy shore! I see so many tiny treasures hiding in the sand — sparkly bits, little shell pieces, smooth spots. It is like the ocean left presents on the beach! What do you see in the sand?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I see shells!" / "There are little shiny things!"
2. (Unexpected) "It is just sand" / "I see water"
3. (No response) Child is looking around or distracted.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (impressed) "Great eyes! Those shells did not just appear. The ocean waves washed them right onto the sand. I wonder what else the ocean left here!"
2. (warm, curious) "Sand is pretty cool, right? But look really close. Wet sand looks darker than dry sand. And there are tiny pieces mixed in. The ocean hides its gifts well!"
3. (wait 2s) (playful whisper) "Psst — look really close at the sand. See any tiny bits that are not just sand grains?"

**Screen:** Child's beach photo centered with gentle wave animation along the bottom edge and small sparkle effects on sand highlights.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (adventurous, confident) "I have a mission for you! You are now a Shore Detective! Your job is to solve the mystery of what the ocean left behind."

(counting on fingers) "Here is your mission: First, find three things the ocean washed up on the beach. Second, take a photo of each one. Third, tell me WHERE you think it came from!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay!" / "I want to find clues!"
2. (Unexpected) "What should I look for?" / "I do not know what to find"
3. (No response) Child seems unsure or hesitant.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (pumped up) "Let us go, Detective! Walk along the sand and look for shells, seaweed, smooth stones, or driftwood. When you spot a clue, take a photo!"
2. (helpful, warm) "Great question! Look for things that do not look like plain sand. Shells, seaweed, little sticks, or smooth pebbles. They are all ocean clues! Go explore!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, encouraging) "Try walking along the wet sand near the water. That is where the waves drop the best clues. You will find something!"

**Screen:** Mission card with a detective badge labeled "Shore Detective," four slots (first filled with beach photo, three empty with question marks and magnifying glass icons), and a numbered task list.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1:**

*(Child takes a photo of something they found — e.g., a seashell)*

**AI says:** (thrilled, detective voice) "Clue number one! Ooh, that looks smooth and curved. What did you find, Detective? Tell me about it!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "A shell!" / "It is a seashell! It is white."
2. (Unexpected) "A rock" / "I do not know what it is"
3. (No response) Child just took the photo silently.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (amazed) "A shell, yes! Shells come from animals that live in the ocean. The waves washed this one right onto the sand. Where do you think it lived before? In the deep water or close to shore?"
2. (warm, validating) "Nice find! It does look rocky. I think it might be a piece of shell that the waves smoothed down. The ocean is like a big tumbler — it rolls things around until they get smooth. That is one clue solved!"
3. (encouraging) "I see something smooth and light colored. It looks like it has been tumbled by the waves! Is it hard or soft when you touch it?"

**Screen:** Photo slides into slot 2 with a bubble celebration burst; counter updates to "1 of 3 clues found."

**Round 2 — "Seaweed Find":** Child photographs another item (e.g., seaweed); AI identifies it as something the waves tore loose and carried to shore, comparing the tide to a delivery truck. Counter: "2 of 3."

**Round 3 — "Final Clue":** Child photographs a third item (e.g., a smooth pebble or driftwood); AI celebrates completion and explains how the ocean rolled and polished it over a long time. Counter: "3 of 3 — Complete!"

**STUCK BRANCH:** AI suggests walking to where wet sand meets dry sand — that line is where waves drop things — and looking for shells, sticks, smooth stones, or seaweed.

#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (proud, gathering-the-evidence) "All three clues are in! Look at them together, Detective. You found a [item 1], a [item 2], and a [item 3]. They all ended up right here on this beach."

(thoughtful, mystery-solving) "Now here is the big question — can you tell me the whole story? How did the ocean bring all these things to the shore?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "The waves pushed them!" / "The ocean carried them and dropped them on the sand!"
2. (Unexpected) "They were already here" / "I do not know" / offers a creative or fantastical explanation
3. (No response) Child looks at the collection silently.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (deeply impressed) "That is exactly right! The waves pick things up from the ocean floor, carry them in, and drop them on the sand when the tide goes out. Every single thing on this beach has a journey story!"
2. (warm, building) "I love that idea! Here is something cool — the waves are like delivery trucks. They pick up shells, seaweed, and stones from the ocean and push them onto the sand. The tide goes in and out, leaving presents behind every time!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, guiding) "Think about the waves. They come in and go out, right? Each time, they can carry something and leave it on the sand. That is how your clues got here — the ocean delivered them!"

**Screen:** All four photos in a connected timeline layout with animated wave lines flowing left to right and "delivery" arrows connecting each item.

**AI says:** (warm, celebratory) "You are an amazing Shore Detective! You found three ocean clues and solved the mystery. Here is one more thing to wonder about — why do you think some things wash up near the water, and some end up farther up the beach?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Big waves push things farther!" / "The tide goes up high sometimes!"
2. (Unexpected) "I do not know" / "Maybe someone moved them"
3. (No response) Child looks at the beach scene quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (amazed) "You figured it out! Bigger waves push things farther up. And when the tide is high, water reaches way up the beach and leaves things there. You really think like a detective!"
2. (warm, encouraging) "That is a fun guess! The answer is the waves — sometimes they are big and strong and push things way up the sand. Sometimes they are small and only leave things near the water. The beach is always changing!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, guiding) "Look at the waterline. Some waves stop close to the water, and some reach much farther up the sand. That is why clues land in different places."

**Screen:** Collection displayed as a beach scene with items at different distances from an animated waterline, with dotted arrows showing wave paths.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (proud, celebrating) "What an incredible Shore Detective you are! You found ocean clues, figured out where they came from, and solved the mystery of the beach."

(warm, naming concepts naturally) "You discovered that everything on this beach has a reason — the waves CAUSED each shell and pebble and piece of seaweed to land right here. That is the power of Causation — understanding WHY things happen! And you saw how the ocean, the sand, the shells, and the seaweed are all connected — part of one big beach family. That is Connection — seeing how things belong together. You earned your Shore Detective badge!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I did it!" / "I want to find more clues!" / "The beach has lots of stories!"
2. (Unexpected) "Can I keep my shell?" / "I want to go in the water."
3. (No response) Child smiles or looks at the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (proud, warm) "You sure did! Every time you visit a beach, you can be a detective again and look for new ocean stories."
2. (gentle, connecting) "Those are great ideas! Beaches are full of clues and adventures. Now you know how to spot them and wonder where they came from."
3. (wait 2s) (soft closing) "Your Shore Detective badge is shining. Great detective work today."

**Screen:** Detective badge spinning into center labeled "Shore Detective" with collection photos as insets; "Causation" and "Connection" in ocean-blue lettering with shell and wave icons; celebration sparkles.
