## Activity: The Sky-Light Brigade

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Sky-Light Brigade
- **Activity Category**: Category 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4-6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: Form, Connection
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Discovery, Pattern, Classification, Nature
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research (Observation, Data collection), Thinking (Creative thinking, Transfer), Self-Management (Organization)
- **Trigger Entity**: Feather
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a feather found on the playground

### B. Activity Overview

- **1. Brief Description**: After the child photographs a feather on the playground, the AI marvels at how light and delicate it is and wonders what else could ride the wind. The child becomes a "Sky-Light Captain" on a mission to recruit a brigade of light, floaty things by finding and photographing them around the playground. Once the brigade is assembled, the child names the team and invents its wind-powered superpower.
- **2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Feather, lightweight, float, breeze, texture — vocabulary for weight, air movement, and surface qualities
  - **U (Understand)**: Many different things share the property of being light enough to move in wind (Form); objects that look very different can share a hidden quality that connects them (Connection)
  - **D (Do)**: Observe and compare physical properties across objects; classify items by a shared criterion (lightness); synthesize findings into a creative concept (team name and power)
- **3. Design Highlight**: The feather becomes a "team captain" recruiting other lightweight things into a wind-powered brigade. The child physically explores the playground, judging lightness by look and touch, then names the whole team with a superpower — turning scientific observation into imaginative play.
- **4. Typical Scenario**: A 5-year-old finds a small feather on the playground, photographs it, then searches for dandelion fluff, a thin leaf, and a flower petal, photographing each one and deciding they are all light enough to join the brigade. She names them "The Cloud Flyers" with the power of "super floating."

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

---

**Step 1: Transition Bridge**

> **AI says**: "*(amazed, gentle)* Oh wow, look at this feather! It has such tiny soft lines. I bet if you blew on it, it would fly away! What do you think it feels like?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Soft!" / "It feels fluffy!" / "Really light!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "A bird dropped it!" / "It's white!" / names a color
> 3. (No response) Child is silent or examining the feather.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm)* Yes! So soft and light! Almost like a tiny cloud. I wonder what else is that light around here..."
> 2. "*(curious)* Ooh, you're right! A bird left this gift. And it's sooo light — feel how light it is!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(soft, inviting)* Try touching it gently. So soft! And so, so light — like a little piece of sky."
>
> **Screen**: Child's photo of the feather centered on screen. A soft glow highlights the fine barb lines of the feather. Gentle breeze animation with tiny floating particles drifting slowly across the background.

---

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, conspiratorial)* You know what? This feather is SO light, it could float on the wind! What if we made a team of light things? You could be the Sky-Light Captain! Here is your mission: Find 3 more things nearby that are super light — things the wind could carry. Take a photo of each one. Then we will name your whole team!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Okay!" / "Yeah!" / "I want to find them!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What things?" / "Like what?" / child seems unsure
> 3. (No response) Child looks around but doesn't speak.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(pumped)* Let's go, Captain! Your feather is member number one. Find something light and take a photo!"
> 2. "*(helpful)* Great question! Think about things you could blow off your hand — like a leaf or a little petal. Go look around and photograph what you find!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* Look around the playground! Anything super light — maybe near the grass or under a tree. Take a photo when you find one!"
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears. At the top, a badge reads "Sky-Light Captain" with a small feather icon. Below it, 4 circular slots in a row — the first slot holds the child's feather photo, the other 3 are empty with dotted outlines and a "?" in each. A numbered checklist shows: 1. Find 3 light things, 2. Photograph each one, 3. Name your team.

---

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3-4 rounds)**

**Round 1 — First Find**

> *(Child takes a photo of something they found)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited discovery)* Ooh, look at that! Tell me — do you think the wind could pick this up?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yes! It's really light!" / "It could fly!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know" / "It's pretty!" / describes color or shape
> 3. (No response) Child is silent.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* Yes! That is super light! Welcome to the brigade! 2 more to go, Captain!"
> 2. "*(warm)* It IS pretty! And I think it looks light enough for the wind. That means it joins the team! 2 more to find!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* I see something interesting! It looks light to me. I think the wind could carry it. Great find, Captain! 2 more to go!"
>
> **Screen**: The new photo slides into the second slot with a gentle pop animation. A small wind-swirl icon appears beside it. The counter updates: "2 more to find!" A brief confetti burst celebrates the find.

**Round 2 — Second Find**

> *(Child takes another photo)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(curious)* Another one! Is this one lighter or heavier than the feather?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Lighter!" / "Heavier!" / "Same!" — child makes a comparison
> 2. (Unexpected) "I found it over there!" / describes where it was
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Great thinking! You compared them! This one joins the brigade too. Only 1 more, Captain!"
> 2. "*(interested)* Wow, good searching! And this looks nice and light. It's in the brigade now! Just 1 more to go!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(playful)* Hmm, I think this one looks pretty light! It's on the team. Just 1 more, Captain!"
>
> **Screen**: The third slot fills in. A faint dotted line connects the new find to the previous ones, hinting at their shared quality. Counter: "1 more to find!"

**Round 3 — Third Find**

> *(Child takes another photo)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(celebratory)* Yes! One more for the brigade! Does this one feel soft like the feather, or different?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It's soft too!" / "No, it's smooth!" / makes a texture comparison
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can I find more?" / "I like this one best!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* I love that you noticed! Every light thing feels a little different. Your brigade is complete!"
> 2. "*(warm)* I love your excitement! This one is great. And guess what — your brigade is all here now!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(cheerful)* Amazing find! Your brigade is now complete, Captain! All four are here!"
>
> **Screen**: The fourth slot fills in with a satisfying click animation. All four photos glow briefly in unison. A banner unfurls: "Brigade Complete!" Small wind-swirl particles float across all four images.

**STUCK BRANCH** *(if child can't find something light)*

> **AI says**: "*(helpful, friendly)* Hmm, let's think! Light things like to hide near the ground. Try looking in the grass for a tiny leaf or a petal. Or check under a tree — sometimes little seeds float down! Even a piece of dandelion fluff counts!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I found one!" / Child points to a petal, leaf, or fluff.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I still don't know." / "Nothing is light."
> 3. (No response) Child keeps scanning without moving closer.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Great eye, Captain! Go snap a photo and we'll welcome it into the brigade!"
> 2. "*(reassuring)* That's okay! Try the tiniest things first — a petal, a seed, or a skinny leaf. Even one tiny floaty thing counts."
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Let's look low near the grass, then under the tree. Do you see the smallest, floatiest thing nearby?"
>
> **Screen**: A simple illustrated map appears showing playground hints: an arrow pointing to "near grass," another to "under trees," and a third to "near flowers." Each label has a small icon (leaf, seed, petal).

---

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis (NAMING)**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, excited)* Captain, your Sky-Light Brigade is ready! Look at all four of them together. Now here is the fun part — what is your team's name? And what is their wind superpower?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The Cloud Flyers! They can super fly!" / "Wind Runners! They go really fast!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I don't know" / gives a single word / names just one item
> 3. (No response) Child is silent or looking at the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* The [team name]! With the power of [power]! That is the best brigade ever, Captain!"
> 2. "*(supportive)* Hmm, how about we start — are they flyers? Floaters? Spinners? Pick one and that's your team! What can they do when the wind blows?"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(playful)* What if they were called... the Breeze Buddies? Or YOU pick a name! What sound does the wind make when it carries them?"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos arranged in a diamond formation. A glowing text field at the top reads "Team Name: ___" and below the diamond, "Superpower: ___." When the child names them, the text fills in with playful handwriting-style font. Wind-trail animations connect all four photos in a swirl.

---

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, proud)* Wow, Captain! You built a whole brigade of light things! They all look so different — a feather, and three other finds. But they ALL share something. Why do you think some things are so light the wind can carry them?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Because they're small!" / "They're thin!" / "They don't weigh a lot!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I like the feather best!" / talks about a favorite find
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thoughtful)* That's such smart thinking! Light things are often thin or small. The wind can carry them on an adventure!"
> 2. "*(warm)* The feather is special! And it's amazing that you found other things that are light just like it. They are all connected!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle)* I think it's because they are thin and small. The wind can carry them like tiny travelers!"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos displayed in a row with gentle floating animation — each photo bobs slightly up and down as if riding a breeze. Thin animated wind lines flow between them, showing their shared quality. The team name glows above.

---

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(gentle, celebratory)* You are an amazing Sky-Light Captain! You looked closely at each thing you found — the shape, the softness, the tiny details. That's called exploring Form! And you discovered that a feather, and all your finds, share something special — being light. Finding what connects different things is called Connection! Great exploring today, Captain!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "That was fun!" / "Can I find more?" / child smiles or cheers
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to keep the feather!" / says something about one item
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheerful)* So much fun! You can always look for more light things. Keep exploring, Captain!"
> 2. "*(warm)* Yes, keep that feather safe! It started your whole brigade. Great job today, Captain!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(soft, kind)* You did such great work today. Your brigade is amazing. See you next time, Captain!"
>
> **Screen**: A badge animation appears: a golden shield with a feather icon and the text "Sky-Light Captain" in bold, friendly letters. The four collection photos appear as small insets around the badge. Below the badge, two concept words fade in artistically: "Form" with a magnifying-glass icon and "Connection" with a chain-link icon, both surrounded by soft wind-trail flourishes. After 3 seconds, gentle closing screen with the team name and "Great exploring today!" text.
