## The Brave Things Hunt

### A. Basic Info

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Activity Name | The Brave Things Hunt |
| Activity Category | Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device) |
| Recommended Tier | T0 (ages 2–4) |
| Core IB Key Concepts | Form, Function |
| Related Concepts | Adaptation, Habitat, Discovery, Growth |
| ATL Skills Focus | Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking) |
| Game Style | comparison_chart |

### B. Activity Overview

**① Brief Description**

After the child photographs a toy lion in the yard, the AI roars with excitement and wonders what else around here looks strong and brave like a lion. The child becomes a "Brave Things Scout" — searching the yard for 2 things that look big, strong, or tough (a big rock, a thick tree trunk, a sturdy fence post). After photographing each find, the child sorts them by comparing: which is biggest? Which is toughest? The lion started the brave parade!

**② Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Learn that lions have yellow-brown fur. Learn that lions are very big cats. Learn that lions make a loud roaring sound. Learn the word "strong" (hard, tough, not easy to break).
- **U (Understand):** Understand that a lion's Form — big body, strong paws — helps it look brave and powerful. Understand that the Function of being big and strong helps animals and things survive and do their jobs.
- **D (Do):** Practice looking for things that share a physical property — "big and strong." Practice comparing collected items by size and toughness. Practice photographing finds and describing them.

**③ Design Highlight**

The toy lion becomes the captain of a "Brave Parade" — the child recruits big, strong-looking things from the yard to join the lion's team. The comparison_chart synthesis has the child sort finds by "biggest" and "toughest," making abstract qualities concrete through side-by-side comparison. The roar call-and-response thread ties every round together with sensory play.

**④ Typical Scenario**

Child photographs toy lion in the yard → AI roars and wonders what else is brave and strong → child becomes "Brave Things Scout" → finds and photographs 2 strong-looking things → sorts them by big vs. tough → celebrates the Brave Parade → reflects on why big things seem strong.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T0 (ages 2–4)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (amazed, roaring voice) "ROARRR! A lion! So big and strong! Does it look brave to you?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yeah!" / "It's brave!" / "Roar!" / child roars back
2. (Unexpected) "It's soft" / "It's my toy" / "It's yellow"
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (thrilled) "So brave! Big brave lion. I bet there are more brave things here!"
2. (warm, validating) "Soft AND brave! Yellow like sunshine. Such a strong lion! I wonder what else is big like lion?"
3. (wait 2s) (soft roar) "Roarrr. This lion looks SO strong. Big paws! Big face! What else is big here?"

**Screen:** Lion photo centered with golden shimmer around the mane, small paw-print stamps on the border, and a gentle roar sound-wave icon.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (excited, playful) "You are a Brave Scout! Find 2 big strong things. Take a photo! Ready? Go!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yeah!" / "I go find!" / child starts walking
2. (Unexpected) "What's strong?" / "Like what?"
3. (No response) Child stands still or looks around slowly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (cheering) "Go go go, Brave Scout! Big and strong — like lion!"
2. (helpful, warm) "Strong means hard! Big! Like a rock. Or a tree. Go look!"
3. (wait 2s) (encouraging) "Look around! A big rock? A thick tree? Find something strong!"

**Screen:** Mission card with "Brave Scout" badge (lion paw icon), 3 slots (first filled with lion photo, 2 empty with paw-print placeholders), and a "Find 2!" counter.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1 — First Find:**

*(Child takes a photo of something — e.g., a big rock, a tree trunk, a sturdy post)*

**AI says:** (excited discovery) "Ooh! What is it? Is it big?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "A rock!" / "Big tree!" / "It's hard!"
2. (Unexpected) "A flower" / "Grass" / names something small or soft
3. (No response) Child is quiet after taking the photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted) "A big rock! So strong! Like lion. Roarrr! 1 more to go!"
2. (warm, accepting) "Ooh, pretty! Is it big or small? Even small things can be strong! That counts. 1 more to go!"
3. (wait 2s) (warm) "I see it! It looks strong to me. Nice find, Brave Scout! 1 more!"

**Screen:** Photo slides into slot 2 with a lion-paw stamp animation; counter updates to "1 of 2 found."

**Round 2 — Second Find:** Child photographs another item (e.g., a fence, a thick branch, a wall); AI celebrates the tough find and declares the Brave Scout collection complete.

**STUCK BRANCH:** AI suggests looking down for a big rock or up for a thick tree, noting strong things like to hide. If still stuck, AI points toward the fence or the biggest thing in sight.

#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (proud, building) "Look! Lion and two friends. Which one is biggest?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "The tree!" / "The rock!" / child points at one
2. (Unexpected) "Lion!" / "All of them!" / "I don't know"
3. (No response) Child looks at the photos quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (impressed) "Yes! So big! Now — which is toughest? Which one is really hard?"
2. (warm, scaffolding) "Lion IS big! Now look — is the rock bigger? Or the [other find]? Which is the biggest of ALL?"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, guiding) "Look at all three. Point to the BIGGEST one!"

**AI says (after child picks biggest):** (curious) "Now — which is toughest? Really hard?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "The rock!" / "The tree!" / child picks one
2. (Unexpected) "Lion!" / "Everything!" / unrelated answer
3. (No response) Child is quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (celebrating) "So tough! Hard like lion's paws. The brave parade is ready!"
2. (warm) "Good thinking! Lion has strong paws too. You picked a great brave team!"
3. (wait 2s) (playful) "Touch one — is it hard? That's the toughest! Lion says roarrr!"

**Screen:** All 3 photos side by side with comparison labels (crown for biggest, shield for toughest) and an animated "The Brave Parade!" banner.

**AI says:** (warm, celebrating) "Your brave parade! Lion, and two strong friends. All big! All tough! Why are big things strong?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Because they're big!" / "They don't break!" / "Hard!"
2. (Unexpected) "I like lion!" / "Roar!" / unrelated answer
3. (No response) Child looks at the collection with a smile.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (amazed) "Yes! Big and hard! That's what makes them strong, just like lion. You are so smart!"
2. (warm, connecting) "Lion is the best! And look — lion picked strong friends. Big things are tough! Roarrr!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle, warm) "Big things are hard to push. That's why they seem brave — just like lion!"

**Screen:** Collection displayed as a parade line with the lion leading, animated footsteps trailing behind each photo, and sparkle effects.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (warm, proud) "Brave Scout! You did it! You found things with strong Form — big, tough, and hard. And you saw their Function — being big helps them stay strong! Lion says ROARRR — thank you! You earned your Brave Scout badge!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Engaged) "Roar!" / "Yay!" / "I did it!"
2. (Quiet) Child smiles or waves at the screen.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (celebration) "ROARRR! Great job, Brave Scout! Bye bye!"
2. (gentle) "You were so brave today. Bye, Brave Scout!"

**Screen:** Golden badge spinning into center labeled "Brave Scout" with lion paw and crown motif; collection photos as small insets; "Form" and "Function" in bold golden lettering; golden confetti and a roaring lion silhouette.
