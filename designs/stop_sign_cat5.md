# Activity Design: Stop Sign + Category 5 (Collection/Tracking Exploration)

## Activity: Sign Spotter Safari

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: Sign Spotter Safari
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: Function, Responsibility
- **Related Concepts**: Rules (mapping), Community (mapping), Safety (designed), Discovery (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection), Thinking Skills (critical thinking, transfer)
- **Trigger Entity**: Stop sign
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a stop sign at the street corner during a walk
- **Mapping Source**: signs_symbols_stop_sign
- **IB Theme**: How We Organize Ourselves (mapping: primary, weight=0.43)
- **Dimension Anchors**: appearance (physical), structure (physical), relationship (engagement)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: appearance — white_border, reflective_shine; structure — post_pole

---

### B. Activity Overview

- **1. Brief Description**: After photographing a stop sign, the child becomes a "Sign Spotter" on a mission to find other signs and symbols in the neighborhood that tell people what to do. The child photographs 3 different signs or symbols nearby, describes what each one says or means, and then sorts the collection by whether each sign helps people stop, go, or stay safe.

- **2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Signs have different shapes and colors; a stop sign is an octagon with a white border; signs tell people rules without talking; signs are placed at corners and crossings where people need help
  - **U (Understand)**: Signs have a Function — they tell us what to do without words (Function). People who follow signs help keep everyone safe (Responsibility).
  - **D (Do)**: Observe and compare visual features of signs (Research — observation); sort collected signs by purpose (Thinking — critical thinking); transfer the idea that signs are rules to new examples (Thinking — transfer)

- **3. Design Highlight**: The "Sign Spotter Safari" metaphor turns a neighborhood walk into a detective mission. The child is not just collecting random objects — they are discovering that their neighborhood is full of silent helpers that keep everyone safe. The synthesis step (sorting by "stop, go, or stay safe") makes the collection meaningful and earns the Function and Responsibility concepts organically.

- **4. Typical Scenario**: Child spots a stop sign on a walk, photographs it from a safe sidewalk spot, then goes on a safari to find 3 more signs or symbols they can see from the sidewalk or another grown-up-approved safe place, photographs each, and sorts them by what they tell people to do.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the stop sign.
> **Conversation anchor**: appearance — white_border ("a bright white edge that frames the red"); structure — post_pole ("a tall pole holding the sign up high")
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* You noticed the stop sign has a bright white border, like a picture frame! And it stands way up high on a tall pole. I bet there are MORE signs nearby. Want to go find them from a safe spot?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah! I want to find more signs!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to look at this stop sign more."
> 3. (No response) Child looks around quietly or is distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited)* Great! Let's go on a sign safari! I think there are lots of signs nearby."
> 2. "*(warm, validating)* This stop sign is pretty cool, right? After we look at it, let's see what other signs live near here too!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, inviting)* Look around — do you see any other signs nearby? Even a small one counts!"
>
> **Screen**: Stop sign photo centered with the white border area softly glowing. A faint "conversation recap" shimmer highlights the pole and border. A small safari binoculars icon appears in the corner.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs a stop sign with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted surprise)* Wow, a stop sign! Look at that big, bold red color. It is like a shield on the street corner. I wonder who this sign is protecting!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It tells cars to stop!" / "It keeps people safe!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It says STOP!" / "It is red!"
> 3. (No response) Child stares at the sign or looks around.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Yes! It helps cars stop so people can cross safely. This sign is like a helper for the whole neighborhood!"
> 2. "*(validating)* You are right, it IS red! That bright red color means 'pay attention.' This sign helps everyone stay safe at the corner."
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(warm, leaning in)* This sign stands here every day, helping cars and people. It is like a quiet helper!"
>
> **Screen**: Stop sign photo centered with a soft red glow around it. Gentle shield-like outline animation pulses once around the octagon shape.

---

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(excited, mission tone)* You are now a Sign Spotter! Your mission has three parts. First, find three more signs or symbols nearby. Second, take a photo of each one. Third, we will figure out what each sign tells people to do. Ready, Sign Spotter?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Let's go!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "What is a sign spotter?" / "I only see the stop sign."
> 3. (No response) Child looks uncertain or doesn't reply.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(pumped up)* Let's go! Stay on the sidewalk or another safe spot and look for any sign or symbol you can see. When you find one, take a photo!"
> 2. "*(friendly, explaining)* A Sign Spotter finds signs that help people! Signs can be on poles, on walls, or at the edge of a parking area. Look from your safe spot — I bet you will spot one soon!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* Try looking up, down, and to the sides from where you are standing. Signs can be anywhere!"
>
> **Screen**: Mission card appears with "Sign Spotter" badge (binoculars icon over a shield). Four collection slots displayed — the first slot filled with the stop sign photo, three empty slots with question marks. A numbered task list shows: 1) Find 3 signs, 2) Photo each one, 3) Sort them.

---

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

**Round 1** (full detail):

> *(Child takes a photo of a sign or symbol they can see safely from the sidewalk or another grown-up-approved safe place — e.g., a speed limit sign, yield sign, one-way arrow, no-parking sign, or pedestrian signal.)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(excited discovery)* You found one! I can see a sign. What does this sign tell people to do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It tells cars to go slow!" / "It means people can walk here!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I do not know." / "It is just a sign."
> 3. (No response / silent) Child waits or takes another look.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Nice spotting! That sign helps people know what to do here. One sign in the collection! Two more to go, Sign Spotter!"
> 2. "*(warm, scaffolding)* That is okay! Look at its shape and color. Big and bright means it wants people to notice it. I think this sign is a helper too! One down, two to go!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(friendly)* I see something in your photo! It looks like a sign that helps people. One collected — two more to find!"
>
> **Screen**: New photo slides into the second collection slot. A small star animation bursts. Counter updates: "1 of 3 found!" The stop sign stays in slot 1 for comparison.

**Round 2**:

> *(Child takes a photo of another sign or symbol)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(playful surprise)* Another one! This sign looks different from the stop sign. What color is it?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It is yellow!" / "It is green!"
> 2. (Unexpected) Child describes something else about it.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(connecting)* Different color from the stop sign! Each color tells people something different. Great eye, Sign Spotter — one more to go!"
> 2. "*(validating)* Good noticing! Every little detail matters when you are a Sign Spotter. One more to find!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* This one is a cool find. Look how different it is from the red stop sign! Just one more to go!"
>
> **Screen**: Photo slides into the third slot. Brief color-comparison sparkle animation between the new sign and the stop sign. Counter: "2 of 3 found!"

**Round 3**:

> *(Child takes a photo of a third sign or symbol)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(celebrating)* You found the last one! Look at your collection — four signs! Are any of them the same shape?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "No, they are all different!" / "That one is round!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to find more!" / Child describes something else.
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* You are right! Different shapes, different colors, but they are ALL helpers on the street. Your collection is full!"
> 2. "*(enthusiastic)* I love that you want more! You are a true Sign Spotter. Let's look at what we have first — then you can keep spotting!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(warm)* Look at all four on screen — the stop sign and three new ones! What a great collection!"
>
> **Screen**: Final photo fills the fourth slot. All four photos displayed with a brief celebratory shimmer. "Collection Complete!" banner appears with confetti dots.

**STUCK BRANCH** (if child cannot find a sign):

> **AI says**: "*(helpful, specific)* Signs like to hide in plain sight! Stay on the sidewalk and look up at poles, fences, and parking-lot entrances. Ask a grown-up to point if you need help. I bet you can spot one without going near the road!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Finds something) Child takes a photo.
> 2. (Still stuck) "I still can not find one."
> 3. (No response) Child wanders.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* You found one! Great spotting!"
> 2. "*(reassuring)* That is okay! Look for anything with a shape or symbol on it — even a sign on a fence, post, wall, or parking entrance. Those all count!"
> 3. *(wait 3 seconds)* "*(gentle)* Try looking from where you are standing. Do you see any sign on a pole, wall, or fence?"
>
> **Screen**: A soft pulsing "hint" overlay appears — small arrow icons pointing up (to poles) and down (to ground markings). A magnifying glass icon gently bounces.

---

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, mission-complete tone)* Sign Spotter, your collection is amazing! Now look at all four signs together. Can you sort them? Which ones tell people to STOP? Which ones tell people to GO? Which ones tell people to STAY SAFE?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The stop sign says stop! That one says go!" / Child points and sorts.
> 2. (Unexpected) "They all help people!" / Child doesn't sort clearly.
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* Great sorting! Some signs say stop, some say go, and some say be careful. They each have a different job — but they all work together!"
> 2. "*(validating, scaffolding)* You are right — they ALL help people! Let's try this: point to the one that says 'stop.' Now which one helps people walk safely? See — they have different jobs!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(warm, guiding)* Let's start with the stop sign. It says 'stop,' right? Now look at the others — does any one say 'go' or 'be careful'?"
>
> **Screen**: All four photos displayed in a row. Three sorting labels appear below: "STOP" (red), "GO" (green), "STAY SAFE" (yellow). As the child sorts verbally, photos gently slide under the matching label. Connection lines link photos in the same group.

---

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* You found four signs that all help people on this street! Each sign has a different shape and color, but they all do the same big thing — they keep the neighborhood safe. Why do you think we need so many different signs and not just one?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Because they tell people different things!" / "One sign cannot do everything!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I do not know." / "Because there are lots of cars."
> 3. (No response) Child thinks quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Exactly! Each sign has its own job. Just like people work together, signs work together to keep everyone safe."
> 2. "*(warm, extending)* That is a great thought! There ARE a lot of cars and people. So we need lots of helpers — each sign has its own special job to do!"
> 3. *(wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, wondering)* Think about it — the stop sign says stop, but the other signs say different things. They are like a team of helpers!"
>
> **Screen**: Collection displayed as a connected set — all four photos arranged in a diamond pattern. Animated dotted lines connect them, forming a "helper network." A small neighborhood illustration appears in the background.

---

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, celebratory)* You are an amazing Sign Spotter! You found signs all around the neighborhood and figured out what each one does. You discovered that every sign has a special job — that is called Function! And you learned that following signs keeps everyone safe — that is Responsibility! You earned your Sign Spotter Safari badge!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I am a Sign Spotter!" / Child cheers.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can I find more signs?" / "I like the stop sign best!"
> 3. (No response) Child watches the badge animation.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming)* The best Sign Spotter on this street! Keep your eyes open — signs are everywhere!"
> 2. "*(warm)* You can ALWAYS spot more signs! Now you know what to look for. And the stop sign is a great favorite — it has a very important job!"
> 3. "*(celebratory)* Look at your badge! You earned it by exploring and thinking. Great job today!"
>
> **Screen**: Badge animation — a shield-shaped "Sign Spotter Safari" badge with the child's four collection photos as corner insets. The words "Function" and "Responsibility" appear artistically in a road-sign style (Function in a blue rectangle, Responsibility in a green circle). Gentle confetti and star animations.
