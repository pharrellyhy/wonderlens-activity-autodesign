## Activity: The Safety Solver (安全小帮手)

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Safety Solver (安全小帮手)
- **Activity Category**: 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Responsibility** (What is our responsibility?) & **Function** (How does it work?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Rules (mapping), Community (mapping), Safety (designed), Communication (mapping)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Thinking Skills (critical thinking, transfer), Communication Skills (expressing ideas, listening), Social Skills (empathy)
- **Game Style**: helper_hotline
- **Trigger Entity**: Stop sign (picture on the fridge)
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a stop sign picture on the fridge and helps solve traffic safety scenarios
- **Mapping Source**: signs_symbols_stop_sign
- **IB Theme**: How We Organize Ourselves (mapping: primary, weight=0.43)
- **Dimension Anchors**: emotions (engagement), relationship (engagement), function (physical)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: emotions — "How do you feel when you see a stop sign — safe, annoyed, or calm?"; function — turn_taking ("helping cars take turns safely at crossings")

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After the child photographs a stop sign picture, the AI marvels at the bold red octagon and how it keeps everyone safe. The child becomes a "Safety Solver" who gets pretend calls from people, animals, and vehicles who need help with tricky traffic situations. Each round, the AI presents a safety problem at a crossing, and the child decides what the right thing to do is — building an intuition for rules, turn-taking, and caring about others on the road.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: A stop sign has a bright white border that frames the red; it has a shiny coating that glows when lights hit it at night; stop signs stand at corners where roads cross each other; the sign tells drivers to stop fully before going; stop signs help cars take turns safely at crossings
  - **U (Understand)**: The stop sign has a specific job — to make everyone stop and take turns at crossings. That is **Function** (how it works). Everyone — drivers, bikers, walkers — has a duty to follow the stop sign rule to keep the community safe. That is **Responsibility** (what is our responsibility).
  - **D (Do)**: Practice deciding what to do in safety scenarios (Thinking Skills — critical thinking, transfer). Practice explaining choices in words (Communication Skills — expressing ideas). Practice considering other people's safety (Social Skills — empathy).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Safety Solver" metaphor transforms traffic rules into a dispatch-style problem-solving game. The child is not memorizing rules — they are making decisions about what someone should DO in tricky situations involving crossings, turns, and sharing the road. Each caller presents a scenario with increasing complexity, from a simple "should I stop?" to situations involving helping others and making fair choices.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a stop sign picture on the fridge → AI notices its bold red shape and purpose → child becomes a Safety Solver who receives calls about tricky traffic situations and decides what to do.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_1 conversation about the stop sign.
> **Conversation anchor**: function — turn_taking ("helping cars take turns safely at crossings"); emotions — "How do you feel when you see a stop sign?"
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* You figured out that stop signs help cars take turns at crossings — like sharing a toy, but with the road! That is so smart. I keep wondering... what if someone did NOT know what to do at a stop sign? What if WE could help them?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yeah! I can help!" / "We tell them to stop!" / "Okay!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Stop signs are red." / "I see them on the street." / "My mom stops."
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the screen silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited, conspiratorial)* Ooh, telling them to stop — perfect! What if people CALLED us for help? Let's set up a Safety Solver hotline!"
> 2. "*(encouraging)* Your mom is a great driver! But some people get confused at crossings. I bet you could help them figure out what to do. Want to try?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, playful whisper)* Hey — what if people called us when they were confused at a crossing? And YOU told them what to do? Like a safety helper!"
>
> **Screen**: Stop sign photo centered with a soft red glow around it, subtle "conversation recap" shimmer highlighting the octagon shape. A small phone icon fades in at the bottom corner.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs a stop sign picture with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted gasp)* Ohhh, a stop sign! Look at that bright red color and that special shape — it has eight sides! This sign is like a superhero for the street. It keeps everyone safe. If you were walking to the park and you saw this sign, what would YOU do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Stop!" / "I would wait!" / "Look both ways!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's red!" / "I know that sign!" / "My dad stops the car."
> 3. (No response) Child watches the screen silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, impressed)* Yes! You would stop and look — that is exactly right! You already know the stop sign's rule. I bet you could help OTHER people learn it too."
> 2. "*(enthusiastic agreement)* You DO know that sign! It is everywhere, keeping everyone safe. But some people get confused at crossings. What if we could help them figure it out?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft, wondering tone)* This stop sign works SO hard to keep the street safe. What if we helped it out? Want to be a safety helper with me?"
>
> **Screen**: Stop sign photo fills the center of the screen. Gentle red pulse animation radiates outward from the octagon. Tiny sparkle animations glint off the white border, like the reflective coating catching light.

**Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo — "The Safety Solver Hotline"**

> **AI says**: "*(playful, setting up the game)* Okay, here is how our Safety Solver Hotline works! Someone calls us because they are confused about what to do at a crossing. You decide what they should do! Watch — I will show you."
>
> **AI says**: "*(mimicking a phone ring)* Ring ring! 'Hello, I am riding my bike and I see a stop sign. Should I keep going?' Hmm, what should we tell them? I think... STOP! Put your feet down, look both ways, and THEN go when it is safe. See? Your turn next!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Okay! My turn!" / "I want to help!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I ride a bike too!" / "Can I call?"
> 3. (No response) Child is watching quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(enthusiastic)* Great! Get ready — here comes the first call to our hotline!"
> 2. "*(warm, redirecting)* You ride a bike — so you know about stopping! Now let's help other people. Ready for a call?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* The phone is ringing! Should we pick it up together?"
>
> **Screen**: The stop sign photo moves to the top left corner. A large, colorful phone icon appears in the center with a "ring ring" animation (phone wiggling). After the demo, a small bicycle icon slides in next to a checkmark, showing the solved problem. A "Call 1" badge glows softly below.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction (3–5 rounds)**

**Round 1: The Rushing Driver** *(Simple, confidence-building)*

> **AI says**: "*(phone ring sound effect)* Ring ring! 'Help! I am driving and I am in a hurry. I see a stop sign but I want to go fast. What should I do?' Oh! What should we tell this driver?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Stop!" / "You have to stop!" / "Wait your turn!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Go fast!" / "Honk the horn!" / "Drive!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* Yes! Even if you are in a hurry, you MUST stop at the stop sign. Stop fully, look both ways, and then go when it is your turn. The stop sign's job is to keep everyone safe. That is its Function! Great solving!"
> 2. "*(gently guiding)* Going fast sounds fun — but at a stop sign, EVERYONE has to stop, even if they are late. If cars do not take turns, they could crash! The stop sign's job — its Function — is to make sure nobody gets hurt. What should the driver do first?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(helpful hint)* The driver is in a hurry. But the stop sign says STOP. Should the driver listen to the sign... or just keep going?"
>
> **Screen**: A cartoon street corner with a stop sign. A rushing car with speed lines approaches. When the child answers, the car slows and stops at the sign. A green checkmark appears. Small eye icons show the driver looking left and right. "CALL 1 ✓" stamp appears.

**Round 2: The Kid on the Crosswalk** *(Introduces caring for others — responsibility angle)*

> **AI says**: "*(phone ring)* Ring ring! 'Hello! I stopped at the stop sign. But a little kid is still walking across the street really slowly. Should I go now or keep waiting?' Hmm, this one needs your help!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Wait!" / "Let the kid cross!" / "Keep waiting!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Honk at the kid!" / "Tell the kid to run!" / "Go around!"
> 3. (No response) Child is thinking.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(touched, warm)* That is so kind and RIGHT! The driver waits until the little kid finishes crossing. People walking come FIRST. Taking care of walkers is a big Responsibility — and you just showed it!"
> 2. "*(gently guiding)* Honking might scare the kid! What if the driver just waits patiently? The kid is almost across. Sometimes being safe means being patient. Taking care of people walking is a Responsibility we all share."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm prompt)* A little kid is still walking across. Should the driver go now... or wait until the kid is safe on the other side?"
>
> **Screen**: A street corner scene. A car is stopped at the stop sign. A small child figure walks slowly across the crosswalk. When the child responds, the car stays put and the walking child makes it safely across. Small heart icons float up. "CALL 2 ✓" stamp appears.

**Round 3: Two Cars at the Same Time** *(Tricky, peak engagement — introduces fairness)*

> **AI says**: "*(curious, playful tone)* Ring ring! This one is tricky! 'Hello! TWO cars got to the stop sign at the same time. We both stopped! But now we BOTH want to go. Who goes first?' Oh wow — what should they do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Take turns!" / "One goes first!" / "The one on the right!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Both go!" / "Nobody goes!" / "Rock paper scissors!"
> 3. (No response) Child is thinking or distracted.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* YES! They take turns — one goes, then the other. On real streets, the car on the right usually goes first. It is like sharing — you cannot both go at once, or CRASH! Taking turns at a stop sign is what keeps the whole community safe. Awesome solving!"
> 2. "*(validating warmly)* Rock paper scissors — ha! That IS one way to decide! On the real road, the car on the right side usually gets to go first. It is a rule so everybody knows whose turn it is. If they both went at the same time — uh oh, crash! Taking turns is how stop signs keep the community safe."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enticing whisper)* Two cars, one crossing — they cannot both go at once! What happens when two kids both want the same swing? They take... ?"
>
> **Screen**: An intersection viewed from above with two cars facing each other at stop signs. Both are stopped. When the child responds, one car proceeds while the other waits, then the second car goes. A "taking turns" arrow animation shows the sequence. A fairness scales icon appears briefly. "CALL 3 ✓" stamp appears with a sparkle effect.

**Round 4 (if child is engaged): The Missing Stop Sign** *(Open-ended — ethical thinking)*

> **AI says**: "*(soft, wondering tone)* Bonus call! Ring ring! 'Help! The stop sign at my corner fell down in a big storm. Now nobody knows to stop! Cars are zooming by and it feels scary. What should we do?' Wow, this is a big one. What do you think?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Tell someone to fix it!" / "Stand there and tell cars to stop!" / "Call the police!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Stay inside!" / "Just be careful!" / "Put a new one up!"
> 3. (No response) Child may be getting tired or thinking hard.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(gentle, impressed)* That is EXACTLY right! When a stop sign is missing, we have a Responsibility to help. We can tell a grown-up, call the city, or help people be extra careful at that corner. You are thinking about the whole community — not just yourself. That is real Responsibility!"
> 2. "*(validating warmly)* Being careful is a good start! And telling a grown-up so they can get it fixed would help the WHOLE neighborhood. When the sign is gone, everybody has to look out for each other. That is what Responsibility means — caring about everyone's safety."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(cozy whisper)* This is the toughest call. No stop sign — cars are confused. What could someone do to make the corner safe again? Maybe tell a grown-up... or call someone who can help?"
>
> **Screen**: A corner scene where the stop sign pole is empty — the sign lies on the ground. Cars approach uncertainly. When the child responds, a helper figure appears and directs traffic, then a repair person puts the sign back up. The community looks relieved. "BONUS CALL ✓" stamp glows faintly in gold.

**Step 4: Celebration + Role Title**

> **AI says**: "*(proud, warm celebration)* WOW! You answered every single call! You helped a rushing driver stop, kept a little kid safe, taught two cars to take turns, and even fixed a missing sign. You are officially a... SAFETY SOLVER! You knew exactly what everyone needed to do."
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yay!" / "I did it!" / Giggles or cheers.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can we play again?" / "I want more calls!" / "I like stop signs now."
> 3. (No response) Child smiles or looks pleased.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming)* The BEST Safety Solver! Every caller got the right help because of you. The whole street is safer now!"
> 2. "*(delighted)* You want more calls? That means you are a REAL safety expert — always watching out for people! We can play again another day. For now, let me tell you something amazing about what you just did..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm whisper)* You know what? I think the stop sign would be proud of you. You really understand how to keep people safe."
>
> **Screen**: A golden badge animation fills the center of the screen reading "SAFETY SOLVER" with a small stop-sign icon at the top. Confetti rains down in red and white. The stop sign photo reappears surrounded by small icons of each caller (car, walking kid, two cars, repair worker) all with checkmarks.

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective pride)* You know what you showed me today? You figured out that the stop sign has ONE big job — making everyone stop, look, and take turns. That is called Function — everything has a purpose! And you chose to help every single caller, even the tricky ones. You made sure everyone — drivers, walkers, the whole neighborhood — stayed safe. That is called Responsibility — caring about others and doing the right thing. You are a true safety hero!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Function!" / "Responsibility!" / "I'm a hero!" / Repeats a word happily.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can I go see a real stop sign?" / "Bye stop sign!"
> 3. (No response) Child listens or looks at the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebrating)* That is right — Function and Responsibility! You earned your badge, Safety Solver. Next time you see a stop sign, you will know it is working hard — just like you!"
> 2. "*(warm, laughing)* Go look at a real stop sign — you will see it in a whole new way now! And remember, it is always on duty. See you next time, Safety Solver!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle closing)* Great job today, Solver. The streets have a really smart new friend. See you next time!"
>
> **Screen**: The Safety Solver badge remains centered. Below it, the words **"Function"** and **"Responsibility"** appear one at a time in bold, friendly lettering — each word accompanied by a small icon (a gear for Function, a heart-and-hands for Responsibility). The stop sign photo glows warmly behind the text. Soft sparkle animations surround the concept words. After 3 seconds, a gentle "The End" ribbon scrolls across the bottom.
