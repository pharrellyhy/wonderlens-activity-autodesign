## Activity: The Sunshine Parts Patrol

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Sunshine Parts Patrol
- **Activity Category**: 5 — Collection/Tracking Exploration (Out-of-Device, Solo, Outdoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Change** (How is it changing?) & **Function** (How does it work?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Growth (mapping — Science), Cycles (mapping — Science), Discovery (designed), Structure (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Research Skills (observation, data collection — close looking at plant parts), Thinking Skills (critical thinking — comparing how parts serve purposes), Self-Management Skills (organization — tracking finds across a space)
- **Trigger Entity**: Sunflower
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs a tall sunflower in the garden.
- **Mapping Source**: plants_sunflower
- **IB Theme**: How the World Works (mapping: primary, weight=0.46)
- **Dimension Anchors**: appearance (physical — petal shape, seed pattern, stem thickness), structure (physical — flower head, veiny leaves), relationship (engagement — comparisons to other things)
- **Conversation Anchor Dimensions**: appearance — petal_shape ("long skinny petals like sunny little rays"), structure — flower_head ("one big head made of many tiny flowers")

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After photographing a sunflower, the AI marvels at its many different parts — petals like tiny sun rays, a bumpy seed center, a thick strong stem — and wonders what each part DOES for the plant. The child becomes a "Plant Parts Inspector" on a mission to find 3 more things in the garden that have parts with a special job. Each find gets logged with its "part" and "job," and at the end the child picks which find changes the most over time — connecting Function (parts have jobs) to Change (things grow and transform).

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: Learn the vocabulary "petals," "stem," "seeds," "nectar," "job." Learn that sunflower petals attract bees, the thick stem holds the heavy head up, and the seeds inside the center can grow into new plants.
  - **U (Understand)**: Understand that every part of a living thing has a **Function** — a special job that helps the whole plant or creature. Understand that living things **Change** over time as their parts grow, open, or make new things like seeds.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice close observation of plant and nature parts (Research Skills — observation), compare what different parts do for their owners (Thinking Skills — critical thinking), and track multiple finds across a garden space (Self-Management Skills — organization).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Plant Parts Inspector" role reframes a nature walk as a detective mission — the child is not just finding things but investigating what each PART does. The metaphor of "every part has a job" is concrete enough for T1 and connects naturally to both Function (what does this part do?) and Change (how do parts grow and transform?). The synthesis twist asks the child to pick which find changes the most, linking observation to temporal thinking.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs a sunflower in the garden → AI points out how many parts it has, each with a special job → launches a "Plant Parts Patrol" to find 3 more things with parts that have jobs → child picks which find changes the most over time.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1 (ages 4–6)]

**Step 1a: Transition Bridge — Warm Start** (post-conversation)

> **Context**: Child has just finished a tier_guidance conversation about the sunflower.
> **Conversation anchor**: appearance — petal_shape ("long skinny petals like sunny little rays")
>
> **AI says**: "*(warm, building on earlier)* Remember those long skinny petals we talked about? You said they look like sunny little rays! Each ray has a special job — it waves at bees and says 'come visit me!' I wonder what OTHER things around here have parts with special jobs too..."
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The leaves have jobs too!" / "Bees like the petals!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to touch the petals!" / "It is really tall."
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the sunflower quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* You are so right! Leaves, stems, petals — they all have jobs. What if we went on a mission to find MORE things with parts that do something special?"
> 2. "*(warmly agreeing)* It IS really tall! That thick stem has a big job — holding up that heavy flower head! I bet we can find other things with parts that have jobs too..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, curious)* See how the thick stem holds up the big flower? That is the stem's job! What if we looked for more things with parts that have special jobs?"
>
> **Screen**: Sunflower photo centered with a soft golden glow around the petals. A small animated arrow points from a petal to a tiny bee icon, showing the "job" connection. A "conversation recap" shimmer highlights the petal area.

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs a sunflower with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(amazed wonder)* Wow — look at this sunflower! It is like a tiny sun growing right out of the ground! Those petals, that big round middle, that tall strong stem — every part looks so different. I wonder... does each part have its own special job?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The petals are pretty!" / "The stem holds it up!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I want to pick it!" / "It is yellow!"
> 3. (No response) Child gazes at the sunflower or screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* Yes! The petals wave at bees to come visit. And you know what? The stem holds the big heavy head up high! Every part has a job. I bet there are MORE things out here with parts that have special jobs..."
> 2. "*(warm, validating)* It IS so bright and yellow! Those yellow petals have a job — they tell the bees 'come get some nectar here!' Every part does something. I wonder what other things have parts with jobs..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft, inviting)* See those long petals? They wave at bees! And the thick stem? It holds up the heavy head. Every part has a job! What if we found more things like that?"
>
> **Screen**: Close-up of the sunflower photo with soft glow. Gentle animated sparkles trace along the petals, then the stem, then the seed center — each briefly labeled with a tiny icon (bee, arrow-up, seed). A "Parts Counter" icon shows "1" in the bottom corner.

**Step 2: Mission Briefing**

> **AI says**: "*(adventurous, inspector tone)* Alright — you are now an official Plant Parts Inspector! Your patrol mission has three parts. Part one: find 3 more things nearby that have a part with a special job. Part two: take a photo of each one. Part three: at the end, you pick which one CHANGES the most over time! Ready to start your patrol, Inspector?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yes!" / "Let's go!"
> 2. (Uncertain) "What do you mean a special job?" / "What should I look for?"
> 3. (No response) Looks around the garden.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* Patrol is GO! Find something with a part that does a job, snap a photo, and report back!"
> 2. "*(encouraging)* A special job means the part DOES something for the thing it belongs to. Like a flower petal waves at bees. Or a thorn keeps animals away. Look at plants, bugs, even rocks with moss — what part stands out?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gently)* Here is your first clue — look at any plant near you. See any leaves, thorns, or berries? Each one does something special! Take a photo when you spot one."
>
> **Screen**: A mission card appears with an "Inspector" badge outline showing a magnifying glass over a leaf. Below: 3 empty square slots (plus the sunflower photo above with a golden check). A numbered task list with icons (magnifying glass, camera, clock) shows the three parts of the mission.

**Step 3: Multi-Round Exploration (3–4 rounds)**

> **Round 1 — First Find:**
>
> *(Child photographs something — e.g., a leaf on a bush)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(eager)* Inspector report incoming! What part do you see, and what do you think its job is?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A leaf! It catches the sun!" / "This leaf helps the plant eat!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "A green thing." (minimal description)
> 3. (No response) Just takes the photo.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(thrilled)* A leaf that catches sunlight — YES! That is exactly its job. The leaf soaks up sun and helps the plant make food. Patrol Log: 'Green Leaf — job: catching sunshine!' Great first find, Inspector. Two more to go!"
> 2. "*(looking closely)* A green thing! Look at how flat and wide it is. That flat shape helps it catch as much sunlight as possible — that is its job! Patrol Log: 'Flat Green Catcher — job: soaking up sun!' Two more, Inspector!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enthusiastic)* Oooh, let me see! I see something flat and green. That flat shape has a job — it catches sunlight to help the plant grow. What do YOU think this part does?"
>
> **Screen**: New photo slides into the first empty slot. A brief sparkle burst. A small label appears below: "Part: ? Job: ?" which fills in based on the conversation. Counter updates to "2 of 4."

> **Round 2 — Second Find:**
>
> *(Child photographs something — e.g., a flower bud or a berry)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(detective voice)* Report number two! What part did your inspector eyes find this time?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "A berry! It has seeds inside!" / "A flower bud that is not open yet!"
> 2. (Stretch) "I found a rock." (item without obvious parts that have jobs)
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(impressed)* A berry with seeds inside — the berry's job is to protect those tiny seeds until they are ready! And one day each seed could grow into a whole new plant. Patrol Log: 'Berry Pod — job: keeping seeds safe!' One more find, Inspector!"
> 2. "*(thoughtful)* A rock! Hmm, look very closely — does it have moss or a tiny plant growing on it? The rock's bumpy surface can hold water for little plants. Even a rock can have a helpful job! Patrol Log: 'Helper Rock — job: holding water for moss!' One more to go!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(curious)* I see your photo! Look at the shape of this find. Does any part stick out or look different from the rest? That special part might have a secret job. What do you notice?"
>
> **Screen**: Second slot fills in. Counter updates to "3 of 4." A dotted trail animation shows the inspector path with a flag at the finish.

> **Round 3 — Third Find (with stuck branch):**
>
> **STUCK BRANCH** *(if child has been searching without finding something)*:
>
> **AI says**: "*(helpful whisper)* Inspector tip! Look close to the ground — do you see any thorns on a stem? Thorns have a job: keeping the plant safe! Or look for a flower with bright petals. Bright colors have a job too — waving at butterflies!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I see thorns!" / "I found a bright flower!" / Child spots something.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I still cannot find anything." / "There is nothing here."
> 3. (No response) Child keeps looking around without choosing.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Great spotting! Go snap a photo and tell me what job that part does!"
> 2. "*(reassuring)* That is okay! Try looking at the bark on a tree trunk. The bark's job is to protect the tree like armor. Even plain-looking bark counts, Inspector!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle prompt)* Let us slow down and look very close at one plant. See any bumps, hairs, or spots on it? Even tiny things have jobs!"
>
> *(Child photographs final find — e.g., a thorn, a bright petal, or tree bark)*
>
> **AI says**: "*(triumphant)* FINAL PATROL FIND! What part is this, and what is its special job?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Tree bark! It keeps the tree safe!" / "A thorn — it is sharp so animals stay away!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "I do not know what it does."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed)* Tree bark — nature's armor! It wraps around the tree to keep bugs and weather out. Patrol Log: 'Tree Armor — job: protecting the trunk!' Inspector, your patrol collection is COMPLETE!"
> 2. "*(excited anyway)* That is what inspectors figure out! Look at how tough and rough this part is. I think its job is protection — keeping the soft insides safe. Patrol Log: 'Tough Guard — job: staying strong!' Collection done, Inspector!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(enthusiastic)* I see your photo! This part looks rough and strong. I think its job is protecting the plant inside. Patrol Log: 'Nature Shield — job: protection!' Your collection is complete!"
>
> **Screen**: Final slot fills in with a celebration animation — all 4 photos light up with golden borders. A small fireworks burst. The inspector trail reaches the finish flag. Badge starts assembling.

**Step 4: Collection Complete + Synthesis**

> **AI says**: "*(proud and excited)* Okay, Plant Parts Inspector — look at your whole collection! A sunflower with petals that wave at bees, a [first find] that [job], a [second find] that [job], and a [third find] that [job]. Every part has a job! Now here is the big question — which one of these do you think CHANGES the most over time? Which one grows, opens, or turns into something new?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "The flower bud! It opens up!" / "The seeds change into new plants!" / "The sunflower grew really tall!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "They all change!" / "I do not know."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(mind-blown)* YES! The sunflower started as a tiny seed, grew into a tall stem, and opened into this giant flower — and now the seeds in the middle will fall out and grow into NEW sunflowers! That is so much change from one little seed!"
> 2. "*(playfully)* You know what? You are RIGHT — they ALL change! The leaf grows bigger, the berry gets ripe, the bark gets thicker. But the sunflower might change the MOST — from a tiny seed to this giant flower with hundreds of new seeds inside. One little seed becomes a whole sun!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(guiding gently)* Here is a clue — think about the sunflower. It started as a teeny tiny seed in the dirt. Then it grew a stem, then leaves, then this BIG flower full of new seeds. Does anything else change that much?"
>
> **Screen**: All 4 photos displayed in a row at the top. Below, a simple timeline arrow appears, labeled "Tiny Seed → Sprout → Tall Stem → Big Flower → New Seeds." The sunflower photo gently pulses. Animated growth lines connect the stages. When the child picks a find, that photo moves to the center with a golden glow.

**Step 5: Discovery Celebration**

> **AI says**: "*(amazed and warm)* Inspector, you found FOUR different things — a sunflower, a [find], a [find], and a [find] — and every single one has parts with special jobs! The petals wave at bees, the bark protects the tree, the seeds grow new plants. Why do you think nature gives every part a job to do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "So everything can help!" / "So the plant can grow!" / Any creative reasoning.
> 2. (Unexpected) "I do not know."
> 3. (No response)
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(delighted)* That is such a smart thought! Every part helps the whole plant stay alive and grow. The petals bring bees, the stem stands tall, the seeds make new flowers. When every part does its job, the whole plant works!"
> 2. "*(warmly)* It IS a big wonder! Here is one idea — if the stem did not hold the flower up, bees could not find it. If the petals were not bright, bees would fly past. Every part helps, and together they keep the plant going and growing!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(sharing a secret)* I will tell you something amazing — the sunflower's petals bring bees, the bees help make seeds, and the seeds grow into NEW sunflowers. Every part's job leads to the next part's job. It is like a team where everyone helps!"
>
> **Screen**: The collection displayed beautifully with each photo labeled with its "Part: Job" pair. Gentle animated lines connect parts to their jobs. A soft sun animation warms the background. Tiny animated bees visit the sunflower photo.

**Step 6: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm celebration)* Congratulations, Plant Parts Inspector! You did something really special today. You discovered the **Function** of so many parts — petals wave at bees, stems hold flowers up, bark protects trees, and seeds grow new plants. Every part has a job! And you noticed something even bigger — the sunflower is always **Changing**. It started as a tiny seed, grew into this tall flower, and now it is making new seeds to start all over again. You earned your Plant Parts Patrol Badge!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Engaged) Cheers, talks about wanting to find more parts, or asks about the badge.
> 2. (Quiet) Smiles or says nothing.
> 3. (No response) Child looks at the sunflower quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(encouraging)* Next time you are in the garden, keep your inspector eyes open — every plant, every bug, every tree has parts with secret jobs! See you on the next patrol, Inspector!"
> 2. "*(warm)* Your badge is saved! Remember — every part you see has a job to do. Bye for now, Inspector!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft)* Your inspector badge is shining bright. Bye for now, Inspector!"
>
> **Screen**: A golden "Plant Parts Patrol Badge" appears — shaped like a magnifying glass with the sunflower at the center and the 4 collection photos as small insets around the edges. The words **"Function"** and **"Change"** float up artistically — "Function" styled with gear-like letters and tiny tool icons, "Change" with letters that morph from a seed shape to a flower shape. A soft chime plays. Tiny animated seeds drift upward and sprout into miniature stems.
