## Activity: Goldfish Guess-What Lab

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: Goldfish Guess-What Lab
- **Activity Category**: 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device, Indoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T1 (ages 4–6)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Causation** (Why is it like this?) & **Function** (How does it work?)
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Habitat (designed), Adaptation (designed), Discovery (designed), Prediction (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Thinking Skills (critical thinking, cause-and-effect reasoning), Communication Skills (expressing ideas, listening), Research Skills (observation, data collection)
- **Experience Pillar**: Discovery
- **Game Style**: prediction_lab
- **Trigger Entity**: Goldfish
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs their pet goldfish gliding in a bowl or small tank at home
- **Mapping Source**: none

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: After the child photographs their goldfish, the AI marvels at its gliding, shimmery movement and invites the child to become a "Goldfish Scientist" who runs prediction experiments. In each round, the AI describes something that is about to happen near the goldfish — and the child must COMMIT to a specific prediction of what the goldfish will do BEFORE the AI reveals the answer. Reveals are dramatic and scored: full points for correct predictions, half points for close guesses. A running tally creates genuine stakes: "You're 2 for 3 — can you go perfect?" The final score celebration makes each child feel like a real scientist who tested their ideas.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: A goldfish has a fan-shaped tail that pushes it through water; fins on the sides help it steer and balance; gills on the sides of its head let it breathe underwater; goldfish dart away quickly when startled; goldfish swim upward toward food floating on the surface.
  - **U (Understand)**: When something happens near a goldfish, the fish reacts in a specific way — a cause creates an effect. That is **Causation**. Each body part of the goldfish has a specific job — the tail pushes, the fins steer, the gills breathe, the mouth eats. That is **Function**.
  - **D (Do)**: Practice committing to a prediction before seeing the outcome (critical thinking). Practice describing and reasoning about animal behavior using specific vocabulary (expressing ideas). Practice observing cause-and-effect patterns in a living creature (observation).

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "prediction lab" mechanic transforms fish-watching into a scientific experiment with real stakes. The child does not just guess — they must COMMIT ("I think the goldfish will...") before the AI reveals the dramatic answer. A running score ("2 for 3!") creates genuine tension and excitement. Half-points reward close-but-not-exact guesses, ensuring no child feels they "lost." Each reveal is dramatized with suspense ("drumroll... let's see what happens..."), making the moment of truth thrilling whether the child is right or wrong.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: Child photographs their pet goldfish in a bowl at home. AI notices how it glides and shimmers. Child becomes a Goldfish Scientist who commits to predictions about the goldfish's reactions, then watches dramatic reveals with a running score.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T1]

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs their pet goldfish in a bowl or tank with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted gasp)* Ohhh WOW — a goldfish! Look at it gliding in there, so smooth and sparkly! It is like a tiny golden submarine. I wonder... does your goldfish have a name?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Goldie!" / "Bubbles!" / "Yes, it's [name]!" — child shares a name.
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's swimming!" / "It's orange!" / "I fed it!" — child describes the fish or their experience.
> 3. (No response) Child watches the screen silently.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, impressed)* [Name] — what a perfect name! [Name] looks like a real adventurer in there, zooming and gliding. I bet you know a LOT about how [name] moves. Hey... want to test how much you know? Like a scientist?"
> 2. "*(enthusiastic)* Yes! Look at it go — swimming and gliding! Those little fins are working SO hard. You really watch your fish closely. Hey... I have an idea. Want to be a scientist and TEST what your goldfish does?"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(soft, wondering)* This goldfish is so pretty — those floaty fins waving like tiny flags! I bet it has lots of secrets. Want to do science experiments with me to find out?"
>
> **Screen**: Goldfish photo centered with gentle blue water-ripple animation and tiny bubble animations floating upward. Soft golden shimmer outlines the fish.

**Step 2: Game Setup + Demo — "The Prediction Lab"**

> **AI says**: "*(playful, scientist voice)* Okay, here is how our lab works! I will tell you something that is ABOUT to happen near the goldfish. But before I tell you what the goldfish does — YOU have to lock in your prediction! You say: 'I think the goldfish will...' and then we find out! You get a point if you are right, and HALF a point if you are close. Let me show you!"
>
> *(demo)* "Ready? Here is my example. Something is about to happen: a shadow passes over the bowl. What will the goldfish do? I PREDICT... the goldfish will hide behind the little castle! Let me lock that in..."
>
> *(dramatic pause)* "And the answer is... the goldfish darts to the bottom and stays very still! Ohhh — I said hide behind the castle, but it stayed still at the bottom. That is close! Half point for me! See how it works? Now YOUR turn, Scientist. Ready?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Ready!" / "Yeah!" / "Let's go!" — child is eager.
> 2. (Unexpected) "My fish does that!" / "What's a prediction?" / "I want a point!" — child reacts to the demo.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet, possibly processing.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(excited countdown)* Okay, Scientist — Experiment Number One! Listen carefully..."
> 2. "*(delighted)* You already know what your fish does? Then you are going to be AMAZING at this! A prediction is just a guess you lock in BEFORE you find out. Here comes Experiment Number One!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging, gentle)* It is easy — I say what happens, you guess what the goldfish does. Any guess is great! Here comes the first one..."
>
> **Screen**: Goldfish photo on the left. On the right, a "Prediction Lab" clipboard icon appears with a scoring area: a row of empty circles (one per round) and a running score display reading "Score: 0". A brief demo animation shows: shadow passes, goldfish darts down, "½" appears with a sparkle. "Experiment 1" badge glows below.

**Step 3: Core Loop (3–5 rounds)**

**Round 1 — "The Food Drop" (Experiment 1)**

> **AI says**: "*(mysterious narrator)* Experiment Number One! Imagine... it is feeding time. You pick up the food container and sprinkle tiny flakes on top of the water. They float down slowly. BEFORE I tell you — lock in your prediction! What does the goldfish do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I think the goldfish will swim up!" / "It eats the food!" / "It goes to the top!" — child commits to a prediction about behavior.
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's happy!" / "Yummy!" / "Bubble bubble!" — child gives an emotional response rather than behavioral.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet or looking at the fish bowl.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(confirming lock-in)* Prediction locked: swim up to the top! Okay, here we go... *(drumroll)* ... and the answer is... the goldfish ZOOMS right up to the surface — swish goes that fan-shaped tail, pushing it up like a paddle! YES! You got it! The food CAUSES the fish to swim up — tail swishing all the way! FULL POINT! Score: 1 out of 1!"
> 2. "*(warm, helping lock in)* Ooh happy — so you predict the fish gets excited? Let me lock that in as 'the goldfish gets excited and wiggles.' Okay, here we go... *(drumroll)* ... and the answer is... the goldfish ZOOMS to the surface, tail swishing like crazy! Getting excited — yes! But it also zooms UP. Half point — you got the feeling right, the fish IS excited! But the big move is swimming up to grab the food. Score: half out of 1!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(helpful, encouraging)* Here is a clue — the food is floating at the TOP of the water. Where would the goldfish swim? Up or down? Lock in your guess!"
>
> **Screen**: Goldfish bowl scene. Food flakes drift down from the top. When prediction is locked, a "LOCKED IN" stamp appears on the clipboard. Then a drumroll animation plays — the goldfish zooms upward with tail-swish motion lines. Score circle fills: full gold circle for a full point, half-gold for half. Running score updates at the top: "Score: X / 1". Brief confetti burst for full point.

**Round 2 — "The Big Tap" (Experiment 2)**

> **AI says**: "*(dramatic whisper)* Experiment Number Two! Someone walks by the table and — THUMP — accidentally bumps it. The whole bowl wobbles a tiny bit. Lock in your prediction, Scientist — what does the goldfish do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "I think it swims away fast!" / "It hides!" / "It gets scared and darts!" — child predicts startle behavior.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Oh no!" / "Don't bump it!" / "It makes bubbles!" — child reacts emotionally or guesses wrong.
> 3. (No response) Child is thinking quietly.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(confirming)* Prediction locked: swims away fast! Here we go... *(drumroll)* ... WHOOSH! The goldfish DARTS to the other side of the bowl — super fast! Its side fins spread out wide to help it turn QUICK. The bump CAUSED the fish to flee! FULL POINT! Score: [X] out of 2! You are on fire, Scientist!"
> 2. "*(validating then revealing)* That is so caring — you do not want anyone to scare the fish! Prediction locked as 'the fish gets worried.' Here we go... *(drumroll)* ... the goldfish DARTS away super fast — whoosh! Its side fins spread out to help it turn. It IS worried — you were right about the feeling! And the big move is darting away. Half point for getting the feeling! Score: [X] out of 2!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle hint)* Imagine someone bumps YOUR chair — would you jump? What might the goldfish do if its whole world shakes a little? Lock it in!"
>
> **Screen**: Bowl scene. A hand illustration taps the table — wobble animation on the bowl. "LOCKED IN" stamp. Drumroll. Goldfish darts across the bowl — speed lines and fin-spread animation. Score circle 2 updates. Running score display refreshes. Star burst if full point.

**Round 3 — "The New Shiny Pebble" (Experiment 3)**

> **AI says**: "*(curious, playful)* Experiment Number Three! You drop a brand-new shiny blue pebble into the bowl. Plop! It sinks to the bottom and just sits there, all sparkly. Lock in your prediction — what does the goldfish do about this mystery object?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It swims over to look!" / "It tries to eat it!" / "It pokes it with its nose!" — child predicts curiosity behavior.
> 2. (Unexpected) "It likes it!" / "It's pretty!" / "Blue is its favorite color!" — child reacts to the pebble itself.
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(locking in)* Prediction locked: swims over to look! Here we go... *(drumroll)* ... the goldfish slowly swiiiiims down... gets close... and NIBBLES the pebble with its little mouth! It is investigating! You said 'swims over' — and YES, it did swim over! The nibble is a bonus — that is how goldfish check if something is food. FULL POINT! The shiny pebble CAUSED the fish to investigate. Score: [X] out of 3!"
> 2. "*(warm)* You think it likes it! Prediction locked as 'the goldfish likes the pebble.' Here we go... *(drumroll)* ... the goldfish slowly swims down, gets close, and NIBBLES the pebble with its mouth! Is that liking it? Hmm — it is checking if it is food! Close enough to 'likes it' — it IS interested! Half point! Score: [X] out of 3!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging)* A new sparkly thing just appeared in the bowl! Would the goldfish ignore it, swim away, or swim closer? Lock in your guess!"
>
> **Screen**: Bowl scene. A shiny blue pebble drops in — plop animation and ripples. "LOCKED IN" stamp. Drumroll. Goldfish swims down and nibbles the pebble — curious investigation animation. Score circle 3 updates. Running tally displays prominently.

**Round 4 — "The Calm Dark Room" (Experiment 4) — Surprise Round**

> **AI says**: "*(soft, mysterious)* Last experiment — this one is tricky! It is nighttime. The lights go off. The room is dark and very, very quiet. No food. No bumps. No new pebbles. Just... darkness and silence. Lock in your prediction — what does the goldfish do?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "It goes to sleep!" / "It stops swimming!" / "It slows down and rests!" — child predicts calming behavior.
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's scared of the dark!" / "It can't see!" / "It hides!" — child predicts fear.
> 3. (No response) Child is unsure — this is a harder scenario.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(locking in)* Prediction locked: slows down and rests! Here we go... *(slow, gentle drumroll)* ... and... the goldfish... slooooows way down. Its tail barely moves. It floats gently near the bottom... resting. YES! FULL POINT! When it is dark and calm, there is nothing CAUSING the fish to zoom or dart. So it rests! Score: [X] out of 4! WOW, Scientist!"
> 2. "*(locking in)* Prediction locked: scared of the dark! Interesting theory! Here we go... *(slow drumroll)* ... and... the goldfish... slooooows way down. Its tail barely moves. It just... floats quietly near the bottom. Not scared — just peaceful! It is resting! No food, no bumps, no new things — nothing to CAUSE a reaction. So the fish just chills! Not quite your prediction, but a really smart guess. Half point — being careful about the dark IS smart thinking! Score: [X] out of 4!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle hint)* This is a hard one! Think about it — there is no food, no bumps, no new things. Nothing is making the fish react. Would it zoom around? Or would it be... calm? Lock it in!"
>
> **Screen**: Bowl scene transitions to a dark blue/purple nighttime palette. Lights dim. "LOCKED IN" stamp. Slow, gentle drumroll. Goldfish gradually slows — movement becomes minimal, floating gently near the bottom. Score circle 4 updates. Full running tally visible: "Final Score: X out of 4". If full marks, extra confetti sparkle.

**Step 4: Payoff — The Magic Moment (Final Score Reveal)**

> **AI says**: "*(building suspense)* Okay, Scientist... the experiments are DONE. Time to count your score! You ran four experiments on your goldfish. You predicted what it would do when food drops... when the table bumps... when a pebble appears... and when the lights go out. And your final score is..."
>
> *(dramatic pause, drumroll)*
>
> "...[X] out of 4! [SCORE-SPECIFIC REACTION]"
>
> **Score-specific reactions**:
> - **4/4 (perfect)**: "FOUR OUT OF FOUR! PERFECT SCORE! You predicted EVERY single thing the goldfish would do! You know this fish better than ANYONE. You are a goldfish GENIUS!"
> - **3–3.5/4**: "That is AMAZING! You got almost every one right! Your goldfish predictions were SO close — you really understand how this fish thinks!"
> - **2–2.5/4**: "Two and a half points — that is great for a first-time scientist! You figured out the food drop AND the bump — that is real cause-and-effect thinking!"
> - **1–1.5/4**: "You know what? Even real scientists do not get everything right the first time. You had some AWESOME half-point guesses — that means you were thinking the right way!"
> - **0.5/4 or 0/4**: "Here is a secret — the BEST scientists learn the most when their predictions are WRONG! You learned four incredible things about goldfish today. That is the REAL score!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Yay!" / "I got [X]!" / "I'm a scientist!" / Cheers with excitement.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can we do more?" / "I want to try again!" / "I knew it!"
> 3. (No response) Child smiles or looks pleased.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(beaming)* The BEST Goldfish Scientist! Your goldfish is lucky to have someone who understands it so well."
> 2. "*(delighted)* You want MORE experiments? That is exactly what real scientists say! We can run more experiments another day. But first — let me tell you something amazing about what you just did..."
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(warm whisper)* You know what? I think your goldfish can tell you have been paying attention. That is what great scientists do."
>
> **Screen**: Giant score display in the center — "[X] / 4" in bold golden numbers. Each experiment appears as a mini-icon below (food flakes, tapping hand, blue pebble, moon) with the score circle beside it (full gold, half gold, or empty). If perfect score: massive confetti explosion and star shower. Any score: the goldfish photo glows with a proud golden frame. "GOLDFISH SCIENTIST" title banner appears above the score.

**Step 5: Celebration + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective pride)* You know what you did today? Every time I told you something was about to happen — food drops, a bump, a pebble, the lights — you PREDICTED what the goldfish would do before I even told you. You figured out that causes make effects! Food CAUSES the fish to swim up. A bump CAUSES the fish to dart away. A new thing CAUSES the fish to investigate. And quiet CAUSES the fish to rest. That is called Causation — when one thing CAUSES another thing to happen!"
>
> "And you noticed something else — the tail PUSHES, the side fins STEER, the mouth NIBBLES, the gills BREATHE. Every part has its own job! That is called Function — how each part works! You are not just a scientist — you are a goldfish expert!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Causation!" / "I'm a scientist!" / "Function!" / Repeats a word happily.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Can I feed my fish now?" / "Bye goldfish!" / "I love my goldfish!"
> 3. (No response) Child listens or looks at the screen.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(celebrating)* That is right — Causation and Function! You earned your badge, Goldfish Scientist. Go say hi to your fish — I bet it knows you understand it now!"
> 2. "*(warm, laughing)* Go ahead — your goldfish is so lucky to have such a smart scientist! See you next time, Goldfish Scientist!"
> 3. *(waits 2 seconds)* "*(gentle closing)* Great job today, Scientist. Your goldfish has a really smart friend. See you next time!"
>
> **Screen**: "GOLDFISH SCIENTIST" badge centered with the goldfish photo as a portrait inside. Below the badge, "Causation" and "Function" appear in golden lettering — "Causation" with a chain-link icon, "Function" with a gear icon. The four experiment icons appear as small trophies around the badge. Soft golden sparkle animations. A gentle "The End" ribbon scrolls across the bottom.
