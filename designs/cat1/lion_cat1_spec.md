## Activity: The Lion's Big Show

### A. Basic Info

- **Activity Name**: The Lion's Big Show
- **Activity Category**: Category 1 — Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device, Indoor)
- **Recommended Tier**: T0 (ages 2–4)
- **Core IB Key Concepts**: **Perspective**, **Form**
- **Related Concepts (Discipline)**: Expression (designed), Identity (designed), Creativity (designed), Discovery (designed)
- **ATL Skills Focus**: Communication Skills (expressing, listening), Thinking Skills (creative thinking), Self-Management Skills (emotional regulation)
- **Experience Pillar**: Performance
- **Game Style**: voice_stage
- **Trigger Entity**: Lion (toy or picture)
- **Trigger Scene**: Child photographs their toy lion on the playroom floor. AI gasps at the big fuzzy mane and invites the child to put on a show where THEY become the lion's voice and a crowd of silly judges reacts.
- **Mapping Source**: none

### B. Activity Overview

- **① Brief Description**: The child becomes a "Roar Star" who performs AS the lion in front of a silly audience of jungle judges (a parrot, a monkey, and an elephant). Each round, the AI sets a performance challenge — roar a greeting, roar a lullaby, do a tiny whisper-roar — and the audience of judges reacts with over-the-top delight, gasps, or laughter. The challenges escalate with a surprise twist, and the finale is a standing ovation where every judge goes wild. The child feels "They LOVED it!" — a true Performance pillar experience.

- **② Educational Purpose (KUD)**:
  - **K (Know)**: (1) Lions have a big fuzzy mane, (2) lions make a loud roaring sound, (3) a roar can say different things — hello, goodnight, surprise, (4) lions are very big cats, (5) the word "audience" means people (or animals!) watching a show
  - **U (Understand)**: (1) The same lion can roar in many different ways depending on the situation — happy, sleepy, surprised — and each way feels different to the audience; that is **Perspective** (the audience hears the same roar differently depending on how you perform it). (2) The lion has a recognizable **Form** — big mane, strong body, loud voice — and the child uses that form to create expressive performances.
  - **D (Do)**: (1) Express emotions through voice and sound in an escalating series of challenges, (2) listen to performance prompts and respond creatively with vocal variety, (3) adapt performance style when a surprise twist changes the rules

- **③ Design Highlight**: The "Jungle Talent Show" metaphor transforms voice play into a PERFORMANCE with real stakes. Three silly jungle judges (Parrot, Monkey, Elephant) react to every roar with over-the-top audience responses — "The parrot fell off the branch!" or "The monkey is doing a happy dance!" This is NOT the old voice_acting where the child just speaks as the lion. Here, there is an AUDIENCE who reacts, creating the feeling of "They loved it!" A surprise twist in Round 3 ("Now do it WHISPERING!") adds genuine challenge and delight.

- **④ Typical Scenario**: A 2–4 year old photographs their toy lion. AI marvels at the mane and introduces the Jungle Talent Show: three silly judges are waiting for the lion's performance. The child performs as the lion in escalating challenges while the audience of judges reacts with increasing excitement, culminating in a standing ovation.

### C. Interaction Flow — Detailed Design [Target Tier: T0]

**Step 1b: Transition Bridge — Cold Start**

> **Context**: Child photographs a toy lion with no prior conversation.
>
> **AI says**: "*(delighted gasp)* Whoa! A LION! Look at that big fuzzy mane! I bet this lion has the BIGGEST roar. Can you show me? What does your lion sound like?"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "RRRR!" or "RAWR!" — child roars enthusiastically.
> 2. (Unexpected) "It's my toy!" or "That's Leo!" or "It's soft!"
> 3. (No response) Child stares at the screen or touches the toy lion.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(amazed, jaw-drop tone)* WOW! That was SO loud! You know what? I think some jungle friends heard that roar. They want MORE! Want to put on a BIG show?"
> 2. "*(warm, excited)* Your lion! So fuzzy! I bet it has a big roar hiding inside. Want to help it do a BIG show? Some silly judges want to hear it!"
> 3. *(Wait 2 seconds)* "*(playful whisper)* Psst... I think your lion wants to say RRRR! Can you help? Some jungle friends are waiting!"
>
> **Screen**: Lion photo centered on screen with warm golden savanna-grass illustration framing the edges. Soft golden sparkles drift around the lion's mane. Three small shadowed animal silhouettes (parrot, monkey, elephant) peek in from the right edge, as if curious.

**Step 2: Game Setup + Demo — "The Jungle Talent Show"**

> **AI says**: "*(excited announcer voice)* Welcome to the Jungle Talent Show! Three judges are here! Parrot... Monkey... and big Elephant! They want to hear YOUR lion roar! I'll tell you HOW to roar. You roar! Judges go CRAZY! Watch — lion says HELLO! RRRR! Ohhh! Parrot fell off the branch! Ha! Your turn now!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child laughs, says "OK!" or roars "RRRR!" eagerly.
> 2. (Unexpected) "What's a judge?" or "I want monkey!" or child giggles.
> 3. (No response) Child watches quietly with interest.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(cheering)* YES! Here comes Round One! Judges are ready! Parrot is sitting on her branch. Monkey has popcorn. Elephant has big ears open. Let's GO!"
> 2. "*(warm, playful)* Judges are silly friends watching the show! Monkey loves it! Ready? You roar, judges go WOW! Here comes Round One!"
> 3. *(Wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging, gentle)* It's easy! Just roar when I say! Judges love EVERY roar! Ready? Here comes the first one!"
>
> **Screen**: "JUNGLE TALENT SHOW" banner in bright jungle-leaf lettering at top. Lion photo at center-left on a small stage platform. Three judge characters appear on the right: a colorful parrot on a branch, a monkey with tiny popcorn, and an elephant with big flappy ears — all drawn in friendly cartoon style with eager faces. A "Round 1" star badge glows in the corner.

**Step 3: Core Loop (3 rounds)**

> **Performance Escalation**: Round 1 is simple and high-success (happy roar). Round 2 raises the stakes (sleepy roar — quieter, contrasting). Round 3 introduces a SURPRISE TWIST (whisper-roar — the hardest and funniest challenge).

**Round 1 — "The Happy Hello Roar":**

> **AI says**: "*(bright announcer)* Round One! Sun is up! Lion is happy! Show the judges your HAPPIEST roar! Ready? GO!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "RRRR!" or "RAWR!" or "Hiiiii!" — child roars or shouts happily.
> 2. (Unexpected) "Meow!" or a silly sound, or "I'm a cat!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet or shy.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(audience goes wild)* OHHH! Parrot fell off the branch — AGAIN! Monkey is jumping up and down! Elephant flapped his ears SO hard the popcorn flew! They LOVED that happy roar! The judges give you... a BIG gold star!"
> 2. "*(delighted, laughing)* Meow?! Ha! The judges LOVE surprises! Parrot is giggling so hard she's spinning! Monkey says 'Do it again!' Even the elephant is smiling! Gold star for the surprise sound!"
> 3. *(Wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, encouraging)* Even a tiny roar counts! Try a little one... rrrr? The judges are waiting with their big silly faces!"
>
> **Screen**: Stage spotlight on lion photo; golden sun animation rises behind. When child responds: parrot character tumbles off branch (comedic wobble animation), monkey bounces with popcorn flying, elephant flaps ears with a swoosh effect. A gold star stamps in the score area. "Round 1 COMPLETE" sparkles.

**Round 2 — "The Sleepy Lullaby Roar":**

> **AI says**: "*(soft, dreamy narrator)* Round Two! Night time! Moon is out. Lion is sooo sleepy. Can you do a sleeeepy roar? Soft and slow... like a lullaby. Ready? Go!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "Rrrrr..." (soft, drawn-out) or "Shhh... rrrr" — child does a quiet roar.
> 2. (Unexpected) "RRRR!" — child does a loud roar instead, or "Night night!"
> 3. (No response) Child is quiet or processing.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(hushed, amazed)* Ohhh... look! Parrot's eyes are closing. Monkey put down the popcorn and is yawning. Elephant is swaying... back and forth... back and forth. Your sleepy roar made the WHOLE audience almost fall asleep! That was beautiful! Second gold star!"
> 2. "*(laughing quietly)* A BIG roar! Ha! The judges jumped awake! Monkey spilled the popcorn everywhere! That wasn't sleepy, but the judges LOVED the surprise! Elephant says 'Whoa! Wide awake now!' Star for the wake-up call!"
> 3. *(Wait 2 seconds)* "*(gentle, modeling)* Try it like this... rrrrrr... nice and soft, like you're tucking the lion in. Even a tiny whisper counts. The judges have their blankets ready!"
>
> **Screen**: Stage dims to moonlit blue-purple tones; a crescent moon rises behind the lion photo. When child responds softly: parrot droops on branch with half-closed eyes, monkey yawns with popcorn lowered, elephant sways gently. A second gold star stamps in. If child roars loudly: judges startle awake comically (parrot pops up, monkey drops popcorn, elephant jumps). "Round 2 COMPLETE" in soft starlight letters.

**Round 3 — "The SURPRISE Whisper-Roar!" (Twist Challenge):**

> **AI says**: "*(dramatic, conspiratorial whisper)* Round Three! SURPRISE CHALLENGE! The judges say... can the lion do a WHISPER roar? Shhh! The tiniest, quietest, most secret roar EVER! This is the HARDEST one! Nobody thinks you can do it! Can you? Shhhh... GO!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) "...rrrr..." (tiny whisper) or "psst... rawr" — child attempts a whisper roar.
> 2. (Unexpected) "RRRR!" — child does a big roar instead, or laughs, or says "I can't whisper!"
> 3. (No response) Child giggles, covers mouth, or is quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(absolutely stunned, building to explosion)* Wait... did you hear that? Parrot leaned in... Monkey leaned in... Elephant put his GIANT ear right up close... and... THEY HEARD IT! A WHISPER ROAR! The crowd is going BANANAS! Monkey is doing backflips! Parrot is screeching 'ENCORE! ENCORE!' Elephant STOMPED so hard the whole jungle shook! Nobody thought it was possible! THIRD GOLD STAR!"
> 2. "*(delighted, amazed)* You did a BIG roar instead! And guess what? The judges LOVED the surprise! Parrot says 'That's NOT a whisper — that's a SUPER roar!' Monkey fell over laughing! The elephant says 'My ears are still ringing!' They give you the star for being BRAVE! Gold star number three!"
> 3. *(Wait 2 seconds)* "*(encouraging whisper)* The quiet... IS the whisper roar! Even silence can be a performance! Parrot is leaning in so close she almost fell off again! Try a teensy tiny one? Even just... 'rr'?"
>
> **Screen**: Stage goes dramatic — a single spotlight narrows on the lion photo. "SURPRISE CHALLENGE" banner flashes in bold red-gold letters. When child whispers: judges lean forward in exaggerated poses (parrot stretches neck, monkey cups hand to ear, elephant tilts giant ear). Then EXPLOSION of celebration — confetti burst, parrot spirals, monkey backflips, elephant trumpets with trunk up. Third gold star stamps in with extra sparkle. "Round 3 COMPLETE — ALL STARS!" flashes.

**Step 4: Payoff — Standing Ovation (The Magic Moment)**

> **AI says**: "*(building excitement, announcer crescendo)* Three rounds! Three gold stars! And now... ALL the judges are standing up! Parrot is flying in circles screeching 'BRAVO! BRAVO!' Monkey is throwing popcorn in the air like confetti! Elephant is TRUMPETING so loud the trees are shaking! The whole jungle is cheering! STANDING OVATION for the lion! You are the ROAR STAR!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child roars triumphantly, giggles, claps, or says "Yay! I did it!"
> 2. (Unexpected) "Again! More!" or "I want to do another one!" or "Encore!"
> 3. (No response) Child beams, smiles wide, or watches the celebration animation with delight.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(overjoyed)* The BEST Roar Star the jungle has EVER seen! Every judge says you win! The parrot wants your autograph!"
> 2. "*(thrilled)* ENCORE? The judges are SO happy! They say 'come back anytime!' You can roar for them again another day, Roar Star!"
> 3. *(Wait 2 seconds)* "*(warm, celebratory)* Look at that big smile! The judges see it too. Parrot says 'That smile is the best encore!' You are a real Roar Star!"
>
> **Screen**: Full celebration scene: confetti rains across the entire screen. Parrot spirals around the lion photo trailing rainbow sparkles. Monkey throws cartoon popcorn confetti. Elephant raises trunk with golden trumpet-blast rays. A large golden "ROAR STAR" badge descends from the top of the screen with the lion photo framed inside it, three gold stars underneath. Triumphant jungle fanfare plays. The crowd (silhouette animals in the background) throws hats in the air.

**Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts**

> **AI says**: "*(warm, reflective, gently amazed)* Wow, Roar Star. You showed the judges that one lion can sound SO many ways! Happy roar, sleepy roar, whisper roar — every roar was different! The same lion, different feelings. That's called Perspective — one lion, many voices! And they loved the big mane, the loud voice, the fuzzy shape — that's what makes a lion a lion. That's called Form! You are the Roar Star of the Jungle Talent Show!"
>
> **Possible child responses**:
> 1. (Ideal) Child roars one more time, says "Bye lion!" or "I'm a Roar Star!" or smiles happily.
> 2. (Unexpected) "What's perspective?" or "I want to be a monkey!" or asks a question about lions.
> 3. (No response) Child watches the screen, content and quiet.
>
> **AI follow-up**:
> 1. "*(warm, proud)* RRRR! That's lion saying 'Thank you, Roar Star!' Bye bye! The judges are waving!"
> 2. "*(warm, simple)* Perspective means same lion, different roars — and that's what YOU showed! And monkeys are fun too — maybe next time! Bye, Roar Star!"
> 3. *(Wait 2 seconds)* "*(soft, closing)* The judges are waving goodbye. Parrot says 'See you next time!' Bye bye, Roar Star. RRRR!"
>
> **Screen**: Lion photo at center with "Perspective" (speech-bubble icon with multiple expressions inside) and "Form" (lion-silhouette icon) in artistic jungle-leaf lettering. "ROAR STAR" badge glows in the corner with three gold stars. The three judges wave from the side of the screen. Warm golden glow fades to soft savanna sunset as the scene closes.
