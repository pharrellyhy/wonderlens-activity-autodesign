### B. Activity Overview

**① Brief Description**

The child becomes a "Secret Eye Detective" who investigates how their own eye works by solving a series of mystery cases. Each case presents a real-world scenario where the eye does something remarkable, and the child must figure out WHY, using clues from their own observation and reasoning. The game blends imaginative detective play with real optical science grounded in the child's own body.

**② Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know)**:
  1. The pupil changes size to control how much light enters the eye
  2. The iris is a colored ring with tiny muscles that change pupil size
  3. Special cells in the eye help the brain tell colors apart
  4. Blinking is a reflex that protects and cleans the eye surface
  5. Eyes work as a team so the brain can judge distance
- **U (Understand)**:
  1. What you see depends on where you stand, how close you are, and what direction you look — that is Perspective
  2. Each part of the eye has a specific job that helps you see clearly — that is Function
- **D (Do)**:
  1. Generate creative explanations for real phenomena
  2. Observe their own eye and describe what they notice
  3. Reason about cause-and-effect relationships in how the eye works

**③ Design Highlight**

The detective metaphor transforms anatomy learning into a mystery game. Each "case" is a real optical phenomenon the child has experienced (adjusting to dark rooms, watery eyes in wind, blurry close-ups), and the child solves it by reasoning about how the eye works. The child is never tested — they are the expert detective filing reports.

**④ Typical Scenario**

A 6–8-year-old photographs their own eye in a mirror, marvels at the detail, and becomes a detective investigating mysteries of how their eye protects itself and helps them see the world.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T2 (ages 6–8)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (amazed, leaning-in) "Whoa — look at THAT! You just captured something incredible. That's YOUR eye staring right back at you, with all those tiny details. It's like discovering a hidden world right on your own face! What's the first thing you notice when you look at it?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I can see the colors! And the black dot in the middle."
2. (Unexpected) "It looks weird up close."
3. (No response) Child stares at the photo quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (delighted) "Yes! That black dot is called your pupil, and the colorful part is your iris — and they both have secret jobs! What if we investigated those secrets like detectives?"
2. (playful, validating) "It DOES look a little weird up close, doesn't it? Most things look surprising when you zoom in that much! But here's the cool part — your eye is actually doing amazing things right now that you can't even see. Want to be a detective and figure out what those secret things are?"
3. (wait 2s) (warm, curious) "Take a good look — do you see that dark circle in the middle? That little circle is actually changing size RIGHT NOW while you look at it. Pretty mysterious, right?"

**Screen:** Child's eye photo centered with a gentle zoom-in animation, soft sparkle particles drifting across the iris, and warm ambient lighting.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (official but playful) "Welcome to the Secret Eye Detective Bureau! Here's how it works: I'll give you a mystery case about something strange your eye does. You think about it, and then tell me your detective theory for WHY it happens. Ready? Let me show you with Case Zero..."

(dramatic pause) "Case Zero: Sometimes when you wake up in the morning and open your eyes, everything looks super bright and you have to squint. Why do you think that happens?"

(modeling) "Hmm, my detective theory is... maybe the eye was resting in the dark all night, so it opened up wide to catch more light — and then BOOM, morning light rushes in and it's too much! What do you think — does that make sense?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yeah! Because your eyes were used to the dark!"
2. (Unexpected) "I don't squint in the morning."
3. (No response) Child is thinking quietly.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (impressed) "Exactly right, detective! Your pupil gets bigger in the dark to let in more light, and when morning comes, it needs a second to shrink back down. That's your first clue about how the eye works! Ready for your first real case?"
2. (curious, accepting) "That's interesting — maybe your room has curtains that let light in slowly, so your eyes have time to adjust! Either way, your pupil IS changing size all the time depending on the light. Now you know the trick — ready to solve your first real case?"
3. (wait 2s) (encouraging) "No rush, detective! Here's a hint — think about what the dark circle in the middle of your eye might be doing while you sleep. It gets bigger! And then morning light is a surprise. Make sense? Let's try a real case now!"

**Screen:** A playful "detective case file" card beside the eye photo with "CASE ZERO" stamped on top; a simple pupil animation expands and contracts with sun/moon icons.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1 — "The Watery Wind Mystery":**

**AI says:** (mysterious briefing) "Case One: You're riding your bike on a windy day, and suddenly your eyes start watering even though you're not sad at all. Your eyes are making tears for NO emotional reason! Detective, what's your theory — why would your eyes water when it's windy?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Maybe the wind is drying them out, so the tears keep them wet?"
2. (Unexpected) "Because dust gets in your eyes from the wind."
3. (No response) Child is thinking.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (amazed) "Brilliant theory, detective! That's exactly it — your eye has a thin wet layer on its surface, and wind can dry it out. So your eye makes extra tears to protect that layer! It's like your eye has its own sprinkler system that turns on automatically. Case One: SOLVED!"
2. (validating, building) "Oh, that's a great theory too! Dust AND dryness — you actually got TWO reasons! Wind can blow tiny particles toward your eyes, and it also dries the surface out. Either way, your eyes make extra tears to wash and protect themselves. Double solve, detective!"
3. (wait 2s) (helpful hint) "Here's a clue — think about what wind does to a puddle of water on the sidewalk. It dries it up, right? Now imagine a tiny layer of water sitting on your eyeball..."

**Screen:** Case file card "CASE ONE: THE WATERY WIND MYSTERY" with a cartoon eye, wind lines, and tear drops; green "SOLVED" stamp appears when resolved.

**Round 2 — "The Color Fade Mystery":** Child enters a dark room and colors look gray; they reason that the eye needs light to see colors, learning about rod and cone cells that switch teams depending on brightness.

**Round 3 — "The Impossible Close-Up":** Child holds a finger close to their nose and it goes blurry; they discover the eye has a tiny lens that changes shape to focus but has a limit on how thick it can squeeze.

**Rounds 4–5 — Open-ended:** Child proposes their own eye mystery and the AI helps them investigate it together.

#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (proud, ceremonial) "Attention! By the authority of the Secret Eye Detective Bureau, I am proud to award you the title of... Eye Mystery Investigator! You solved real cases about how your own eye protects itself, adjusts to light, detects colors, and focuses. Not many detectives can say they investigated their own body and cracked the code!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes! I'm an Eye Detective!" / laughs with pride
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do more cases?"
3. (No response) Child smiles or looks at the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (celebratory) "The best Eye Mystery Investigator I've ever worked with! Every time you blink, squint, or notice your eyes watering — you'll know exactly why."
2. (delighted) "I love that you want more! Keep watching your eyes in the mirror and noticing what they do in different situations — you're building new cases all the time, detective!"
3. (wait 2s) (warm) "That badge looks good on you, detective. You earned every bit of it."

**Screen:** Golden detective badge with "EYE MYSTERY INVESTIGATOR" engraved, child's eye photo inset in the badge center, confetti particles and subtle fanfare.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (warm, reflective) "You know what I think is the most amazing thing you figured out today? You discovered that your eye sees the world differently depending on where you are — bright rooms, dark rooms, close up, far away. The same eye, but different views every time. That's the power of Perspective — what you see changes based on how and where you look."

(building, appreciative) "And you figured out that every part of your eye — the pupil, the lens, the tear layer, those tiny color cells — each one has its own special job that keeps everything working together. That's Function — understanding HOW each piece does its part. Detective, you didn't just solve mysteries today. You discovered something about who you are and how YOU see the world."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "My eyes are so cool!" / reflects on what they learned
2. (Unexpected) "What's perspective again?"
3. (No response) Child listens thoughtfully.

**AI follow-up:**

1. (proud, closing) "They really are. And now you'll notice your eyes doing amazing things every single day. Case files closed, detective — until next time!"
2. (patient, clear) "Perspective means that what you see depends on where you are and how you look. Like how the same room looks bright or dark depending on when you walk in! Your eyes showed you that today. Case closed, detective!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle closing) "Case files closed, detective. Your eyes will keep solving mysteries for you every day — now you just know how to notice them. Until next time!"

**Screen:** "PERSPECTIVE" and "FUNCTION" in elegant bold typography; "PERSPECTIVE" framed by two eyes with light beams, "FUNCTION" surrounded by labeled icons (pupil, lens, tear drop, color cell), detective badge below with eye photo inset.
