## Rainy Day Feelings

### A. Basic Info

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Activity Name | Rainy Day Feelings |
| Activity Category | Sustained Verbal Interaction (In-Device) |
| Recommended Tier | T0 (ages 2–4) |
| Core IB Key Concepts | Perspective, Connection |
| Related Concepts | Expression, Identity, Creativity, Discovery |
| ATL Skills Focus | Communication Skills (listening, expressing), Self-Management Skills (emotional regulation), Thinking Skills (creative) |
| Game Style | voice_acting |

### B. Activity Overview

**① Brief Description**

The child's raincoat becomes a magical "feeling coat." AI presents tiny rainy-day moments — splashing in a puddle, hearing rain on the hood, wind blowing — and the child says how the raincoat feels in each one. The raincoat "talks" through the child, turning sensory moments into feelings words.

**② Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Raincoats are smooth and slippery; they keep you dry; they make a swish-swish sound; rain feels different with and without a coat; a hood covers your head
- **U (Understand):** The same raincoat can feel different in different moments — cozy when it's cold, splashy when puddles come (Perspective). Weather and clothing are connected — rain means we need our coat (Connection).
- **D (Do):** Name feelings in response to scenarios; connect sensory cues to emotions; listen to short prompts and respond

**③ Design Highlight**

The raincoat "whispers" its feelings to the child — the child becomes the coat's voice. Each tiny rainy scene uses a different sound (pitter-patter, swish-swish, whoosh) as an onomatopoeia hook before the child names a feeling. This turns emotional vocabulary into a sensory game.

**④ Typical Scenario**

A 2–4 year old photographs their yellow raincoat by the door. AI reacts with delight to the bright color and invites the child to be the raincoat's "feelings friend" — voicing how the coat feels in little rainy moments.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T0 (ages 2–4)

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** (delighted surprise) "Ooh! A yellow coat! So bright and shiny! Do you like it?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes!" / "I like it!" / "It's mine!"
2. (Unexpected) "It's for rain" / "Mommy's coat"
3. (No response) Child is silent

**AI follow-up:**

1. (warm) "Me too! Cozy coat! Let's play!"
2. (impressed) "For rain! So smart! Let's play!"
3. (wait 2s) (playful whisper) "That coat looks so cozy! Swish-swish!"

**Screen:** Raincoat photo centered with gentle rain-drop animation and a small sunshine icon in the corner.


#### Step 2: Rule Introduction + Demo

**AI says:** (excited, conspiratorial whisper) "Guess what? Coat has feelings! Pitter-patter rain. Happy coat! Your turn!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Happy!" / "Yay!" / repeats "pitter-patter"
2. (Unexpected) "Coat can't talk!" / "No it doesn't"
3. (No response) Child is quiet

**AI follow-up:**

1. (celebrating) "Yes! Happy coat! You heard it! Let's do more!"
2. (playful gasp) "What if it could? Let's pretend!"
3. (wait 2s) (gentle) "Pitter-patter! The coat smiles! What does it feel?"

**Screen:** Raincoat photo with animated smiley face and tiny blue raindrops with "pitter-patter" text bouncing below.


#### Step 3: Multi-Round Interaction

**Round 1 — Splashy Puddle:**

**AI says:** (playful, bouncy) "Splash-splash! Big puddle! How feels coat?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Happy!" / "Excited!" / "Wet!" / "Fun!"
2. (Unexpected) "I don't like puddles" / "Yucky" / "Woof!"
3. (No response) Child is quiet

**AI follow-up:**

1. (cheering) "Splash-splash! Coat loves it!"
2. (warm, validating) "Yucky puddle! Coat says brrr!"
3. (wait 2s) (encouraging) "Splash-splash! Wet or dry? How does the coat feel?"

**Screen:** Raincoat photo with animated blue puddle splashing and water droplets bouncing upward.

**Round 2 — Windy Whoosh:** The wind goes whoooosh and the coat flaps; the child names how the coat feels (cold, scared, silly, flappy). AI celebrates the feeling word.


#### Step 4: Celebration

**AI says:** (big warm celebration) "Wow! Feelings Friend! You heard coat feelings!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) Laughs, claps, says "Yay!" or "I did it!"
2. (Unexpected) "More!" / "Again!" / repeats sounds
3. (No response) Child is quiet but smiling

**AI follow-up:**

1. (proud) "Best Feelings Friend ever!"
2. (warm) "More? Coat loves you!"
3. (gentle cheer) "Coat says thanks!"

**Screen:** Golden "Feelings Friend" badge with a tiny raincoat icon and confetti animation.


#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** (warm, proud) "Same coat, new feelings. That's Perspective! You and coat belong together. That's Connection!"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) Smiles, says "bye coat!" or waves
2. (Unexpected) "I want to wear it!" / "More game!"
3. (No response) Child looks at screen

**AI follow-up:**

1. (warm) "Bye-bye, Feelings Friend! See you next rainy day!"
2. (delighted) "Go wear it! Swish-swish!"
3. (soft, closing) "Bye-bye, coat! Bye-bye, Feelings Friend!"

**Screen:** Raincoat photo centered with "Perspective" in a cloud shape and "Connection" in a raindrop shape floating artistically, connected by a soft rainbow arc. "Feelings Friend" badge in the corner.
