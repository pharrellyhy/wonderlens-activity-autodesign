## Two-Color Mix Lab

| Field | Value |

### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child collects three two-color pairs, predicts each result, and watches the set become a small color-mix wheel.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know that two chosen colors can combine into a different result color.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that each collected color pair causes its own mix result.
- **D (Do):** Children collect a two-color pair, predict the result, and save the revealed result to the set.

**3. Design Highlight**

The repeated action is collecting color pairs. Each round saves a pair and result, so the final reveal is earned by a set of child-built examples rather than one isolated prediction.

**4. Typical Scenario**

The child names or finds red plus yellow, blue plus yellow, and red plus blue. Each pair gets a prediction and a result stamp on the lab board.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm invitation] "Two colors are ready for a tiny experiment. Want to be the Color Mix Tester?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes" or nods.
2. (Unexpected) "What mission?" or gives an unrelated idea.
3. (No response) Child watches or stays quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. We will collect color pairs, then make a guess before each reveal."
2. [friendly] "It is a color lab mission. You choose two colors at a time, and each pair joins the board."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "You can point to a color or say its name."

**Screen:** The activity badge "Color Mix Tester" appears with three empty progress tokens. If `color_mixing_board_01` is available, show it as support; if not, use voice-only fallback and do not claim the asset is visible.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction

**AI says:** [clear guide tone] "Lab rule: collect a pair, guess the mix, save the result. Three pairs make a color wheel."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay."
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do it my way?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Round one is ready."
2. [validating] "Yes, your way can help. We will still save each pair before the result."
3. [wait 2s] [modeling] "I will start, and you can copy my first answer if you want."

**Screen:** A simple rule strip appears: Listen or look, answer, save a token. Token 1 pulses.

#### Step 3: Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Collect Red + Yellow:**

**AI says:** [focused lab tone] "First pair: collect red and yellow. Point to them, name them, or find objects with those colors. What do you think they will make?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Red and yellow make orange."
2. (Unexpected) "Banana." / "Purple."
3. (No response) Child looks at the color spots or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Red plus yellow is collected, and orange is the result guess. Token 1 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Banana gives us yellow. We still need the red partner, then the mix points to orange."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try this pair with me: red plus yellow makes orange."

**Screen:** Pair slot 1 fills with red and yellow dots. A small result stamp appears as orange after the AI reveal. Token 1 fills with "red+yellow."

**Round 2 -- Collect Blue + Yellow:**

**AI says:** [curious lab tone] "Second pair: collect blue and yellow. Which result should this pair make?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Green."
2. (Unexpected) "Orange again." / "I only found blue."
3. (No response) Child studies the board.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Blue plus yellow is collected, and green is the result. Token 2 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Orange belonged to the first pair. For this pair, blue needs yellow to make green."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try this pair with me: blue plus yellow makes green."

**Screen:** Pair slot 2 fills with blue and yellow dots. The result stamp flips to green, and token 2 fills with "blue+yellow."

**Round 3 -- Collect Red + Blue:**

**AI says:** [building suspense] "Final pair: collect red and blue. What result should join our set?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Purple."
2. (Unexpected) "Green." / "I want rainbow."
3. (No response) Child looks between the red and blue spots.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Red plus blue is collected, and purple completes the set. Token 3 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Green was blue plus yellow. Red plus blue points us to purple."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try this pair with me: red plus blue makes purple."

**Screen:** Pair slot 3 fills with red and blue dots. The result stamp flips to purple. All three pair slots pulse in order.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [delighted reveal] "Look at that. Your collected pairs made a color wheel: red plus yellow made orange, blue plus yellow made green, and red plus blue made purple."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I made it" or explains the result.
2. (Unexpected) "Can we change one?"
3. (No response) Child watches the reveal.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [celebrating] "Yes. Your collected pairs caused this color-wheel reveal."
2. [replay invitation] "That is a great next-round idea. This version shows the path you made first."
3. [wait 2s] [soft recap] "I will read the three tokens back so you can see your path."

**Screen:** The three pair tokens expand into a final board labeled "Color Mix Tester." Arrows connect each collected pair to its result color, with all three child-built examples visible.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm celebration] "You earned the Color Mix Tester badge. You used Causation by collecting color pairs, and Change by watching each pair make a new color."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again" or names a favorite round.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for a different topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud guide] "Next time we can make a new path with a new answer."
2. [flexible] "That can be the next mission. This badge saves what you did today."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle goodbye] "Your Color Mix Tester badge is saved."

**Screen:** The badge, the three round tokens, and a next-step card remain visible. Next-step card: "Try one new answer next time."
