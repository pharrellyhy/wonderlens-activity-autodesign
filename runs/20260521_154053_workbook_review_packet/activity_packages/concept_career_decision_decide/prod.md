### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The AI first gives the child a professional role, then the child makes simple expert decisions inside that work scenario.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know that helpers use tools and first actions to solve simple needs.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that a responsible expert choice matches the role, tool, and situation.
- **D (Do):** Children act as the named helper, choose a tool, and decide the first safe action.

**3. Design Highlight**

The package is profession-first role play: the child becomes the helper before the scenario decisions begin.

**4. Typical Scenario**

The child is told, "Today you are the firefighter." A simple fire-safety scenario appears, and the child decides what this expert should do first, which tool fits, and what safe action comes next.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm invitation] "Today you get a helper job. You are the firefighter for this mission. Want to make the expert choices?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes" or nods.
2. (Unexpected) "What mission?" or gives an unrelated idea.
3. (No response) Child watches or stays quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. Firefighter badge on. Your decisions build the safe plan."
2. [friendly] "It is a role-play choice mission. I will give the scene, and you decide as the firefighter."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "You can say yes, no, or point to one expert choice."

**Screen:** The activity badge "Firefighter Decision Maker" appears with three empty progress tokens. If `career_portrait_cards_01` is available, show a firefighter card as support; if not, use voice-only fallback and do not claim the asset is visible.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction

**AI says:** [clear guide tone] "Desk rule: first you are the helper. Then you hear the work problem and choose what your expert should do."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay."
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do it my way?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Round one is ready, Firefighter."
2. [validating] "Yes, your way can help. We will still make one clear expert decision each round."
3. [wait 2s] [modeling] "I will start, and you can copy my first answer if you want."

**Screen:** A simple rule strip appears: Role, scene, expert decision, save a token. Token 1 pulses.

#### Step 3: Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Decide The First Response:**

**AI says:** [scenario tone] "Firefighter, a smoke alarm is ringing at a small building. Should your team send the fire truck now: yes or no?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes, send it."
2. (Unexpected) "No." / "Maybe later."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Yes. Smoke alarm means the firefighter responds quickly. Token 1 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Waiting could be unsafe. A firefighter sends help first, then checks details."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: yes, send the truck."

**Screen:** Scenario card shows the firefighter badge, smoke alarm icon, and fire truck choice. Response token fills with "send help."

**Round 2 -- Choose The Tool:**

**AI says:** [tool-matching tone] "Now choose the safer tool for the fire: water hose or cooking oil?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Water hose."
2. (Unexpected) "Oil."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Water fits this safety choice. Token 2 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Oil can make fire worse. A firefighter chooses water or the right extinguisher."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: water hose."

**Screen:** Tool cards narrow to water hose and extinguisher; unsafe oil is marked not first.

**Round 3 -- Choose the First Action:**

**AI says:** [decision tone] "Before going close, what should the firefighter check first: are people safe outside, or run inside alone?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "People safe outside."
2. (Unexpected) "Run inside alone."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Yes. Firefighters check people first. Token 3 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Going alone is not safe. The expert first checks people and calls the team."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: check people first."

**Screen:** Help plan now reads: firefighter -> send truck -> water hose -> check people first. Unsafe solo action is marked not first.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [delighted reveal] "Look at that. Your three firefighter decisions made the safety plan light up: send help, choose water, check people first."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I made it" or explains the result.
2. (Unexpected) "Can we change one?"
3. (No response) Child watches the reveal.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [celebrating] "Yes. Your answers caused this reveal."
2. [replay invitation] "That is a great next-round idea. This version shows the path you made first."
3. [wait 2s] [soft recap] "I will read the three tokens back so you can see your path."

**Screen:** The three tokens expand into a final board labeled "Firefighter Decision Maker": Role, Scene, Tool, First Safe Action. The smoke-alarm scenario remains visible so the decision is consequential.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm celebration] "You earned the Firefighter Decision Maker badge. You used Responsibility by choosing a safe first action, and Function by matching the expert role to the right tool."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again" or names a favorite round.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for a different topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud guide] "Next time we can make a new path with a new answer."
2. [flexible] "That can be the next mission. This badge saves what you did today."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle goodbye] "Your Firefighter Decision Maker badge is saved."

**Screen:** The badge, the three round tokens, and a next-step card remain visible. Next-step card: "Try one new answer next time."
