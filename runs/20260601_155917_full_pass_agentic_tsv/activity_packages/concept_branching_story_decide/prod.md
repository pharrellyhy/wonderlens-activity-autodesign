### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The AI tells a story with choice points, and the child's choices decide what happens next.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know the visible rule, role, or clue that starts this activity.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that their action changes what happens next.
- **D (Do):** Children complete the repeated `decide` action and explain or show one piece of evidence.

**3. Design Highlight**

Fresh full-rerun package generated from the workbook-derived source snapshot. Runtime wording uses behavior contracts plus example lines so the LLM can adapt while preserving the source sequence.

**4. Typical Scenario**

Child enters story mode or photographs a toy or object that can become a story character.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**Runtime AI instruction:** Open from the Branching Choice Story starter cue, name the child as two-path storyteller, and preview the first story choice turn.

**Example AI line:** "Branching Choice Story starts now. You are the two-path storyteller; I will help one story choice turn at a time."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) The child accepts the two-path storyteller role, notices the starter cue, or names something connected to the first story fork.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for another game, starts the choice before the Branching Choice Story mission is framed, or follows an unrelated topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the Branching Choice Story title/trigger card without taking the two-path storyteller role yet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. Name the two-path storyteller role, connect it to the starter cue, and preview the first choice.
2. Acknowledge the request, return to the Branching Choice Story promise, and offer the smallest supported first action.
3. [wait 2s] Point to the Branching Choice Story role card and first token, then model one tiny in-frame response.

**Screen:** Shows title, child role, source trigger, and empty progress tokens.

#### Step 2: Role And Rules

**Runtime AI instruction:** Explain the rule as an action loop and name any required asset or honest fallback.

**Example AI line:** "Rule: I give one story choice cue, you answer or try it, and we save one progress token each turn."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) The child agrees to the choice loop for Branching Choice Story or asks for the easiest version.
2. (Unexpected) Child tries to skip the first story fork, ignore the required rule/asset, or count a different kind of response.
3. (No response) Child looks at the Branching Choice Story rule strip without confirming how to start the first turn.

**AI follow-up:**

1. Restate the Branching Choice Story loop as AI prompt, child choice, saved token, and show the first response slot.
2. Keep the rule tied to the first story fork, name the supported fallback, and offer one allowed first turn.
3. [wait 2s] Read the Branching Choice Story rule in one sentence and ask for yes, a point, or the first chance to make a choice.

**Screen:** Shows the rule strip, current round token, and asset/fallback chip. No prebuilt asset is required; show progress tokens and the active story choice cue.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Start The Source Action:**

**Runtime AI instruction:** Preserve the workbook promise: The AI tells a story with choice points, and the child's choices decide what happens next. Ask the child to choose and respond in the first small turn.

**Example AI line:** "Let us start: The AI tells a story with choice points, and the child's choices decide what happens next. Choose the first story path now."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) The child chooses the first offered story path and accepts the immediate consequence.
2. (Unexpected) Child invents a third opening path, asks to skip the fork, or describes the hero without choosing a path.
3. (No response) Child looks between the first two path tokens without naming or tapping one.

**AI follow-up:**

1. Lock the first path, narrate the consequence, and place its token at the start of the trail.
2. Use the idea as story color, then restate the two opening paths and ask for one choice.
3. [wait 2s] Read the two opening path labels, model "I choose this path," and ask for one tap or word.

**Screen:** Shows the active round token, child response slot, and story choice cue. No prebuilt asset is required; show progress tokens and the active story choice cue.

**Round 2 -- Repeat With A Variation:**

**Runtime AI instruction:** Keep the same source frame and ask for a second decide turn with a small variation.

**Example AI line:** "Now try one more turn in the same game. What changes this time?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) The child chooses the next branch based on what happened after the first path.
2. (Unexpected) Child tries to undo the first branch, adds an unavailable route, or talks about the ending before making the second choice.
3. (No response) Child watches the second fork and previous path token without choosing the next branch.

**AI follow-up:**

1. Connect the choice to the previous consequence, narrate the new turn, and add the second path token.
2. Keep the first consequence in place, show the two current options, and ask which branch happens now.
3. [wait 2s] Point from the first token to the two new options, then model one short branch choice.

**Screen:** Shows the active round token, child response slot, and story choice cue. No prebuilt asset is required; show progress tokens and the active story choice cue.

**Round 3 -- Complete The Loop:**

**Runtime AI instruction:** Ask the child to recap, show, choose, or explain the result so the source action has closure.

**Example AI line:** "What should we remember from your story choice turns?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) The child chooses or explains the ending created by the saved story branches.
2. (Unexpected) Child starts a new unrelated story, rejects the saved path tokens, or asks for an ending that does not follow the chosen trail.
3. (No response) Child looks at the completed trail without saying what ending the choices made.

**AI follow-up:**

1. Name the final path chain, narrate the earned ending, and let the child claim the ending token.
2. Point to the saved branch tokens, explain which endings still fit, and ask for one ending that follows them.
3. [wait 2s] Read the path chain aloud, offer two fitting endings, and invite the child to pick one.

**Screen:** Shows the active round token, child response slot, and story choice cue. No prebuilt asset is required; show progress tokens and the active story choice cue.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**Runtime AI instruction:** Reveal the outcome caused by the child's saved turns and recap concrete choices.

**Example AI line:** "Your story choice turns are saved: we started Branching Choice Story, practiced story choice, and reached the finish."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) The child notices how the ending choice for the story trail changed the Branching Choice Story board or names a favorite saved turn.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks to restart before seeing the Branching Choice Story payoff or ignores how the saved choice turns connect.
3. (No response) Child watches the Branching Choice Story reveal without commenting on the saved turns.

**AI follow-up:**

1. Tie the reveal to the child's choice turns, name one concrete saved token, and invite a short reflection.
2. Hold the Branching Choice Story reveal, point to the saved turn that matters, and ask what changed because of it.
3. [wait 2s] Narrate one before/after change from the Branching Choice Story board, then offer two favorite-turn choices.

**Screen:** Shows a final board with saved turns, asset/fallback note when relevant, and source-specific payoff.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**Runtime AI instruction:** Close with the two key concepts and one parent-reviewable recap.

**Example AI line:** "Today you practiced Form and Responsibility. You used your own answer to move the activity forward."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) The child names a favorite Branching Choice Story moment, asks to play again, or watches the branching story recap badge.
2. (Unexpected) Child shifts topic before the recap names the choice skill or Form and Responsibility.
3. (No response) Child stays on the Branching Choice Story recap badge without responding.

**AI follow-up:**

1. Offer a next-time variation using the same decide mechanic and the branching story frame.
2. Close Branching Choice Story first, name the practiced choice, and then offer one next-round seed.
3. [wait 2s] Read the Branching Choice Story badge in one sentence and end with one concrete next-time invitation.

**Screen:** Recap badge lists title, mechanic `decide`, focal attribute `branching_story`, and next-step hint.
