Review Criteria

Packages are still judged with the `program.md` Phase 3 10-dimension rubric. A package passes only when every applicable dimension passes; Dimension 8 may be N/A for non-mapping packages. Any FAIL or credible reviewer uncertainty requires repair before logging or checkoff.
#DimensionPass CriteriaWhy It Fails
1V1 Technical ComplianceNo runtime beat depends on blocked V1 capabilities such as OCR, pose/expression detection, IMU sensing, before/after comparison, or unsupported non-speech audio detection.Fail when the package promises capability the current runtime cannot safely execute or verify.
2Hook & TransitionStep 1 opens from emotional resonance, grows naturally from the trigger, and bridges into the activity without feeling like a sudden quiz.Fail when the opening is a cold task assignment, knowledge test, or disconnected transition.
3Edge Case CoverageEvery dialogue step covers ideal, unexpected, and no-response branches with validating redirects, wait timing, and concrete scaffolds.Fail when branches are missing, generic, or leave a child stuck after silence or an unexpected answer.
4IB CompletenessKey Concepts, Related Concepts, KUD, ATL skills, and closing dialogue are specific and match what the child actually does.Fail when IB language is vague, missing, or forced onto an activity that does not support it.
5Tier AppropriatenessLanguage, response length, number of rounds, task complexity, and vocabulary fit the declared target tier.Fail when a T0/T1/T2 package asks for too much, uses over-complex language, or under-delivers for the target age.
6Dialogue SpecificityAI lines are concrete spoken dialogue with tone markers and branch-specific reactions, not abstract narration.Fail when the runtime would need to invent missing prompts, feedback, or child-facing wording.
7Screen & UI CompletenessEach step names what appears, persists, changes, and how progress or payoff is represented on screen.Fail when screen states are generic, missing, or disconnected from the dialogue.
8Entity Mapping AlignmentMapping-informed designs use mapping concepts, themes, related concepts, tier language, anchor dimensions, and approved warm-start bridge logic. Non-mapping packages may be N/A.Fail when a mapping-informed package invents ungrounded facts or ignores required mapping anchors.
9Game FeelThe child experiences uncertainty, stakes, surprise, replay value, and an earned magic moment tied to repeated action.Fail when the activity is only a conversation or generic badge award without a game-like payoff.
10Mechanic Fidelity + Scaffold HonestyStep 3's repeated child action matches the declared mechanic and source promise, while the pillar/style scaffold supports rather than distorts it.Fail when the scaffold hides a mechanic mismatch, unsupported capability, or diluted source promise.
