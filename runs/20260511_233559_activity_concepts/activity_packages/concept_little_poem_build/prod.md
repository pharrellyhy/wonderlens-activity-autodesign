### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child contributes words, images, or feelings and co-creates a short poem with the AI.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know a poem can be built from a seeing word, a feeling word, and an action word.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that their chosen words shape the poem's image, mood, and movement.
- **D (Do):** Children contribute three poem ingredients and hear them woven into a short, child-owned poem.

**3. Design Highlight**

The child contributes ingredients each round. The AI weaves, but the poem is visibly built from child choices.

**4. Typical Scenario**

The child photographs a leaf, chooses green, windy, and sleepy as ingredients, and the AI builds a short poem line by line.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm invitation] "I found a perfect moment for Tiny Poem Workshop. You can be the Tiny Poet Builder today. Ready for a tiny mission?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes" or nods.
2. (Unexpected) "What mission?" or gives an unrelated idea.
3. (No response) Child watches or stays quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. I will keep it short and playful."
2. [friendly] "Good question. I will show one tiny choice at a time."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "You can answer with one word, a point, or a small idea."

**Screen:** The activity badge "Tiny Poet Builder" appears with three empty progress tokens.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction

**AI says:** [clear guide tone] "Here is the rule: I give you one small challenge, you answer, and we save one progress token. Three tokens make the final reveal."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay."
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do it my way?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Round one is ready."
2. [validating] "Yes, your idea can help. I will still keep each round easy to answer."
3. [wait 2s] [modeling] "I will start, and you can copy my first answer if you want."

**Screen:** A simple rule strip appears: Listen or look, answer, save a token. Token 1 pulses.

#### Step 3: Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Add a Seeing Word:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "Give me one thing we can see in the poem."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "green leaf."
2. (Unexpected) "truck noise."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [poet voice] "Green leaf gives the poem something to see. I am saving that tile."
2. [bridging] "Truck noise is a hearing idea. For this first tile, give me something the poem can show."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Look near you and name one thing: leaf, shoe, cup, or sky."

**Screen:** Ingredient one appears as a leaf word tile. Token 1 fills with a short label from the child's response.

**Round 2 -- Add a Feeling Word:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "What feeling should the poem have: cozy, silly, brave, sleepy, or surprised?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "sleepy."
2. (Unexpected) "angry monster."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [soft delight] "Sleepy makes the poem gentle. I will tint the tile quiet blue."
2. [validating] "Angry monster is strong and pretend. Let us choose one safe poem feeling: cozy, silly, brave, sleepy, or surprised."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Pick one feeling word. Sleepy is ready if you want it."

**Screen:** Ingredient two becomes a soft feeling tile. Token 2 fills with a short label from the child's response.

**Round 3 -- Add an Action Word:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "What should the poem thing do?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "dance."
2. (Unexpected) "nothing."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [building tone] "Dance gives the poem movement. Now all three ingredient tiles are ready."
2. [gentle scaffold] "Nothing can be still, but a poem needs one action. Try sway, hide, shine, or dance."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Choose one action word. I can use dance."

**Screen:** Ingredient three becomes an action tile. Token 3 fills with a short label from the child's response.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [delighted reveal] "Look at that. Your three word tiles made a poem: Green leaf. Sleepy blue. Dance, little leaf. I made you."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I made it" or explains the result.
2. (Unexpected) "Can we change one?"
3. (No response) Child watches the reveal.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [celebrating] "Yes. Your answers caused this reveal."
2. [replay invitation] "That is a great next-round idea. This version shows the path you made first."
3. [wait 2s] [soft recap] "I will read the poem softly so you can hear your words."

**Screen:** The three tokens fold into a four-line poem card labeled "Tiny Poet Builder". The seeing word, feeling word, and action word remain highlighted inside the poem so the payoff is earned by the child's earlier choices.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm celebration] "You earned the Tiny Poet Builder badge. You used Connection by noticing the important part, and Perspective by connecting your answers to what happened next."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again" or names a favorite round.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for a different topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud guide] "Next time we can make a new path with a new answer."
2. [flexible] "That can be the next mission. This badge saves what you did today."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle goodbye] "Your Tiny Poet Builder badge is saved."

**Screen:** The badge, the three round tokens, and a next-step card remain visible. Next-step card: "Try one new answer next time."
