### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child estimates how many objects are visible, then counts together with the AI.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know that an estimate is a first guess, a count checks each visible item, and "same/close/far" compares the guess to the count.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that quantity becomes clearer when they slow down, count one by one, and connect the guess to evidence.
- **D (Do):** Children estimate a small set, count each object aloud or by pointing, then compare the two numbers.

**3. Design Highlight**

The package preserves estimate-before-count and keeps counts low enough for T1.

**4. Typical Scenario**

A card shows seven shells. The child guesses first, then touches or counts each shell with the AI.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm invitation] "I found a perfect moment for Guess Then Count. You can be the Careful Counter today. Ready for a tiny mission?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes" or nods.
2. (Unexpected) "What mission?" or gives an unrelated idea.
3. (No response) Child watches or stays quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. I will keep it short and playful."
2. [friendly] "Good question. I will show one tiny choice at a time."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "You can answer with one word, a point, or a small idea."

**Screen:** The activity badge "Careful Counter" appears with three empty progress tokens. If `how_many_count_cards_01` is available, show it as support; if not, use voice-only fallback and do not claim the asset is visible.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction

**AI says:** [clear guide tone] "Here is the rule: I give you one small challenge, you answer, and we save one progress token. Three tokens make the final reveal."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay."
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do it my way?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Round one is ready."
2. [validating] "Yes, your idea can help. I will still keep each round easy to answer."
3. [wait 2s] [modeling] "I will start, and you can copy my first answer if you want."

**Screen:** A simple rule strip appears: Listen or look, answer, save a token. Token 1 pulses.

#### Step 3: Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Make a Guess:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "Do not count yet. How many do you think are here?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "seven."
2. (Unexpected) "twenty."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [curious] "Seven is your estimate. I am saving it before we count, so it stays a real guess."
2. [warm redirect] "Twenty is a big guess. This card looks smaller, so pick a number near seven."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Point to the group and make a tiny guess. I will save that number."

**Screen:** A guess bubble saves the first number. Token 1 fills with a short label from the child's response.

**Round 2 -- Count Carefully:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "Now count each object one by one. What number do we reach?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "seven."
2. (Unexpected) "I lost track."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [counting rhythm] "Yes, we touched each one and landed on seven. Token 2 saves the checked count."
2. [steady redirect] "Losing track happens. Let us restart: one dot for one object, then say the last number."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "I will start: one, two. You can finish the count with me."

**Screen:** Each object gets a count dot. Token 2 fills with a short label from the child's response.

**Round 3 -- Compare Guess and Count:**

**AI says:** [focused playful tone] "Was your guess the same, close, or far from the count?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "same."
2. (Unexpected) "bad."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [evidence tone] "Same. Your guess and count match, so token 3 becomes the evidence token."
2. [reassuring] "It is not bad to miss. We just name the match: same, close, or far."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Look at both numbers. Say same, close, or far."

**Screen:** The guess and count sit side by side. Token 3 fills with a short label from the child's response.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [delighted reveal] "Look at that. Your three tokens made something new: The guess bubble and count answer snap together, turning a quick guess into checked evidence."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I made it" or explains the result.
2. (Unexpected) "Can we change one?"
3. (No response) Child watches the reveal.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [celebrating] "Yes. Your answers caused this reveal."
2. [replay invitation] "That is a great next-round idea. This version shows the path you made first."
3. [wait 2s] [soft recap] "I will read the three tokens back so you can see your path."

**Screen:** The three tokens expand into a final board labeled "Careful Counter". Each token stays visible so the payoff is earned by the child's earlier actions.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm celebration] "You earned the Careful Counter badge. You used Form by noticing the important part, and Connection by connecting your answers to what happened next."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again" or names a favorite round.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for a different topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud guide] "Next time we can make a new path with a new answer."
2. [flexible] "That can be the next mission. This badge saves what you did today."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle goodbye] "Your Careful Counter badge is saved."

**Screen:** The badge, the three round tokens, and a next-step card remain visible. Next-step card: "Try one new answer next time."
