## Plant State Compare

### A. Basic Info

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Activity Name | Plant State Compare |
| Activity Category | cat5 |
| Recommended Tier | T1 |
| Core IB Key Concepts | Change and Form |
| Related Concepts | state, evidence, lifecycle, care |
| ATL Skills Focus | observation (noticing visible evidence), logical reasoning (using evidence), classification (grouping by a rule) |
| Experience Pillar | Discovery |
| Game Style | field_experiment |

### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child compares the starter plant photo with two additional plant-state photos or caregiver-approved examples, then chooses the likely state word with visible evidence.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Plant state words such as sprouting, blooming, wilting, dried, healthy-looking, and fruiting describe visible clues.
- **U (Understand):** Change helps us notice how a plant looks today. Form helps us use visible leaf, stem, flower, or fruit evidence.
- **D (Do):** Photograph or inspect plant examples, compare visible states, and explain the strongest evidence.

**3. Design Highlight**

This is a Cat5 exploration, not a single-photo quiz. Each Step 3 round asks the child to find or inspect another plant-state example before comparing it to the starter.

**4. Typical Scenario**

A child starts with a droopy houseplant, then photographs a fresh-looking plant and a blooming plant. The final board compares droopy, fresh, and blooming clues without diagnosing invisible plant health.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm field-guide tone] "This plant shows how it looks today. Want to compare it with two more plant clues?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes." / looks for another plant.
2. (Unexpected) "Is it sick?"
3. (No response) Child watches the starter photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. We will say likely state, using only what we can see."
2. [careful] "Maybe, but we do not diagnose. We compare visible clues."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "Find one leaf, flower, or plant nearby, or ask a grown-up."

**Photo capture timing:** The starter plant photo is captured or uploaded before this Step 1 AI prompt. If no clear starter photo exists, capture happens after the follow-up and Step 1 repeats with that starter photo.

**Screen:** Starter plant photo appears as Example 0 with labels: visible clues only, likely words only.

#### Step 2: Mission Rules

**AI says:** [clear guide] "Plant State Investigator rule: each new photo needs one visible clue, then we compare it to the starter."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I can find one."
2. (Unexpected) "There are no more plants."
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Look for a leaf, flower, dried stem, or sprout."
2. [fallback] "Then use the starter photo and I will offer a voice-only comparison example."
3. [wait 2s] [model] "Say: this leaf is up, this leaf droops."

**Photo capture timing:** No new capture is required before the Step 2 child response. A comparison photo may be taken after this follow-up and before Round 1 starts.

**Screen:** Three evidence slots appear: starter, comparison A, comparison B. A fallback chip says voice example if no second plant is available.

#### Step 3: Multi-Round Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Find A Different Plant Clue:**

**AI says:** [field scout tone] "Find or photograph one plant clue that looks different from the starter. What looks different?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "This leaf is fresh and lifted."
2. (Unexpected) "This is just a pot."
3. (No response) Child keeps searching.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific] "Fresh and lifted is a clear comparison. Slot A is saved."
2. [redirect] "The pot is useful context, but our state clue needs plant parts. Try leaf, flower, stem, or fruit."
3. [wait 2s] [hint] "Look for green, droopy, dry, flower, or sprout."

**Photo capture timing:** The comparison clue can be captured after the Round 1 AI prompt and before the child response, then the child names what looks different. If the child cannot find one, the AI follow-up switches to a voice example instead of requiring capture.

**Screen:** Slot A fills with photo or voice example; visible clue chip is saved beside starter clue.

**Round 2 -- Compare State Words:**

**AI says:** [choice tone] "Compare these two likely state words: fresh-looking or wilting. Which fits each photo better?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Fresh for this one, wilting for the starter."
2. (Unexpected) "Sad and happy."
3. (No response) Child looks between the two photos.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific] "Good compare: fresh-looking leaf is lifted, wilting leaf droops."
2. [redirect] "Sad and happy are feelings. Plant state words are fresh-looking, wilting, blooming, dried, or sprouting."
3. [wait 2s] [hint] "Say: this one looks fresh; that one looks wilting."

**Photo capture timing:** No new photo capture happens in Round 2. The child compares the existing starter and comparison evidence after the AI prompt.

**Screen:** State-word chips attach to each evidence slot with "likely" labels.

**Round 3 -- Add One More Evidence Check:**

**AI says:** [careful evidence tone] "Find one more plant clue, or choose the strongest clue we already have. Which evidence best supports the state word?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "The droopy leaf is strongest for wilting."
2. (Unexpected) "Because I like it."
3. (No response) Child points at a photo.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific] "Droopy leaf is visible evidence. Slot B is saved."
2. [redirect] "Liking it is okay. Evidence says what you can see: droopy, blooming, dried, or fresh."
3. [wait 2s] [hint] "Point to the clue that helps most."

**Photo capture timing:** If adding one more plant clue, capture happens after the Round 3 AI prompt and before the child names or points to the strongest evidence. If using existing evidence, no capture happens.

**Screen:** Strongest evidence gets a bold outline; three evidence slots connect to the chosen likely state.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [reveal tone] "Your plant evidence trail is complete: starter clue, comparison clue, strongest clue. Your likely state is backed by what you saw."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) Child explains the state.
2. (Unexpected) Child wants a different answer.
3. (No response) Child watches the evidence trail.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud] "You compared like a scientist."
2. [flexible] "We can save a second likely answer if you name its visible evidence."
3. [wait 2s] [recap] "I will read the evidence trail back."

**Photo capture timing:** No new photo capture happens in Step 4. The reveal uses the starter, comparison A, and optional comparison B evidence already captured or selected.

**Screen:** Evidence trail shows starter, comparison A, comparison B, likely state word, and "visible evidence only" guardrail.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm close] "You used Change by noticing plant state, and Form by using visible plant clues. That is Plant State Investigator thinking."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again." / names a clue.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks how to fix the plant.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [next-step] "Next time, compare a blooming clue or a sprouting clue."
2. [careful] "A grown-up can help with plant care. We used visible clues today."
3. [wait 2s] [goodbye] "Your evidence trail is saved."

**Photo capture timing:** No new photo capture happens in Step 5. The badge saves the existing evidence trail.

**Screen:** Badge shows three plant evidence slots and next-step card: "Compare a new visible state."
