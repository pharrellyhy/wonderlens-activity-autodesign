### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child helps choose what a helper or professional should do in a simple scenario.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know that helpers use roles, tools, and first actions to solve simple needs.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that a responsible choice matches the helper, tool, and situation.
- **D (Do):** Children choose a helper, match a tool, and decide the first safe action.

**3. Design Highlight**

The package keeps roles broad and inclusive; the child decides actions rather than memorizing career facts.

**4. Typical Scenario**

A playground gate is stuck and a child is worried because the class cannot enter. The AI presents one concrete situation first, then the child decides which helper fits, what tool helps, and what safe first action should happen.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm invitation] "Someone needs a helpful plan. Want to be the Helpful Decision Maker?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes" or nods.
2. (Unexpected) "What mission?" or gives an unrelated idea.
3. (No response) Child watches or stays quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. We will choose a helper, a tool, and the first kind action."
2. [friendly] "It is a choice mission. Your decisions build the help plan."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "You can point to a helper or say one choice."

**Screen:** The activity badge "Helpful Decision Maker" appears with three empty progress tokens. If `career_portrait_cards_01` is available, show it as support; if not, use voice-only fallback and do not claim the asset is visible.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction

**AI says:** [clear guide tone] "Desk rule: hear the problem first. Then choose helper, tool, and first action."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay."
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do it my way?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Round one is ready."
2. [validating] "Yes, your way can help. We will still make one clear decision each round."
3. [wait 2s] [modeling] "I will start, and you can copy my first answer if you want."

**Screen:** A simple rule strip appears: Listen or look, answer, save a token. Token 1 pulses.

#### Step 3: Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Read the Need:**

**AI says:** [scenario tone] "The playground gate is stuck. The class is waiting outside. Which helper fits this need: teacher, gardener, bus driver, or firefighter?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Teacher first." / "A grown-up teacher."
2. (Unexpected) "Firefighter." / chooses a dramatic role without evidence.
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Teacher first fits the school problem. Token 1 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "A firefighter helps danger. This is a stuck gate with waiting children, so a teacher or caretaker fits first."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: teacher first."

**Screen:** Scenario card shows stuck gate + waiting class. Helper choice token fills with the selected role and one visible evidence chip.

**Round 2 -- Match the Tool:**

**AI says:** [tool-matching tone] "Now choose the tool for the helper: phone to call help, key ring, garden shovel, or bus ticket. Which belongs with this stuck gate problem?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Key ring." / "Phone to call caretaker."
2. (Unexpected) "Garden shovel."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Key ring or phone fits the stuck gate. Token 2 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "A shovel helps soil. This problem needs opening or asking the caretaker."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: key ring or phone."

**Screen:** Tool cards narrow to key ring and phone; mismatched tools fade but remain visible as evidence choices.

**Round 3 -- Choose the First Action:**

**AI says:** [decision tone] "What should happen first: check if the gate is safe, call the caretaker, or tell the class to push it?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Check if it is safe first."
2. (Unexpected) "Push it hard."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Checking first keeps everyone safe. Token 3 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Pushing might hurt fingers. A responsible helper checks first or calls help."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: check first."

**Screen:** Help plan now reads: stuck gate -> teacher/caretaker -> key or phone -> check first. Unsafe push choice is marked not first.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [delighted reveal] "Look at that. Your three tokens made something new: The help plan lights up: the stuck-gate problem connects to the helper, tool, and safe first action the child chose."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I made it" or explains the result.
2. (Unexpected) "Can we change one?"
3. (No response) Child watches the reveal.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [celebrating] "Yes. Your answers caused this reveal."
2. [replay invitation] "That is a great next-round idea. This version shows the path you made first."
3. [wait 2s] [soft recap] "I will read the three tokens back so you can see your path."

**Screen:** The three tokens expand into a final board labeled "Helpful Decision Maker": Problem, Helper, Tool, First Safe Action. The stuck-gate scenario remains visible so the decision is consequential.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm celebration] "You earned the Helpful Decision Maker badge. You used Responsibility by choosing a safe first action, and Function by matching the helper to the tool."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again" or names a favorite round.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for a different topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud guide] "Next time we can make a new path with a new answer."
2. [flexible] "That can be the next mission. This badge saves what you did today."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle goodbye] "Your Helpful Decision Maker badge is saved."

**Screen:** The badge, the three round tokens, and a next-step card remain visible. Next-step card: "Try one new answer next time."
