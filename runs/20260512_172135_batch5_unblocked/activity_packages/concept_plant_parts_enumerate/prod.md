### B. Activity Overview

**1. Brief Description**

The child looks at a plant and identifies visible parts such as leaf, stem, flower, fruit, seed, or root.

**2. Educational Purpose (KUD)**

- **K (Know):** Children know common visible plant parts such as leaf, stem, flower, bud, seed, fruit, and root.
- **U (Understand):** Children understand that different parts work together as one living plant.
- **D (Do):** Children point to or name visible parts and add them to a simple plant map.

**3. Design Highlight**

The activity is a visible-evidence hunt. Leaf and stem are expected targets, but the third token is a surprise part: flower, fruit, seed, root, bud, or an honest not-visible chip.

**4. Typical Scenario**

A child photographs a potted plant. The AI asks them to find leaf, stem, and one special part, then makes a labeled plant map.

### C. Interaction Flow

> Recommended Tier: T1

#### Step 1: Transition Bridge

**AI says:** [warm invitation] "This plant has tiny parts to discover. Want to be the Plant Parts Scout?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Yes" or nods.
2. (Unexpected) "What mission?" or gives an unrelated idea.
3. (No response) Child watches or stays quiet.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [excited] "Great. We will find a leaf, a stem, and one surprise part."
2. [friendly] "It is a plant map mission. We name only parts we can see."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle] "You can point to one plant part or say its name."

**Screen:** The activity badge "Plant Parts Scout" appears with three empty progress tokens.

#### Step 2: Rule Introduction

**AI says:** [clear guide tone] "Scout rule: point or name a visible part, then we pin it on the plant map."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Okay."
2. (Unexpected) "Can I do it my way?"
3. (No response) Child hesitates.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [encouraging] "Round one is ready."
2. [validating] "Yes, you can look your way. We will still save one visible plant part each round."
3. [wait 2s] [modeling] "I will start, and you can copy my first answer if you want."

**Screen:** A simple rule strip appears: Listen or look, answer, save a token. Token 1 pulses.

#### Step 3: Core Loop

**Round 1 -- Leaf Evidence Hunt:**

**AI says:** [field-guide tone] "Find a leaf clue: flat shape, edge, or green part. What proves it is a leaf?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Leaf, flat green shape."
2. (Unexpected) "The pot." / cannot see a leaf.
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Flat green shape is leaf evidence. Leaf token saved."
2. [warm redirect] "The pot is not a plant part. If no leaf is visible, say leaf not visible."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: leaf, or not visible."

**Screen:** The photo gets a green leaf marker, or a gray "leaf not visible" chip. Either way, the evidence trail continues.

**Round 2 -- Stem Path Hunt:**

**AI says:** [investigator tone] "Trace the path that holds the plant up. Is it a stem, stalk, or branch?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "stem."
2. (Unexpected) "Stick." / stem is hidden.
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Stem is the part holding the leaves up. Token 2 is saved."
2. [warm redirect] "Stick is close. On a plant, say stem if it is visible, or stem hidden."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: stem, or hidden."

**Screen:** A line marker climbs the stem area, or a gray "stem hidden" chip appears. A comparison line connects leaf and stem evidence.

**Round 3 -- Surprise Part Check:**

**AI says:** [discovery tone] "Mystery part check: flower, fruit, seed, root, or bud. What can we honestly name?"

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "flower."
2. (Unexpected) "nothing."
3. (No response) Child looks at the screen or waits.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [specific praise] "Flower is visible, so the surprise token is honest."
2. [warm redirect] "Nothing visible is useful evidence. Say no extra part visible if the photo hides it."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle hint] "Try: flower, bud, or not visible."

**Screen:** Surprise token fills with the named part or a gray "not visible" chip. The map still counts because the child inspected honestly.

#### Step 4: Magic Moment

**AI says:** [delighted reveal] "Your plant detective badge opens: leaf clue, stem path, and surprise part check. Hidden parts count when you name what the photo cannot show."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "I made it" or explains the result.
2. (Unexpected) "Can we change one?"
3. (No response) Child watches the reveal.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [celebrating] "Yes. Your answers caused this reveal."
2. [replay invitation] "That is a great next-round idea. This version shows the path you made first."
3. [wait 2s] [soft recap] "I will read the three tokens back so you can see your path."

**Screen:** The three tokens expand into a plant evidence map. Visible parts glow; any not-visible token becomes a gray evidence chip, so honest observation is part of the payoff.

#### Step 5: Closing + IB Concepts

**AI says:** [warm celebration] "You earned the Plant Parts Scout badge. You used Form by naming visible parts, and Function by noticing how the stem holds leaves up."

**Child responses:**

1. (Ideal) "Again" or names a favorite round.
2. (Unexpected) Child asks for a different topic.
3. (No response) Child watches the badge.

**AI follow-up:**

1. [proud guide] "Next time we can look for a new plant part, like flower, seed, fruit, or root if it is visible."
2. [flexible] "That can be the next mission. This badge saves what you did today."
3. [wait 2s] [gentle goodbye] "Your Plant Parts Scout badge is saved."

**Screen:** The badge, the three round tokens, and a next-step card remain visible. Next-step card: "Try one new visible plant part next time."
