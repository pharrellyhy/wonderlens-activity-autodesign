L1 Notice name one attribute
"Can you say 'red'?"
AIgasps "Oooh! A ladybug! So red! Can you say 'red'?"
[child tries: "red!" or babbles]
AI"Yes! Red! Pretty red ladybug!"
"What color is the ladybug?"
AI"Look at this ladybug! What color is it?"
Child"Red!"
AI"That's right — bright red. Almost like it's wearing a little coat."
"Name the first attribute that catches your eye."
AI"Before we investigate, name the first attribute that catches your eye about this ladybug."
Child"It's red."
AI"Noted — dominant color is red. Hold that observation; we'll come back to it."
L2 Extend multiple + locate
"Red body! Black dots! Say it with me?"
AI"Red body! And look — black dots! Can you say 'red body, black dots'?"
[child tries to repeat]
AI"Yes! Red AND black! Two colors!"
"Tell me all the colors and where each one is."
AI"Can you tell me all the colors you see on the ladybug, and where each one is?"
Child"Red body, black spots, and black legs."
AI"Three colors in three places — that's careful observation."
"List three visual attributes and locate each."
AI"List three distinct visual attributes and tell me exactly where on the ladybug each one appears."
Child"Red shell on top, black spots on the shell, six black legs underneath."
AI"Three attributes, three locations. That's systematic observation — a researcher would document it the same way."
L3 Reason explain why
"Why red? Birds don't eat red bugs!"
AI"Why is ladybug red? Because birds don't eat red bugs! Can you say 'don't eat me!'?"
[child tries: "don't eat!" or giggles]
AI"Don't eat me! That's what red means! Clever ladybug!"
"Why do you think the ladybug is red?"
AI"Here's a mystery: why do you think the ladybug is bright red? What's the red color for?"
Child"So birds don't eat it?"
AI"Exactly — it's called a warning color. The red says 'I taste bad, don't even try!'"
"Would that red help or hurt against predators? Why?"
AI"You noticed the red. Think about this: if the ladybug wanted to avoid predators, would that bright red help or hurt? And can you explain why?"
Child"It helps — red means 'don't eat me' to birds, because they learned red bugs taste bad."
AI"That's the aposematism hypothesis — bright coloring evolved as a toxicity signal. The 'learning' part is key: predators remember bad experiences."
