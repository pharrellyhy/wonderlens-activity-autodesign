What the child
sees at the end.

This doc specifies the post-activity screen — the one the child sees when beat 5 ends. Its job is to celebrate the specific cognitive act the child just performed and leave them with a warm, bounded ending. Explicitly not to hook them into another session.

§ 01 / Purpose & stance

A warm ending,
not a hook.

The recap screen has exactly one job: reflect back what the child just did, in language the child can feel. It is not a dashboard. It is not a streak tracker. It is not a reason to play again right now.

What this screen does. Names the specific cognitive move the child just made (the highlight_moment). Shows the concepts they touched (child-friendly words, never IB terminology). Gives a soft sign-off tailored to the tier.
What this screen explicitly doesn't do. No streaks, no daily-goal rings, no "N more until the next level", no rarity tiers, no push notifications, no infinite scroll, no peer comparison, no push toward another session.
§ 02 / Principles

Eight patterns,
eight refusals.

These are anti-patterns standard to ed-tech. We refuse each deliberately.

Pattern Why it hurts Our stance
Streaks ("don't break your chain") Creates guilt on skip days; pushes daily use regardless of need No streaks. Ever.
Variable rewards (random drops) Slot-machine dopamine loop Deterministic — earned by a specific cognitive act
Unbounded collection Grinding incentive; never "done" Bounded set, visible from day 1
Rare vs. common badges Gambling / hoarding instinct All badges equal. No rarity tiers
Leaderboards / peer comparison Social anxiety, competition Never show another child
External push notifications Manufactured FOMO App never pings the child
Infinite-scroll recap Eyes-on-screen loop One screen, one ending, dismissible
Time-based XP Rewards duration over learning Rewards tied to cognitive moves
Mechanism 01
Bounded badge universe
7 axes × 3 rungs = 21 slots. Fixed from day 1; no mystery / locked slots. The child sees the full universe the first time they open the collection.
Mechanism 02
Mastery display, not tally display
Depth per axis (filling bar L1→L2→L3), not a pile of medals. "You're getting deeper on Form" beats "you have 47 Form badges."
Mechanism 03
highlight_moment is hero, badges are ambient
The runtime-generated one-liner is large and serif; badges are secondary with no sparkles / pops / animation.
Mechanism 04
Soft session cap
After N activities in one sitting (T0: 2, T1: 3, T2: 4), the recap ends with "Good thinking today. See you tomorrow." App doesn't lock — a child who really wants to keep going can.
Mechanism 05
Replay is welcomed, not re-earned
Second playthrough of the same entity celebrates the new noticing, doesn't mint a new badge. "You saw the ladybug again, and this time you noticed…"
Mechanism 06
No streaks UI, anywhere
Not hidden, not built. The parent dashboard can show frequency if a parent wants it; the child never sees a "N days in a row" counter.
Mechanism 07
Exit with warmth, not a hook
End on an invitation to look at the real world ("Next time you're outside, see if you can find another round, spotted thing"). Never "tap to continue" or "N more until Level M."
Mechanism 08
Opt-in history
Earned badges live on a separate collection screen. A child who wants to see it can. It's never pushed in front of them.

§ 04 / Tag-block contract

Six fields in,
one recap out.

The tag-block schema lives in Template 0 §04. This table enumerates which fields this screen reads, and how each one renders. Nothing else from the tag block reaches the child's eyes.

Field Renders as Notes
role_title Badge title (serif) Main identity beat of the screen.
pillar Subtitle ("Mystery · solved") Emotional mode, sets the tone.
highlight_moment Large serif pull-quote Runtime-generated. Generation rule in §05.
related_concepts "You earned" chip row Child-friendly concept words. 2–4 chips.
atl_skills "You practiced" chip row Worded child-friendly, not IB jargon.

Explicitly not read. key_concepts, transdisciplinary_theme, subject_tags, progression.* — those are parent-facing (see Parent growth path doc). The child sees what they did, not how the IB framework classifies it.
§ 05 / highlight_moment generation

One sentence,
six to twelve words.

The hero of the recap is generated at activity-end from the child's actual responses during the session. These are the rules the generator follows.

Input
  • Activity transcript (child turns)
  • attributes — what the game invoked
  • pillar — emotional mode
  • progression.topic_axis
Output

"You spotted the warning sign on the ladybug's back"

A sanitized summary. Second person, past tense, evocative and specific. 6–12 words.

Rules
  • Always second-person ("You ___"), not child's name
  • Past tense, grounded in something the child actually said or did
  • No IB terminology, no tier names, no level numbers
  • 6–12 words — a sentence, not a paragraph
  • If no usable material: pillar-generic fallback ("You solved a small mystery today.")

By tier

Tier 0 · 2–4 yrs

"You said 'red' when you saw the ladybug"

Tier 1 · 4–6 yrs

"You figured out why the ladybug is red"

Tier 2 · 6–8 yrs

"You predicted what birds do with red bugs, and you were right"

Privacy. The transcript is input to the generator only; it never surfaces verbatim in the recap. The output is a sanitized summary — paraphrased, abstracted, safe to display without review. Raw transcripts are a separate, opt-in feature of the parent dashboard (see Parent growth path doc §09).
