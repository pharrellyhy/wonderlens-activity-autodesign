§ 00 / How to read this

A child's journey through rungs — four scenarios.

Template 0 §07 specifies WHEN the runtime promotes, holds, or demotes a child's rung on each axis. This doc shows WHAT that looks like for specific child journeys. Not a live simulator — hand-authored scenarios chosen to make the temporal dynamics concrete.

↑ promote
next rung up
· hold
same rung, next activity varies exemplar
↓ demote
drop one rung, same axis (dignity rule applies)
⇢ sibling jump
switch to related axis at L1
~ soft-reframe
same rung, different beat (not a demote)

Why this matters: most activities in real use will look like this. Promotions happen at natural breakpoints (spontaneous L+1, or 3-session consecutive completion), not on every success. Holding is not stagnation — it's how the system gives exemplars room to vary while the child internalizes the rung.
§ 02 / Hit-a-wall recovery

Causation hits a floor — runtime preserves dignity and jumps to a sibling axis.

§ 04 / Reluctance vs inability

Silence isn't a demote — the runtime has a softer move for when a child needs a breath, not a lower rung.

Mia goes quiet mid-activity after two correct attributes. The runtime reads it as an emotional pause, not a cognitive gap — shifts to a lower-pressure beat (soft-reframe), holds the rung, and Mia re-engages well enough to earn a promote on the next activity.

Silence ≠ Demote

A demote requires 2 attempts — silence plus off-topic wander plus repeat-prompt-back. A single silence after a correct response is often just the child thinking, resting, or needing a gentler re-entry.

The runtime has a separate soft-reframe move for this — it's a beat-level adjustment within the activity, NOT a rung change.

Demote Child's cognitive state genuinely mismatches the rung. Drop.
Soft-reframe Child's emotional / attention state needs a breath. Same rung, softer prompt.
Why this matters: The runtime isn't rung-greedy. It has intermediate moves — soft-reframe, exemplar variation — that preserve rung without forcing the child past their current edge. Distinguishing can't from won't is the system's clearest design statement: children aren't optimized through, they're listened to.
