What parents
see over time.

This doc specifies the parent-facing dashboard — default weekly view, with monthly and term toggles. Its job is to make the child's growth legible and actionable to parents, without turning into a grade report or an engagement loop.

§ 01 / Purpose & stance

Legible,
not clinical.

Parents want five things from this screen: progress (is my child growing?), personality (what kind of thinker are they becoming?), connection (what did they actually do?), action (how do I support them?), and awareness (is time on this app healthy?). The dashboard answers those. It explicitly does not do peer-comparison, letter grades or scores, predictive trajectories, or activity transcripts.

Typical dashboard feature Why we don't Our stance
Peer comparison ("vs. other kids") Anxiety, competition, parental guilt Never. Only this child's journey
Letter grades / scores Anti-IB philosophy; reduces nuance No scores, no grades
Predictive trajectories Over-promises; sets up disappointment No forecast lines
Activity transcripts Surveillance vibe Out of scope this round

§ 03 / Curiosity profile

What kind of
thinker?

A compact 7-lobed radial — one lobe per IB Key Concept. Its job is to answer, at a glance, what kind of thinker is my kid this week? without ranking, scoring, or comparing. Filled area tracks how often each concept was touched; outline reach tracks how deep the child went.

“Mia is a pattern-finder and a storyteller. She explores Form and Perspective most deeply; Responsibility is next to try.”
Caption rules · how this sentence is generated
  1. Name the 1–2 dominant concepts with personality words (pattern-finder, storyteller, investigator, carer, …) — not raw concept names.
  2. Mention the lowest-frequency axis as a gentle “next to try”, never as a weakness or a gap.
  3. Never imply the child should “catch up” on anything. This is a profile, not a checklist.
Reads from: aggregate key_concepts + progression.difficulty_level across the session window
§ 04 / In their words · this week

The week,
in her voice.

Two to three pull-quotes per week, rendered in serif and warm — deliberately journal-like, not metric-like. These are the lines the child noticed, in the child’s language. No counters, no averages, no achievement frame.

“The flower closes to save energy at night.”
Mia said Tuesday · playing with a sunflower Replay ↗
“The ladybug’s spots are a warning, like a stop sign.”
Mia said Wednesday · on a garden walk Replay ↗
“If I sort the leaves by their edges, the pointy ones all go together.”
Mia said Saturday morning · sorting leaves on the table Replay ↗
Privacy Default shows highlight_moment strings only — these are runtime-generated summaries, not raw transcripts. Direct child-utterance highlights are surfaced only if the parent opts in during onboarding.
§ 05 / Try this at home · offline

Close the app,
go outside.

Two to three concrete, offline, parent-participation suggestions per week — never more. The bound is deliberate: past three, it turns into a chore list and loses its warmth. These prompts read the week's axis profile and the activities the child actually touched, and hand back something the pair can do together without a screen.

On your walk, look for three spotted things together.
Extends Connection axis · matches pattern-finding
At dinner, ask her which leaf on her plate would sort with the pointy pile.
Extends Form axis · matches pattern-finding
Before bed, let her tell you one thing the ladybug's spots could mean to a bird.
Extends Perspective axis · matches storytelling
Intent This module's purpose is to send the parent-child pair away from the app. It's the anti-engagement feature. Copy here never says “open the app again” or “do one more activity” — it says “go do this with them, no screen.”
Reads from: current progression.topic_axis + progression.next_step_hint + entity hints from the week's activities

Ambient,

§ 08 / View cadence

Weekly by default,
with two zooms.

The dashboard opens to a weekly rolling window. Two toggles widen the lens without changing the modules. Empty states are handled in copy, not in half-populated modules.

  • Default Weekly — rolling 7 days (Sun–Sat).
  • Toggle · monthly Calendar month. Same six modules, aggregated across the month.
  • Toggle · term 12 weeks. Same six modules, aggregated across the term.
Empty state. If there are fewer than 3 activities in the current window, the dashboard collapses to a “still getting to know Mia” onboarding state — warm, welcoming copy — rather than rendering half-empty modules. Half-empty gauges and an empty pull-quote stack would read as a failure; an explicit onboarding state reads as honest.
§ 09 / Privacy & data scope

What shows,
what never does.

Four categories — default, opt-in, never shown, retention. The defaults are deliberately conservative; the parent earns more detail by opting in, not the other way around.

Default shown highlight_moment, aggregate tag counts, session duration.
Opt-in Specific child-utterance highlights in the “In their words” module, turned on via an onboarding setting. Off by default.
Never shown Raw activity transcripts, other children's data, predictive scores. Not a feature flag — a stance.
Retention Rolling 12 months on the dashboard; archived beyond that.
§ 10 / Edge cases

Four moments
the dashboard handles.

Named explicitly so that the default renderer knows what to do when the happy-path assumptions break.

Situation Response
New child (< 3 activities this week) Collapse to “getting to know Mia” onboarding state.
Single-axis over-indexing Curiosity profile copy nudges toward a neighbor axis as “next to try” — never framed as a deficit.
Demote streaks Do not surface as a negative; absorbed into ladder state silently.
Multiple children on one account Each child has a separate dashboard; never cross-compared.
§ 11 / Out of scope

Five things this doc
doesn't design.

Named explicitly to bound the scope and keep the dashboard honest about what it is — and what it isn't.

  • Cross-child comparisons (same account or other families) A stance, not a feature gap. Comparison is a habit we don't want to teach.
  • School-report export Not its job. The dashboard is a conversation prompt for parents, not a document for schools.
  • Notification cadence (push frequency, email digests) Separate product decision. Lives outside this design.
  • Activity-transcript drill-down view Needs its own design round — privacy, summarization, and replay semantics all require their own treatment.
  • Developmental-milestones ribbon Deferred: risk of parental anxiety; deferred until a gentler framing exists.
